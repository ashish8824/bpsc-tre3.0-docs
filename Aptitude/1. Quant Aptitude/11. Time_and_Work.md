## 1. CORE CONCEPTS & FUNDAMENTAL IDEA

---

### 🔑 The One Big Idea:

> **Work = Rate × Time**
>
> If a person can finish a job in **n days**, they complete **1/n** of the job per day.

---

### The Golden Table:

| Days to complete work | Work done per day | Work done per hour |
|----------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| 5 days | 1/5 | — |
| 8 days | 1/8 | — |
| n days | 1/n | 1/(n×hours_per_day) |

---

### Why This Works:

Think of total work as **1 unit** (or 100% or any convenient number using LCM).

- Person A finishes in 10 days → does **1/10** per day
- Person B finishes in 15 days → does **1/15** per day
- Together per day = 1/10 + 1/15 = **3/30 + 2/30 = 5/30 = 1/6**
- Together they finish in **6 days**

---

### Three Key Relationships:

```
         Work (W)
        /         \
   Rate (R)    ×   Time (T)

W = R × T
R = W / T
T = W / R
```

Where **Rate = 1/Days** (if work = 1 unit)

---

## 2. FUNDAMENTAL FORMULAS

---

### Basic Formulas:

$$\text{If a person completes work in n days} \Rightarrow \text{Rate} = \frac{1}{n} \text{ per day}$$

$$\text{Combined Rate} = \frac{1}{A} + \frac{1}{B} + \frac{1}{C} + \ldots$$

$$\text{Time together} = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{A} + \frac{1}{B}}= \frac{AB}{A+B}$$

---

### Two-Person Formula (Most Used):

$$\boxed{T = \frac{A \times B}{A + B}}$$

Where A and B are the individual days each person takes alone.

---

### Three-Person Formula:

$$T = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{A} + \frac{1}{B} + \frac{1}{C}} = \frac{ABC}{AB + BC + CA}$$

---

### Work Remaining Formula:

$$\text{Work remaining} = 1 - \text{Work done}$$

$$\text{Work done in x days} = x \times \text{(combined daily rate)}$$

---

### Partial Work Formula:

If A works for **d** days alone, then B joins:

$$\text{Work left after A works d days} = 1 - \frac{d}{A}$$

$$\text{Remaining days (both working)} = \frac{\text{Work left}}{\frac{1}{A} + \frac{1}{B}}$$

---

## 3. THE LCM METHOD — THE FASTEST APPROACH

---

> 🔥 **This is the single most powerful technique for Time & Work problems.**
> Use it for almost every question — it eliminates fractions!

---

### How It Works:

**Step 1:** Find LCM of all the given days.
**Step 2:** Assign LCM as **Total Work Units**.
**Step 3:** Calculate each person's **work per day** = Total Work / Their Days.
**Step 4:** Add work rates, divide total work by combined rate.

---

### Example (LCM Method):

> A completes work in 12 days, B in 15 days. How long together?

**Step 1:** LCM(12, 15) = 60 → Total Work = **60 units**

**Step 2:**
- A's rate = 60/12 = **5 units/day**
- B's rate = 60/15 = **4 units/day**

**Step 3:** Combined = 5 + 4 = **9 units/day**

**Step 4:** Days = 60/9 = **6⅔ days**

> No fractions in the middle — clean and fast!

---

### Why LCM is Faster:

| Method | Calculation |
|--------|------------|
| Fraction method | 1/12 + 1/15 = 5/60 + 4/60 = 9/60 → days = 60/9 |
| LCM method | 60 units ÷ 9 per day = 60/9 ✓ (same but no fraction addition) |

For complex problems with 3-4 people, LCM method is **much faster**.

## 4. BASIC TO ADVANCED EXAMPLES

---

### ─── BASIC LEVEL ───

#### Example 1: Two People Working Together

> A can do a piece of work in 10 days and B in 15 days. How long will they take together?

**Formula Method:**
$$T = \frac{10 \times 15}{10 + 15} = \frac{150}{25} = 6 \text{ days}$$

**LCM Method:**
LCM(10,15) = 30. A = 3/day, B = 2/day. Together = 5/day.
Days = 30/5 = **6 days** ✓

---

#### Example 2: Three People Together

> A, B, C can do work in 6, 8, 12 days respectively. How long together?

**LCM Method:**
LCM(6, 8, 12) = 24

| Person | Days | Rate (units/day) |
|--------|------|-----------------|
| A | 6 | 24/6 = 4 |
| B | 8 | 24/8 = 3 |
| C | 12 | 24/12 = 2 |

Combined rate = 4 + 3 + 2 = 9/day
Days = 24/9 = **2⅔ days = 2 days 16 hours**

---

#### Example 3: Find Individual Time When Together Time is Known

> A and B together finish in 12 days. B alone takes 30 days. How long does A take alone?

$$\frac{1}{A} = \frac{1}{12} - \frac{1}{30} = \frac{5-2}{60} = \frac{3}{60} = \frac{1}{20}$$

**A takes 20 days alone.**

**LCM Method:**
LCM(12, 30) = 60. Together rate = 60/12 = 5. B's rate = 60/30 = 2.
A's rate = 5 − 2 = 3. A's days = 60/3 = **20 days** ✓

---

#### Example 4: Work for Part of the Time

> A can do work in 20 days. After working for 5 days, B joins and they finish in 3 more days. How long would B take alone?

**Solution:**

Work done by A in 5 days = 5/20 = 1/4
Remaining = 3/4

A and B together finish 3/4 work in 3 days:
Combined rate = (3/4)/3 = 1/4 per day

A's rate = 1/20
B's rate = 1/4 − 1/20 = 5/20 − 1/20 = 4/20 = 1/5

**B takes 5 days alone.**

---

#### Example 5: One Person Leaves Early

> A and B together can finish in 12 days. They work together for 9 days after which A leaves. B finishes remaining work in 6 days. Find A's time alone.

**Solution:**

Work done in 9 days together = 9 × (1/12) = 3/4
Remaining = 1/4, done by B in 6 days.
B's rate = (1/4)/6 = 1/24 → **B alone = 24 days**

A's rate = 1/12 − 1/24 = 2/24 − 1/24 = 1/24
Wait — that gives A = 24 too. Let me recheck.

1/A + 1/24 = 1/12 → 1/A = 1/12 − 1/24 = 2/24 − 1/24 = 1/24

**A also takes 24 days alone.**

> **Insight:** Both A and B have same efficiency here, verified by equal times.

---

#### Example 6: A Leaves, B Continues

> A and B can finish work in 15 and 20 days. They start together. A leaves after 5 days. How long does B take to finish?

**LCM Method:**
LCM(15, 20) = 60. A = 4/day, B = 3/day.

Work in 5 days together = 5 × (4+3) = 35 units
Remaining = 60 − 35 = 25 units
B alone: 25/3 = **8⅓ days**

Total time = 5 + 8⅓ = **13⅓ days**

---

### ─── INTERMEDIATE LEVEL ───

#### Example 7: A Joins Later

> A can complete a job in 10 days and B in 15 days. B starts the work, A joins after 5 days. When is the work completed?

**LCM Method:**
LCM(10,15) = 30. A = 3/day, B = 2/day.

B works 5 days alone = 5 × 2 = 10 units.
Remaining = 30 − 10 = 20 units.
Together: 3 + 2 = 5/day.
Time for remaining = 20/5 = 4 days.

**Total time = 5 + 4 = 9 days**

---

#### Example 8: Working on Alternate Days

> A can do work in 10 days, B in 15 days. They work on alternate days with A starting. When is work done?

**LCM Method:**
Total work = 30 units. A = 3/day, B = 2/day.

In every 2-day cycle: A + B = 3 + 2 = 5 units.
Number of complete 2-day cycles = 30/5 = 6 cycles = 12 days.

Work done in 12 days = 30 units → Exactly done!

**Work completed in 12 days.**

> If not exact, check the last few days separately.

---

#### Example 9: Alternate Days — Work Not Exact

> A finishes in 6 days, B in 8 days. Alternate days, A starts. When done?

LCM(6,8) = 24. A = 4/day, B = 3/day.

2-day cycle = 7 units. 24/7 = 3 remainder 3.
After 6 days (3 cycles): 21 units done. Remaining = 3.
Day 7: A works → does 4 units but only 3 needed.
Time on Day 7 = 3/4 day.

**Total = 6 + 3/4 = 6¾ days**

---

#### Example 10: Efficiency Given Directly

> A is twice as efficient as B. Together they complete work in 18 days. How long does each take alone?

**Solution:**

Let B's rate = 1 unit/day → A's rate = 2 units/day.
Together = 3 units/day, finish in 18 days → Total = 54 units.

A alone = 54/2 = **27 days**
B alone = 54/1 = **54 days**

**Verify:** 1/27 + 1/54 = 2/54 + 1/54 = 3/54 = 1/18 ✓

---

#### Example 11: A is x Times More Efficient Than B

> A is 3 times as efficient as B. A finishes work in 12 days. How long for B and together?

A's rate = 3 × B's rate
If A takes 12 days → B takes 12 × 3 = **36 days**

Together: 1/12 + 1/36 = 3/36 + 1/36 = 4/36 = 1/9

**Together = 9 days**

---

#### Example 12: B is Slower Fraction of A

> A can finish in 15 days. B takes 50% more time than A. C takes 25% less time than A. How long do all three together?

A = 15 days
B = 15 × 1.5 = 22.5 days
C = 15 × 0.75 = 11.25 days

LCM approach — use rates:
Let total = 1.
1/15 + 1/22.5 + 1/11.25 = 1/15 + 2/45 + 4/45 = 3/45 + 2/45 + 4/45 = 9/45 = 1/5

**Together = 5 days**

---

#### Example 13: Wages Problem

> A and B together do work in 12 days. A alone takes 20 days. They are paid ₹4800. Find each person's share.

LCM(12,20) = 60. Together rate = 5. A's rate = 3. B's rate = 2.

Wages are distributed in ratio of work done:
A : B = 3 : 2

A's share = (3/5) × 4800 = **₹2880**
B's share = (2/5) × 4800 = **₹1920**

---

#### Example 14: Time When All Three Work Partially

> A, B, C take 10, 12, 15 days respectively. A and B work for 3 days, then C joins. When is work done?

LCM(10,12,15) = 60. A=6, B=5, C=4 units/day.

Work in 3 days (A+B) = 3 × 11 = 33 units.
Remaining = 27. All three = 6+5+4 = 15/day.
Time = 27/15 = 9/5 = **1.8 days**

**Total = 3 + 1.8 = 4.8 days**

---

### ─── ADVANCED LEVEL ───

#### Example 15: Work and Rest

> A can finish work in 20 days. He works for 3 days and rests for 1 day alternately. When is work done?

LCM method: Total = 20 units (rate = 1/day).

In every 4-day block: works 3 days → 3 units.
Number of complete 4-day blocks = 20/3 = 6 blocks (18 units) + 2 remaining.

After 6 × 4 = 24 days: 18 units done. Remaining = 2.
Day 25: works → 1 unit. Remaining = 1.
Day 26: works → 1 unit. Done!

**Total = 26 days**

---

#### Example 16: Worker Joins on Specific Conditions

> A, B, C can do a job in 10, 12, 15 days. They start together but A leaves 2 days before completion, B leaves 1 day before. C works till end. Find total duration.

Let total time = T days.

Work done by A = (T−2) days
Work done by B = (T−1) days
Work done by C = T days

$$\frac{T-2}{10} + \frac{T-1}{12} + \frac{T}{15} = 1$$

LCM(10,12,15) = 60. Multiply through:
$$6(T-2) + 5(T-1) + 4T = 60$$
$$6T - 12 + 5T - 5 + 4T = 60$$
$$15T - 17 = 60$$
$$15T = 77$$
$$T = \frac{77}{15} = 5\frac{2}{15} \text{ days}$$

---

#### Example 17: Group Work (M×D = Work)

> 10 men can build a wall in 8 days. How many men needed to build in 5 days?

Using: **Men × Days = Constant** (same work)

$$10 \times 8 = M \times 5$$
$$M = \frac{80}{5} = 16 \text{ men}$$

---

#### Example 18: MDH Formula

> 12 men working 8 hours/day can complete a job in 10 days. How many men working 6 hours/day can do it in 8 days?

Using: **M₁ × D₁ × H₁ = M₂ × D₂ × H₂**

$$12 \times 10 \times 8 = M_2 \times 8 \times 6$$
$$960 = 48 M_2$$
$$M_2 = 20 \text{ men}$$

---

#### Example 19: Combined Efficiency with Part Completion

> A completes 1/3 of work in 5 days. B completes 2/5 of remaining in 6 days. C completes the rest in 8 days. Who is most efficient?

Work done by A in 1 day = (1/3)/5 = 1/15
After A: remaining = 2/3. B does 2/5 of 2/3 = 4/15 in 6 days.
B's rate = (4/15)/6 = 4/90 = 2/45 per day.
Remaining = 2/3 − 4/15 = 10/15 − 4/15 = 6/15 = 2/5.
C does 2/5 in 8 days → C's rate = (2/5)/8 = 2/40 = 1/20.

Rates: A = 1/15, B = 2/45, C = 1/20.

Convert: A = 3/45, B = 2/45, C = 2.25/45.

**A is most efficient.**

---

#### Example 20: Unknown Number of Days from Ratio

> The ratio of efficiencies of A and B is 5:3. Working together they finish in 15 days. Find individual times.

Let A's rate = 5k, B's rate = 3k.
Together = 8k/day. Total work = 8k × 15 = 120k.

A alone = 120k/5k = **24 days**
B alone = 120k/3k = **40 days**

## 5. PIPES & CISTERNS

---

### 🔑 Core Idea:

Pipes & Cisterns is **exactly the same as Time & Work** with one twist:

| Time & Work | Pipes & Cisterns |
|------------|-----------------|
| Person completes work | Inlet pipe fills tank |
| Work done | Volume filled |
| Negative work | Outlet (leak) pipe |

---

### Key Rules:

- **Inlet pipe** → Fills tank → **Positive rate**
- **Outlet pipe (leak)** → Empties tank → **Negative rate**
- If pipe fills in **n hours** → rate = **+1/n per hour**
- If pipe empties in **n hours** → rate = **−1/n per hour**

---

### Formula:

$$\text{Net rate} = \frac{1}{A} - \frac{1}{B}$$

(A = fill time, B = empty time)

$$\text{Time to fill} = \frac{AB}{B - A}$$

---

#### Example 21: Basic Pipe Problem

> Pipe A fills tank in 6 hours. Pipe B fills in 4 hours. Both open together. How long?

$$T = \frac{6 \times 4}{6 + 4} = \frac{24}{10} = 2.4 \text{ hours} = 2 \text{ hours } 24 \text{ min}$$

---

#### Example 22: Fill and Leak Together

> Pipe A fills in 8 hours. Pipe B (leak) empties in 12 hours. Both open. How long to fill?

$$\text{Net rate} = \frac{1}{8} - \frac{1}{12} = \frac{3-2}{24} = \frac{1}{24}$$

**Time = 24 hours**

---

#### Example 23: Leak Discovered

> A tank is full. Pipe A empties it in 10 hours. With a leak, it empties in 15 hours. How long would the leak alone take to empty?

$$\frac{1}{\text{leak}} = \frac{1}{10} - \frac{1}{15} = \frac{3-2}{30} = \frac{1}{30}$$

**Leak alone empties in 30 hours.**

---

#### Example 24: Partial Fill then Both Pipes

> A fills in 12 hrs, B in 15 hrs, C (outlet) in 20 hrs. All three open. Time to fill empty tank?

LCM(12,15,20) = 60.
A = 5, B = 4, C = −3 (empties).
Net = 5 + 4 − 3 = 6 units/hr.
Time = 60/6 = **10 hours**

---

#### Example 25: Tank 1/3 Full

> A pipe fills tank in 9 hours. Tank is already 1/3 full. How long to fill completely?

Remaining = 2/3 of tank.
Time = (2/3) × 9 = **6 hours**

---

#### Example 26: Two Pipes, One Opens Late

> Pipe A fills in 10 hrs, B in 15 hrs. A starts. After 4 hrs, B also opens. Total time?

Work by A in 4 hrs = 4/10 = 2/5.
Remaining = 3/5.
Together rate = 1/10 + 1/15 = 3/30 + 2/30 = 5/30 = 1/6.
Time for remaining = (3/5)/(1/6) = (3/5) × 6 = 18/5 = 3.6 hrs.

**Total = 4 + 3.6 = 7.6 hours = 7 hours 36 min**

---

#### Example 27: Find Leak Rate

> A tank fills in 6 hrs without leak. With a leak at the bottom it fills in 8 hrs. If the tank is full, how long does the leak take to empty it?

$$\frac{1}{\text{leak}} = \frac{1}{6} - \frac{1}{8} = \frac{4-3}{24} = \frac{1}{24}$$

**Leak alone empties in 24 hours.**

## 6. WORK & WAGES

---

### 🔑 Core Rule:

> **Wages are distributed in proportion to work done (not time spent).**

$$\frac{\text{Wage of A}}{\text{Wage of B}} = \frac{\text{Work done by A}}{\text{Work done by B}} = \frac{\text{Rate of A}}{\text{Rate of B}}$$

---

#### Example 28: Direct Wage Split

> A, B, C do work in 6, 8, 12 days. Total wage = ₹1800. Find each share.

LCM(6,8,12) = 24.
A = 4, B = 3, C = 2 units/day.

If they all work the full duration together:
Together = 9/day. Days = 24/9 = 8/3 days.

Work done by each in 8/3 days:
A: 4 × 8/3 = 32/3
B: 3 × 8/3 = 8
C: 2 × 8/3 = 16/3

Ratio = 32/3 : 8 : 16/3 = 32 : 24 : 16 = **4 : 3 : 2**

A's wage = (4/9) × 1800 = **₹800**
B's wage = (3/9) × 1800 = **₹600**
C's wage = (2/9) × 1800 = **₹400**

---

#### Example 29: A Works Fewer Days

> A, B, C together take 10 days. A works 8 days, B works 6 days, C works 4 days. Total wage = ₹4500. Find shares.

Rate: Together 10 days → combined rate = 1/10.
But we need individual rates... assume equal efficiency for now.

Actually: Work done by A, B, C is proportional to days worked (if same rate):

But rates differ. Let's re-read: "A, B, C together take 10 days" with their combined efficiency.

We need more info. In the typical exam version:

> **A alone = 20, B alone = 30, C alone = 60. All work full 10 days. Wage ₹4500.**

LCM(20,30,60) = 60. A=3, B=2, C=1. In 10 days:
A does: 30 units. B: 20 units. C: 10 units.
Ratio = 3:2:1.
A = ₹2250, B = ₹1500, C = ₹750.

---

#### Example 30: Contract Wage Problem

> A contract for ₹750 is to be completed in 30 days. A alone can complete it in 50 days. After 20 days, B helps and work is completed on time. Find B's share.

Work by A in 20 days = 20/50 = 2/5.
Remaining = 3/5 done in 10 days (30−20) by A and B together.

A's work in last 10 days = 10/50 = 1/5.
B's work = 3/5 − 1/5 = 2/5.
A's total work = 2/5 + 1/5 = 3/5.

Ratio A:B = 3/5 : 2/5 = **3:2**.
B's share = (2/5) × 750 = **₹300**.

## 7. EFFICIENCY & MORE/LESS WORKERS

---

### The MDH (Man-Day-Hour) Formula:

$$\boxed{M_1 \times D_1 \times H_1 = M_2 \times D_2 \times H_2}$$

(When the amount of work W is the same)

When work differs:

$$\frac{M_1 \times D_1 \times H_1}{W_1} = \frac{M_2 \times D_2 \times H_2}{W_2}$$

---

#### Example 31: Workers and Days

> If 15 workers can build a road in 24 days, how many days will 20 workers take?

$$15 \times 24 = 20 \times D_2$$
$$D_2 = \frac{15 \times 24}{20} = 18 \text{ days}$$

---

#### Example 32: Full MDH

> 6 men × 8 hrs × 12 days = same work as M₂ × 6 hrs × 10 days. Find M₂.

$$6 \times 8 \times 12 = M_2 \times 6 \times 10$$
$$576 = 60 M_2$$
$$M_2 = 9.6 \approx 10 \text{ men}$$

---

#### Example 33: More Workers, More Work

> 4 men and 6 women finish in 8 days. 3 men and 7 women finish in 10 days. In how many days will 10 women finish?

Let man's rate = m, woman's rate = w.

$$(4m + 6w) \times 8 = (3m + 7w) \times 10$$
$$32m + 48w = 30m + 70w$$
$$2m = 22w \Rightarrow m = 11w$$

Total work = (4 × 11w + 6w) × 8 = (44w + 6w) × 8 = 50w × 8 = 400w units.

10 women: rate = 10w.
Days = 400w / 10w = **40 days**

---

## 8. WORK DONE IN ALTERNATE DAYS

---

### Pattern 1: A and B work on alternate days

**If A starts:**
- Day 1: A works
- Day 2: B works
- Day 3: A works
- ... and so on

**2-day cycle work = A's rate + B's rate**

---

### Pattern 2: Check if work completes at end of A's day or B's day

After each complete cycle, check remaining work:
- If remaining ≤ A's daily rate → A finishes on next day (partial)
- If remaining > A's rate → B's turn next

---

#### Example 34: Alternate Days Advanced

> A finishes in 10 days, B in 12 days. Alternate days, B starts. When done?

LCM(10,12) = 60. A = 6/day, B = 5/day.
2-day cycle = 11 units.
Cycles = 60/11 = 5 complete cycles = 55 units in 10 days.
Remaining = 5 units.
Day 11: B works → 5 units. Done exactly!

**Work done in 11 days.**

---

## 9. MEN, WOMEN & CHILDREN PROBLEMS

---

### Key Approach:

1. Set up equations relating their rates.
2. Solve for individual rates.
3. Apply to the question.

---

#### Example 35: Classic Three-Type Problem

> 2 men, 3 women, and 4 children do work in 10 days.
> 3 men, 4 women, and 5 children do it in 8 days.
> Find how long 1 man, 1 woman, 1 child take alone (if one more equation given).

**Note:** For a complete solution you need 3 equations or one rate given directly.

Let m, w, c = daily rates of man, woman, child.

$$(2m + 3w + 4c) \times 10 = 1 \quad ...(i)$$
$$(3m + 4w + 5c) \times 8 = 1 \quad ...(ii)$$

From (i): 2m + 3w + 4c = 1/10
From (ii): 3m + 4w + 5c = 1/8

Multiply (i) by 8 and (ii) by 10:
16m + 24w + 32c = 4/5 ... (iii)
30m + 40w + 50c = 5/4 ... (iv)

Need a 3rd condition to solve uniquely. If given child's rate, substitute and solve.

---

#### Example 36: Standard Exam Pattern

> 1 man = 2 women = 3 children (in terms of work rate).
> A group of 2 men, 3 women, 5 children finishes in 20 days. Find time for 5 men alone.

1 man = 2 women → 1 woman = 1/2 man
1 man = 3 children → 1 child = 1/3 man

Convert all to man-units:
Group = 2 + 3×(1/2) + 5×(1/3) = 2 + 1.5 + 5/3 = 2 + 1.5 + 1.667 = 5.167 men

Total work = 5.167 × 20 = 103.33 man-days.

5 men: 103.33/5 ≈ **20.67 days**

## 10. NEGATIVE WORK / DESTRUCTION PROBLEMS

---

### When someone destroys while another builds:

$$\text{Net rate} = \text{Builder's rate} - \text{Destroyer's rate}$$

---

#### Example 37: Builder and Destroyer

> A can build a wall in 10 days. B can destroy it in 15 days. Both work simultaneously. When is wall completed?

Net rate = 1/10 − 1/15 = 3/30 − 2/30 = 1/30.
**Wall completed in 30 days.** (Net effect is building)

---

#### Example 38: Complex Scenario

> A builds in 30 days. B builds in 20 days. C destroys in 40 days. A and C work all days; B works only first 10 days. When is work done?

B's work in 10 days = 10/20 = 1/2.
Remaining = 1/2.

Net rate of A and C = 1/30 − 1/40 = 4/120 − 3/120 = 1/120.
Time for remaining 1/2 = (1/2)/(1/120) = 60 days.

**Total = 10 + 60 = 70 days**

## 11. QUICK TRICKS & SHORTCUTS

---

### 🚀 TRICK 1: Two-Person Formula

$$T = \frac{A \times B}{A + B}$$

Example: A=12, B=18 → T = 216/30 = **7.2 days**

---

### 🚀 TRICK 2: LCM Method — Always Use This

Total Work = LCM of all days.
Rate of each = Total Work / Their Days.
Combined rate = sum of all rates.
Answer = Total Work / Combined Rate.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 3: Efficiency Ratio → Time Ratio (Inverse)

If A : B efficiency = m : n, then time taken ratio = **n : m** (reversed!)

Example: A is 3 times efficient → A takes 1/3 the time of B.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 4: MDH Formula

$$M_1 D_1 H_1 = M_2 D_2 H_2$$

More men → fewer days (inverse). More hours → fewer days (inverse).

---

### 🚀 TRICK 5: One Person Leaves at the End

If A leaves **d** days before completion, the equation is:

$$\frac{T-d}{A} + \frac{T}{B} = 1$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK 6: Both Leave / Join Together — Work in Parts

Split the work into phases. Calculate work done in each phase.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 7: A Alone Takes? (from Together + B Alone)

$$\frac{1}{A} = \frac{1}{\text{Together}} - \frac{1}{B}$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK 8: Alternate Day Total (If Exact)

If 2-day cycle work divides total work exactly:
Days = (Total Work / Cycle Work) × 2

---

### 🚀 TRICK 9: Wages ∝ Work Done

Work done ratio = Rate ratio (if same time) = Days worked × Rate.
Always distribute wages in the ratio of total work done.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 10: Pipe Formula (Same as Work)

Fill pipe = positive. Drain pipe = negative.
Net rate = sum of all rates with signs.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 11: Men-Women Relation

Reduce all to a single unit using given equivalences.
Then apply MDH or LCM method.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 12: Work Equivalence Shortcut

If A is k times as efficient as B:
- A takes time = T/k (faster)
- B takes time = T × k (slower)

---

### 🚀 TRICK 13: Fractions of Work Done

"A does 1/x of work per day" — this directly gives rate = 1/x.

"A completed n/m of work" — work done = n/m, use this to find time or rate.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 14: Group Work Formula

If m₁ men take d₁ days and m₂ men take d₂ days (same work):
$$m_1 \times d_1 = m_2 \times d_2$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK 15: Critical Path for "When Does Work Finish"

For "A leaves x days before end" problems:
Let total days = T.
- A works for (T − x) days
- B works for T days
Set up: Work by A + Work by B = 1.

---

## 12. COMMON MISTAKE ALERTS

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 1: Adding Days Instead of Rates

**Wrong:** If A takes 6 days and B takes 4 days, together = 6+4 = 10 days.
**Right:** Together = (6×4)/(6+4) = 24/10 = 2.4 days. Always add **rates (1/days)**.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 2: Forgetting Negative Work for Outlet/Destroyer

When outlet pipes or destroyers are involved, their contribution is **subtracted**.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 3: Distributing Wages by Time Worked (Not Work Done)

If A works 5 days and B works 3 days at **same rate**, wages = 5:3.
But if rates differ, wage ratio = (A's rate × A's days) : (B's rate × B's days).

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 4: MDH — Forgetting to Flip (Inverse Relationship)

More men → LESS days (not more).
More hours/day → LESS days (not more).

$$M_1 D_1 = M_2 D_2 \Rightarrow D_2 = \frac{M_1 D_1}{M_2}$$

If M₂ > M₁, then D₂ < D₁ (fewer days).

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 5: Not Checking Alternate Day — Who Works Last?

In alternate day problems, the work may finish on A's turn or B's turn. Always check after the last complete cycle!

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 6: Efficiency vs Time Confusion

"A is 3 times more efficient" → A finishes in **1/3 the time** of B.
Many students think A finishes in **3× the time** — that's WRONG.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 7: LCM with Non-Integer Rates

If days are like 7.5 or 3⅓, convert to fractions first, then take LCM of the fractions.
Or just work with the fraction formula directly.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 8: Pipe Fills AND Drains — Net Direction

Always first find net rate. If negative → tank is draining, not filling. Re-read the question.

---

```
CORE FORMULA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Work = Rate × Time
Rate = 1/Days  (if total work = 1 unit)

TWO-PERSON FORMULA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
T = (A × B) / (A + B)
1/A_alone = 1/Together − 1/B_alone

THREE-PERSON FORMULA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
1/T = 1/A + 1/B + 1/C

LCM METHOD (FASTEST)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Total Work = LCM of all days
Each rate = Total Work / Their Days
Days Together = Total Work / Sum of Rates

MDH FORMULA (Men-Days-Hours)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
M₁ × D₁ × H₁ = M₂ × D₂ × H₂

EFFICIENCY ↔ TIME (INVERSE)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Efficiency ratio A:B = m:n → Time ratio A:B = n:m

WAGES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Wages ∝ Work Done ∝ (Rate × Days worked)
Split wages in ratio of work contributed

PIPES & CISTERNS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Inlet pipe → Positive rate (+1/n)
Outlet pipe → Negative rate (−1/n)
Net rate = Sum of all rates (with signs)

ALTERNATE DAYS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
2-day cycle = A's work + B's work
Find complete cycles, then check remaining days separately

NEGATIVE WORK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Net rate = Builder rate − Destroyer rate
Time = Total Work / Net Rate (only if positive)

SPECIAL SHORTCUT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
A alone takes √(x × y) more days than together
where x = extra days for A, y = extra days for B
Together time T = √(x × y)
```

## 🏆 TOP EXAM TIPS

1. **Always use LCM method** for 3 or more workers — eliminates fractions entirely.
2. **Efficiency and time are inversely proportional** — more efficient = fewer days.
3. **Wages are NEVER split equally** unless efficiencies are equal.
4. **MDH formula** — more workers or hours means fewer days (inverse).
5. **Alternate day problems** — check the last partial day carefully.
6. **Pipes: outlet is negative** — always subtract drain rates.
7. **"A leaves x days before end"** → set A's work time = (T−x), B's = T.
8. **Check if net rate is positive** in pipe/destruction problems before computing time.
9. **Men-women problems** — reduce everything to one unit (man-equivalent).
10. **Always verify your answer** by checking total work = sum of individual contributions.
