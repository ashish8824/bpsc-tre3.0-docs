## 1. THE BIG PICTURE — WHAT IS MENSURATION 2D?

---

### 🔑 Definition:

> **Mensuration** is the branch of mathematics that deals with **measurement of geometric figures** — their lengths, areas, and perimeters.
>
> **2D Mensuration** deals with **flat (plane) figures** that have only two dimensions — length and width (no depth/height).

---

### Two Key Measurements in 2D:

| Measurement | What It Measures | Unit |
|------------|-----------------|------|
| **Perimeter** | Total boundary length | cm, m, km |
| **Area** | Total surface enclosed | cm², m², km² |

---

### All 2D Shapes at a Glance:

## 2. CORE TERMINOLOGY

---

### 📐 Perimeter
Total distance around the boundary of a shape.
> Think: "fencing around a garden"

### 📦 Area
Total space enclosed within the boundary of a shape.
> Think: "carpet needed to cover a floor"

### 📏 Base (b)
Any side of a triangle/parallelogram chosen as reference.

### 📏 Height / Altitude (h)
Perpendicular distance from the base to the opposite vertex/side.

### 🔵 Radius (r)
Distance from centre to any point on the circle.

### 🔵 Diameter (d)
Distance across circle through centre = 2r.

### 🔵 Circumference
Perimeter of a circle = 2πr.

### 🔵 Chord
A line segment joining two points on the circle.

### 🔵 Arc
Part of the circumference.

### 🔵 Sector
"Pie slice" of circle — region between two radii and arc.

### 🔵 Segment
Region between a chord and the arc.

## 3. TRIANGLE — ALL TYPES & FORMULAS

---

### 📐 General Triangle

```
         A
        /\
       /  \
      /    \
     /      \
    B────────C
         a
```

- Sides: a, b, c
- Semi-perimeter: s = (a + b + c) / 2

---

### Formulas — Triangle:

| Formula | Expression |
|---------|-----------|
| **Perimeter** | a + b + c |
| **Area (base × height)** | ½ × base × height |
| **Area (Heron's Formula)** | √[s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)] |
| **Area (two sides + included angle)** | ½ × a × b × sin C |

---

### Types of Triangles:

---

#### Type 1: Equilateral Triangle (All sides equal = a)

```
        A
       /\
      /  \
     /    \
    /      \
   B────────C
       a
```

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| Perimeter | 3a |
| Height | (√3/2) × a |
| Area | (√3/4) × a² |
| Inradius | a/(2√3) = a√3/6 |
| Circumradius | a/√3 = a√3/3 |

---

#### Type 2: Isosceles Triangle (Two equal sides = a, base = b)

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| Perimeter | 2a + b |
| Height | √(a² − b²/4) |
| Area | (b/4) × √(4a² − b²) |

---

#### Type 3: Right-Angled Triangle (Hypotenuse = c)

```
    C
    |\
    | \
    |  \ c (hypotenuse)
  a |   \
    |    \
    A─────B
        b
```

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| Pythagoras | c² = a² + b² |
| Perimeter | a + b + c |
| Area | ½ × a × b (the two legs) |
| Inradius | r = (a + b − c)/2 |
| Circumradius | R = c/2 |

---

#### Type 4: Scalene Triangle — Heron's Formula

> Use when all three sides are given but height is NOT given.

$$\boxed{s = \frac{a+b+c}{2}, \quad \text{Area} = \sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}}$$

---

### Examples — Triangle:

#### Example 1: Equilateral Triangle

> An equilateral triangle has side 6 cm. Find area and perimeter.

Perimeter = 3 × 6 = **18 cm**
Area = (√3/4) × 6² = (√3/4) × 36 = 9√3 ≈ **15.59 cm²**

---

#### Example 2: Right Triangle

> A right triangle has legs 8 cm and 6 cm. Find hypotenuse, area, perimeter.

Hypotenuse = √(8² + 6²) = √(64+36) = √100 = 10 cm
Area = ½ × 8 × 6 = **24 cm²**
Perimeter = 6 + 8 + 10 = **24 cm**

---

#### Example 3: Heron's Formula

> Triangle with sides 7, 8, 9 cm. Find area.

s = (7+8+9)/2 = **12**
Area = √[12×(12−7)×(12−8)×(12−9)]
= √[12×5×4×3]
= √720
= 12√5 ≈ **26.83 cm²**

---

#### Example 4: Isosceles Triangle

> Isosceles triangle with equal sides = 10 cm and base = 12 cm. Find area.

Height = √(10² − 6²) = √(100−36) = √64 = 8 cm
Area = ½ × 12 × 8 = **48 cm²**

## 4. QUADRILATERALS

---

### 📦 Square (side = a)

```
    a
┌───────┐
│       │ a
│       │
└───────┘
    a
```

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| Perimeter | 4a |
| Area | a² |
| Diagonal | a√2 |
| Area from diagonal (d) | d²/2 |

---

### 📦 Rectangle (length = l, breadth = b)

```
        l
┌──────────────┐
│              │ b
└──────────────┘
        l
```

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| Perimeter | 2(l + b) |
| Area | l × b |
| Diagonal | √(l² + b²) |

---

### 📦 Parallelogram (base = b, height = h, side = a)

```
    ┌─────────────┐
   /               /
  /   b             /
 /                 /
└─────────────┘
```

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| Perimeter | 2(a + b) |
| Area | b × h |
| Area (diagonals d₁, d₂ at angle θ) | ½ × d₁ × d₂ × sin θ |

---

### 📦 Rhombus (all sides = a, diagonals d₁ and d₂)

```
      /\
     /  \
    /    \
    \    /
     \  /
      \/
```

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| Perimeter | 4a |
| Area | ½ × d₁ × d₂ |
| Area | a × h (base × height) |
| Side from diagonals | a = ½√(d₁² + d₂²) |

> **Key Fact:** Diagonals of rhombus bisect each other at RIGHT ANGLES.

---

### 📦 Trapezium (parallel sides a and b, height h)

```
      a
   ┌────────┐
  /          \
 /            \
└──────────────┘
         b
```

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| Area | ½ × (a + b) × h |
| Perimeter | a + b + sum of non-parallel sides |

---

### 📦 Kite (diagonals d₁ and d₂, where d₁ ⊥ d₂)

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| Area | ½ × d₁ × d₂ |

---

### Examples — Quadrilaterals:

#### Example 5: Square

> Square has diagonal 10 cm. Find area and perimeter.

Side = diagonal/√2 = 10/√2 = 5√2 cm.
Area = d²/2 = 100/2 = **50 cm²**
Perimeter = 4 × 5√2 = **20√2 ≈ 28.28 cm**

---

#### Example 6: Rectangle

> Length = 12 m, breadth = 5 m. Find diagonal, area, perimeter.

Diagonal = √(144 + 25) = √169 = **13 m**
Area = 12 × 5 = **60 m²**
Perimeter = 2(12+5) = **34 m**

---

#### Example 7: Rhombus

> Diagonals of a rhombus are 16 cm and 12 cm. Find area, side, perimeter.

Area = ½ × 16 × 12 = **96 cm²**
Side = ½√(16² + 12²) = ½√(256+144) = ½√400 = ½ × 20 = **10 cm**
Perimeter = 4 × 10 = **40 cm**

---

#### Example 8: Trapezium

> Parallel sides = 10 cm and 6 cm, height = 5 cm. Find area.

Area = ½ × (10+6) × 5 = ½ × 16 × 5 = **40 cm²**

---

#### Example 9: Parallelogram

> Base = 14 cm, corresponding height = 8 cm. Find area.

Area = 14 × 8 = **112 cm²**

## 5. CIRCLE — COMPLETE GUIDE

---

### 🔵 Circle Formulas (radius = r, diameter = d = 2r):

```
            ↑ r
     ───────●───────
        circumference = 2πr
```

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| Circumference | 2πr = πd |
| Area | πr² |
| Diameter | 2r |
| Area from diameter | π(d/2)² = πd²/4 |

> Use π = 22/7 for clean calculations, or π ≈ 3.14159 for decimals.

---

### Useful Values:

| Expression | Value (π = 22/7) |
|-----------|-----------------|
| π | 22/7 ≈ 3.14 |
| π/4 | 11/14 |
| 2π | 44/7 |
| √π | ≈ 1.772 |

---

### Examples — Circle:

#### Example 10: Basic Circle

> Radius = 7 cm. Find circumference and area.

Circumference = 2 × (22/7) × 7 = **44 cm**
Area = (22/7) × 7² = (22/7) × 49 = **154 cm²**

---

#### Example 11: From Circumference to Radius

> Circumference = 88 cm. Find radius and area.

2πr = 88 → r = 88/(2 × 22/7) = 88 × 7/44 = **14 cm**
Area = πr² = (22/7) × 196 = **616 cm²**

---

#### Example 12: Ratio of Radii — Areas

> Ratio of radii of two circles = 3:4. Find ratio of their areas.

Areas ∝ r². Ratio = 3²:4² = **9:16**

---

#### Example 13: Area from Circumference

> Circumference = C. Find area in terms of C.

r = C/(2π). Area = πr² = π × C²/(4π²) = **C²/(4π)**

---

## 6. SECTOR AND SEGMENT OF A CIRCLE

---

### 🍕 Sector (angle θ at centre):

```
      /  \
     /    \
    / θ°   \
   /────────\
    arc
```

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| Arc length | (θ/360) × 2πr |
| Area of sector | (θ/360) × πr² |
| Perimeter of sector | 2r + arc length |

> **If θ = 360°:** Full circle.
> **If θ = 180°:** Semicircle.
> **If θ = 90°:** Quarter circle.

---

### 🍰 Segment (region between chord and arc):

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| Area of minor segment | Area of sector − Area of triangle |
| Area of major segment | Area of circle − Area of minor segment |

---

### Examples — Sector & Segment:

#### Example 14: Sector

> Radius = 14 cm, angle = 90°. Find arc length and area of sector.

Arc length = (90/360) × 2 × (22/7) × 14 = (1/4) × 88 = **22 cm**
Area = (90/360) × (22/7) × 196 = (1/4) × 616 = **154 cm²**

---

#### Example 15: Sector Perimeter

> Radius = 10 cm, angle = 60°. Find perimeter of sector.

Arc = (60/360) × 2π × 10 = (1/6) × 20π = 10π/3 cm
Perimeter = 2 × 10 + 10π/3 = 20 + 10π/3 ≈ 20 + 10.47 = **30.47 cm**

---

#### Example 16: Segment Area

> In a circle of radius 10 cm, a chord subtends angle 60° at centre. Find area of minor segment.

Area of sector = (60/360) × π × 100 = 100π/6 = 50π/3 cm²
Triangle formed is equilateral (angle 60°, two radii = 10): Area = (√3/4) × 10² = 25√3 cm²
Area of segment = 50π/3 − 25√3 ≈ 52.36 − 43.30 ≈ **9.06 cm²**

### Examples — Other Shapes:

#### Example 17: Regular Hexagon

> A regular hexagon has side 8 cm. Find area and perimeter.

Perimeter = 6 × 8 = **48 cm**
Area = (3√3/2) × 64 = 96√3 ≈ **166.3 cm²**

---

#### Example 18: Semicircle

> Diameter = 14 cm. Find perimeter and area of semicircle.

r = 7. Perimeter = 7 × (22/7 + 2) = 7 × (22/7 + 14/7) = 7 × 36/7 = **36 cm**

## 8. COMBINED / COMPOSITE FIGURES

---

### Strategy for Composite Figures:

```
STEP 1: Identify the individual shapes
STEP 2: Split the figure into these shapes
STEP 3: Calculate area/perimeter of each
STEP 4: ADD areas OR SUBTRACT (for cut-out regions)
```

---

### Examples:

#### Example 19: Rectangle with Semicircle on Top

> A window is in the shape of a rectangle (8 m × 4 m) with a semicircle on top. Find total area.

Rectangle area = 8 × 4 = 32 m²
Semicircle: diameter = 8 m, radius = 4 m. Area = π × 16/2 = 8π m²
Total = 32 + 8π ≈ 32 + 25.13 = **57.13 m²**

---

#### Example 20: Square with Circle Cut Out

> From a square of side 14 cm, a circle of max diameter is cut. Find remaining area.

Square area = 196 cm²
Max circle: diameter = 14 cm → radius = 7 cm
Circle area = (22/7) × 49 = 154 cm²
Remaining = 196 − 154 = **42 cm²**

---

#### Example 21: Track Around a Rectangle

> A rectangular field 80 m × 60 m has a 7 m wide track around it inside. Find track area.

Outer rectangle = 80 × 60 = 4800 m²
Inner rectangle = (80−14) × (60−14) = 66 × 46 = 3036 m²
Track area = 4800 − 3036 = **1764 m²**

---

#### Example 22: Circle Inside Square

> A circle is inscribed in a square of side 10 cm. Find area of square not covered by circle.

Circle radius = 5 cm (touches all sides).
Circle area = π × 25 = 25π cm²
Remaining = 100 − 25π ≈ 100 − 78.54 = **21.46 cm²**

---

#### Example 23: Ring (Annulus)

> A ring has outer radius R = 10 cm and inner radius r = 6 cm. Find area of ring.

Area = π(R² − r²) = π(100 − 36) = 64π ≈ **201.06 cm²**

---

#### Example 24: Triangle on Rectangle

> A combined figure has a rectangle (6 m × 4 m) with a triangle on top (base 6 m, height 3 m). Find total area.

Rectangle = 24 m². Triangle = ½ × 6 × 3 = 9 m².
Total = **33 m²**

## 9. PATH / BORDER / FRAME PROBLEMS

---

### Type 1: Uniform Path OUTSIDE a Rectangle

> Outer dimensions = (l + 2w) and (b + 2w), where w = path width.

$$\text{Path Area} = (l + 2w)(b + 2w) - lb$$

---

### Type 2: Uniform Path INSIDE a Rectangle

> Inner dimensions = (l − 2w) and (b − 2w).

$$\text{Path Area} = lb - (l-2w)(b-2w)$$

---

### Type 3: Path Around a Circle

$$\text{Ring Area} = \pi(R^2 - r^2) = \pi(R+r)(R-r)$$

---

### Examples:

#### Example 25: Path Outside Rectangle

> Rectangle 50 m × 30 m. Path of 5 m width outside. Find path area.

Outer = (50+10) × (30+10) = 60 × 40 = 2400 m²
Inner (rectangle) = 1500 m²
Path = 2400 − 1500 = **900 m²**

---

#### Example 26: Path Inside Rectangle

> Rectangle 100 m × 80 m. Path of 5 m inside. Find path area.

Inner = (100−10) × (80−10) = 90 × 70 = 6300 m²
Path = 8000 − 6300 = **1700 m²**

---

#### Example 27: Circular Path

> A circular garden has radius 20 m. A path of 3.5 m width is built outside. Find path area.

R = 23.5 m (outer), r = 20 m (inner).
Path area = π(23.5² − 20²) = (22/7)(552.25 − 400) = (22/7)(152.25) ≈ **478.5 m²**

---

## 10. AREA CONVERSION PROBLEMS

---

### Critical Conversions:

$$1 \text{ m}^2 = 10,000 \text{ cm}^2$$

$$1 \text{ km}^2 = 1,000,000 \text{ m}^2 = 10^6 \text{ m}^2$$

$$1 \text{ hectare} = 10,000 \text{ m}^2$$

$$1 \text{ are} = 100 \text{ m}^2$$

---

### Examples:

#### Example 28: Conversion

> A field of 5 hectares. Express in m² and cm².

5 hectares = 5 × 10,000 = **50,000 m²**
= 50,000 × 10,000 = **5 × 10⁸ cm²**

---

#### Example 29: Cost of Carpeting

> A room 15 m × 10 m is to be carpeted. Cost = ₹25 per m². Find total cost.

Area = 150 m². Cost = 150 × 25 = **₹3750**

---

#### Example 30: Cost of Fencing

> Rectangular plot 60 m × 40 m. Fencing costs ₹12 per metre. Find cost.

Perimeter = 2(60+40) = 200 m. Cost = 200 × 12 = **₹2400**

## 11. QUICK TRICKS & SHORTCUTS

---

### 🚀 TRICK 1: Square from Diagonal

If diagonal = d: **Area = d²/2**

No need to find side first. Direct formula!

---

### 🚀 TRICK 2: Perimeter to Area (Square)

If perimeter = P → side = P/4 → Area = (P/4)²= P²/16

---

### 🚀 TRICK 3: Area to Perimeter (Square)

If area = A → side = √A → Perimeter = 4√A

---

### 🚀 TRICK 4: Area of Rhombus from Diagonals

**Area = ½ × d₁ × d₂**

Only need the diagonals. Side can be found from: side = ½√(d₁² + d₂²).

---

### 🚀 TRICK 5: Circumference to Area

If circumference = C → r = C/2π → Area = C²/4π

---

### 🚀 TRICK 6: Equilateral Triangle — Remember Only Area Formula

**Area = (√3/4)a²**

All other formulas derived:
- Height = (√3/2)a (this × base/2 = area)
- Perimeter = 3a

---

### 🚀 TRICK 7: % Change When Side Changes (Square/Circle)

If side/radius increases by x%:
- **Area increases by** (2x + x²/100)% ← exact
- **Approximate:** 2x% (for small x)

If side decreases by x%: Area decreases by (2x − x²/100)%

**Example:** Side increases 10% → Area increases = 2(10) + (10²/100) = 20 + 1 = **21%**

---

### 🚀 TRICK 8: Rectangle — Area Fixed, Different Dimensions

Perimeter is MINIMUM when shape is a square (for fixed area).
Area is MAXIMUM when shape is a square (for fixed perimeter).

---

### 🚀 TRICK 9: Trapezium Quick Area

Area = ½ × (sum of parallel sides) × height

Memory: "Half the sum of parallel sides times height."

---

### 🚀 TRICK 10: Path Problems — Quick Formula

**Outside path (width w):**
Path area = 2w(l + b + 2w)

**Inside path (width w):**
Path area = 2w(l + b − 2w)

---

### 🚀 TRICK 11: Ratio of Areas When Sides in Ratio

If sides are in ratio m:n, then areas are in ratio **m²:n²**.
If sides are in ratio m:n, then perimeters are in ratio **m:n** (same ratio).

---

### 🚀 TRICK 12: Circle — Area from Circumference Directly

Area = C²/(4π) where C = circumference.
Or: Area = (1/4π) × C²

---

### 🚀 TRICK 13: Triangle — Area without Height

When 3 sides given: Use Heron's Formula.
When 2 sides and included angle given: Area = ½ab sinC.
For right triangle: Area = ½ × leg₁ × leg₂.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 14: Diagonal of Rectangle

d = √(l² + b²). Also: (l + b)² − (l − b)² = 4lb (useful relationship).

---

### 🚀 TRICK 15: Area Scaling

If linear dimensions are scaled by factor k:
- All lengths scale by k
- All areas scale by k²

**Example:** If each side doubled (k=2), area becomes 4 times.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 16: Common Pythagorean Triplets

| Triplet | Remember |
|---------|---------|
| 3, 4, 5 | Most common |
| 5, 12, 13 | Exam favourite |
| 8, 15, 17 | Common |
| 7, 24, 25 | Common |
| 6, 8, 10 | (3,4,5 doubled) |
| 9, 12, 15 | (3,4,5 tripled) |

---

### 🚀 TRICK 17: Semi-perimeter in Heron's Formula

s = (a+b+c)/2. If you know s, you can find sides quickly.
Also: (s−a) + (s−b) + (s−c) = s (useful check).

---

### 🚀 TRICK 18: Inscribed Circle of Triangle (Inradius)

$$r = \frac{\text{Area}}{s}$$

Where s = semi-perimeter. Use this to find inradius quickly.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 19: Circumscribed Circle (Circumradius)

$$R = \frac{abc}{4 \times \text{Area}}$$

For right triangle: R = hypotenuse/2.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 20: Equal Perimeters — Which Has More Area?

Circle > Square > Equilateral Triangle (for same perimeter).

Circle always encloses maximum area for given perimeter!

## 12. COMMON MISTAKE ALERTS

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 1: Forgetting to Double for Perimeter

"Rectangle has l and b" → Perimeter = **2(l+b)**, NOT l+b.
Many students write l+b instead of 2(l+b).

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 2: Using Diameter Instead of Radius in Circle Formulas

Area = πr² (radius, NOT diameter!).
If diameter d is given: r = d/2. Then Area = π(d/2)² = πd²/4.

**Wrong:** Area = πd²
**Right:** Area = πd²/4

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 3: Path Width Applied Only Once (Not Both Sides)

If path is around a rectangle, BOTH length and breadth increase by **2w** (w on each side).
**Wrong:** New length = l + w
**Right:** New length = l + 2w

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 4: Perimeter of Semicircle — Missing Diameter

Perimeter of semicircle = πr + 2r (the straight edge diameter must be included!).
**Wrong:** Perimeter = πr
**Right:** Perimeter = πr + 2r = r(π + 2)

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 5: Heron's Formula — Using Sides Directly

In Heron's formula: Use (s−a), (s−b), (s−c), NOT the sides themselves.
**Wrong:** Area = √(a × b × c × s)
**Right:** Area = √[s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)]

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 6: Area of Rhombus ≠ Side²

Rhombus area = ½ × d₁ × d₂ (product of diagonals ÷ 2), NOT side².
(Side² works only for square, where diagonals are equal and perpendicular.)

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 7: Units — Not Squaring Units for Area

Length in cm → Area in **cm²** (square centimetres).
Always write the correct unit with your answer.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 8: Sector Angle Must Be in Degrees (or Convert Properly)

Arc length = (θ/360) × 2πr → θ must be in **degrees**.
If θ in radians: Arc = r × θ.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 9: Parallelogram Height ≠ Side

Area of parallelogram = base × HEIGHT (perpendicular distance), NOT base × slant side.
Many students multiply base × slant side (which gives wrong answer).

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 10: Scale Factor — Squaring for Area

If scale 1 cm = 5 m, then:
1 cm² on paper = 25 m² in real life (must SQUARE the scale factor for area).

## 🏆 TOP EXAM TIPS

1. **Always write the unit** — cm², m², km² for area; cm, m for perimeter. Marks are lost for missing units.
2. **Semicircle perimeter = πr + 2r** — the diameter (straight edge) must be included. Most common mistake!
3. **Path problems — use 2w on each side** — if path width = w, both dimensions increase by 2w for outside path.
4. **For quick square area from diagonal** — use d²/2 directly. Fastest approach in exams.
5. **Heron's Formula** — always calculate s first, then (s−a), (s−b), (s−c) separately before multiplying.
6. **Circle with π = 22/7** — when radius is a multiple of 7, the calculation becomes very clean.
7. **Ratio of areas = square of ratio of sides** — sides 3:4 → areas 9:16.
8. **For composite figures** — identify whether to ADD (combining shapes) or SUBTRACT (cutout regions).
9. **Right triangle** — identify Pythagorean triplets (3-4-5, 5-12-13) to avoid square root calculations.
10. **Verify your answer** — area should always be positive; perimeter should be greater than any single side.

*Mensuration 2D: Every shape has its own formula, but the logic is always the same — boundary for perimeter, enclosed space for area. Memorise the key formulas and patterns, and no shape will confuse you! 💪*
