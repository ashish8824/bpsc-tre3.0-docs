## 1. CORE CONCEPTS & THE GOLDEN TRIANGLE

---

### 🔑 The One Formula That Rules Everything:

$$\boxed{Distance = Speed \times Time}$$

$$\boxed{Speed = \frac{Distance}{Time}}$$

$$\boxed{Time = \frac{Distance}{Speed}}$$

---

### The Golden Triangle — Never Forget:

```
           Distance (D)
               / \
              /   \
             /     \
            /       \
     Speed ×  Time  =  Distance
      (S)               (T)

Cover what you want to FIND.
What remains tells you the operation:

  Cover D → S × T
  Cover S → D ÷ T
  Cover T → D ÷ S
```

---

### The Core Relationships:

| If Speed ↑ (Distance same) | Time ↓ (Inverse relation) |
| If Time ↑ (Distance same) | Speed ↓ (Inverse relation) |
| If Speed ↑ (Time same) | Distance ↑ (Direct relation) |

---

### Key Principle — Proportionality:

When **Distance is constant**:
$$\frac{S_1}{S_2} = \frac{T_2}{T_1} \quad \text{(Speed and Time are INVERSELY proportional)}$$

When **Time is constant**:
$$\frac{D_1}{D_2} = \frac{S_1}{S_2} \quad \text{(Distance and Speed are DIRECTLY proportional)}$$

## 2. UNIT CONVERSIONS — THE MOST TESTED BASICS

---

### 🚨 The Two Conversion Factors You MUST Memorise:

$$\boxed{1 \text{ km/hr} = \frac{5}{18} \text{ m/s}}$$

$$\boxed{1 \text{ m/s} = \frac{18}{5} \text{ km/hr} = 3.6 \text{ km/hr}}$$

---

### Why These Work:

1 km = 1000 m and 1 hr = 3600 s

$$1 \text{ km/hr} = \frac{1000 \text{ m}}{3600 \text{ s}} = \frac{5}{18} \text{ m/s}$$

---

### Conversion Table:

| km/hr | m/s | km/hr | m/s |
|-------|-----|-------|-----|
| 18 | 5 | 36 | 10 |
| 54 | 15 | 72 | 20 |
| 90 | 25 | 108 | 30 |
| 126 | 35 | 144 | 40 |

> **Memory Trick:** Multiply km/hr by 5/18 → get m/s. Multiply m/s by 18/5 → get km/hr.

---

### Distance Conversions:

| Unit | Equivalent |
|------|-----------|
| 1 km | 1000 m |
| 1 mile | 1.6 km (approx) |
| 1 yard | 0.9144 m |

---

### Time Conversions:

| Unit | Equivalent |
|------|-----------|
| 1 hour | 60 minutes = 3600 seconds |
| 1 minute | 60 seconds |

---

### Quick Conversion Examples:

> Convert 90 km/hr to m/s:
$$90 \times \frac{5}{18} = 5 \times 5 = 25 \text{ m/s}$$

> Convert 15 m/s to km/hr:
$$15 \times \frac{18}{5} = 3 \times 18 = 54 \text{ km/hr}$$

## 3. FUNDAMENTAL FORMULAS & DERIVED RELATIONS

---

### Basic Formula:

$$D = S \times T$$

---

### When Speed Changes — Same Distance:

If a person travels from A to B at speed S₁ and returns at S₂:

$$\text{Average Speed} = \frac{2S_1 S_2}{S_1 + S_2}$$

---

### Time Gained/Lost Due to Speed Change:

If a person increases speed from S₁ to S₂ over distance D:

$$\text{Time difference} = D \times \left(\frac{1}{S_1} - \frac{1}{S_2}\right)$$

---

### If Late/Early Due to Speed:

Let actual speed = S. If person goes at speed S₁ → reaches T₁ late.
If person goes at S₂ → reaches T₂ early.

$$\text{Distance} = \frac{S_1 \times S_2 \times (T_1 + T_2)}{S_2 - S_1}$$

---

### Distance When Two People Start at Same Time:

If A and B start from same point in same direction:
- A's speed = S_A, B's speed = S_B (S_A > S_B)
- Time for A to be d metres ahead = d / (S_A − S_B)

---

## 4. AVERAGE SPEED — THE MOST CONFUSED TOPIC

---

### ⚠️ CRITICAL RULE:

> **Average Speed ≠ (S₁ + S₂)/2**
>
> Average Speed = **Total Distance / Total Time**
>
> The simple average (S₁+S₂)/2 is ONLY valid when time spent at each speed is EQUAL.

---

### Case 1: Same Distance at Two Speeds (Most Common)

$$\boxed{\text{Average Speed} = \frac{2 S_1 S_2}{S_1 + S_2}}$$

This is the **Harmonic Mean** of the two speeds.

---

### Case 2: Same Time at Two Speeds

$$\text{Average Speed} = \frac{S_1 + S_2}{2}$$

Here the arithmetic mean IS valid.

---

### Case 3: Three Speeds Over Equal Distances

$$\text{Average Speed} = \frac{3 S_1 S_2 S_3}{S_1 S_2 + S_2 S_3 + S_3 S_1}$$

---

### Examples — Average Speed:

#### Example 1:

> A goes from P to Q at 40 km/hr and returns at 60 km/hr. Find average speed.

$$\text{Avg Speed} = \frac{2 \times 40 \times 60}{40 + 60} = \frac{4800}{100} = 48 \text{ km/hr}$$

---

#### Example 2: Three Segments

> A travels 1/3 of journey at 20 km/hr, 1/3 at 30 km/hr, 1/3 at 60 km/hr. Average speed?

$$\text{Avg Speed} = \frac{3 \times 20 \times 30 \times 60}{20 \times 30 + 30 \times 60 + 60 \times 20}$$
$$= \frac{108000}{600 + 1800 + 1200} = \frac{108000}{3600} = 30 \text{ km/hr}$$

---

#### Example 3: Different Distances, Different Speeds

> Travels 60 km at 30 km/hr and 90 km at 45 km/hr. Average speed?

Time₁ = 60/30 = 2 hrs. Time₂ = 90/45 = 2 hrs.
Total D = 150 km. Total T = 4 hrs.

$$\text{Avg Speed} = \frac{150}{4} = 37.5 \text{ km/hr}$$

---

## 5. RELATIVE SPEED — WHEN OBJECTS MOVE TOGETHER

---

### 🔑 The Master Rule:

| Direction | Relative Speed |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Same direction** | S₁ **−** S₂ (subtract) |
| **Opposite direction** | S₁ **+** S₂ (add) |

---

### Why This Works:

Imagine you're in a car at 60 km/hr:
- Another car at 40 km/hr going SAME way → appears to move at 60−40 = **20 km/hr**
- A car at 40 km/hr coming TOWARDS you → appears to move at 60+40 = **100 km/hr**

---

### Formula for Meeting Time:

$$\text{Time to meet} = \frac{\text{Initial distance between them}}{\text{Relative Speed}}$$

---

### Examples — Relative Speed:

#### Example 4:

> A and B are 300 km apart. A moves at 40 km/hr towards B. B moves at 60 km/hr towards A. When do they meet?

Relative speed (opposite) = 40 + 60 = 100 km/hr.
Time = 300/100 = **3 hours**

---

#### Example 5:

> A and B start from same point. A at 50 km/hr, B at 30 km/hr. Same direction. After how long is A 40 km ahead?

Relative speed = 50 − 30 = 20 km/hr.
Time = 40/20 = **2 hours**

---

#### Example 6:

> Two trains start from stations 500 km apart towards each other at 70 and 80 km/hr. A bird flies at 200 km/hr between the trains. Total distance bird covers before trains meet?

Train meeting time = 500/(70+80) = 500/150 = 10/3 hrs.
Bird distance = 200 × 10/3 = **2000/3 ≈ 666.67 km**

## 6. TRAIN PROBLEMS

---

### 🔑 The Single Most Important Concept in Train Problems:

> **A train crosses an object by travelling a distance = Length of train + Length of object**

---

### Four Key Scenarios:

| Scenario | Distance Covered by Train |
|----------|--------------------------|
| Cross a **pole / person** (no length) | = Length of train |
| Cross a **platform / bridge** | = Length of train + Length of platform |
| Cross another **train (opposite dir)** | = Sum of both train lengths |
| Cross another **train (same dir)** | = Sum of both train lengths |

---

### Formulas:

**Time to cross a pole:**
$$T = \frac{L_{train}}{S_{train}}$$

**Time to cross a platform:**
$$T = \frac{L_{train} + L_{platform}}{S_{train}}$$

**Two trains, opposite direction:**
$$T = \frac{L_1 + L_2}{S_1 + S_2}$$

**Two trains, same direction:**
$$T = \frac{L_1 + L_2}{|S_1 - S_2|}$$

---

### Examples — Train Problems:

#### Example 7: Train Crosses a Pole

> A train 200 m long travels at 72 km/hr. Time to cross a pole?

Speed = 72 × 5/18 = 20 m/s.
Time = 200/20 = **10 seconds**

---

#### Example 8: Train Crosses a Platform

> A train 300 m long at 54 km/hr crosses a platform in 30 seconds. Find platform length.

Speed = 54 × 5/18 = 15 m/s.
Distance = 15 × 30 = 450 m.
Platform = 450 − 300 = **150 m**

---

#### Example 9: Two Trains Opposite Direction

> Train A (150 m, 60 km/hr) and Train B (200 m, 90 km/hr) approaching each other. Time to pass?

Relative speed = 60 + 90 = 150 km/hr = 150 × 5/18 = 125/3 m/s.
Total distance = 150 + 200 = 350 m.
Time = 350 ÷ (125/3) = 350 × 3/125 = **8.4 seconds**

---

#### Example 10: Two Trains Same Direction

> Train A (100 m, 80 km/hr) overtakes Train B (120 m, 60 km/hr). Time to pass?

Relative speed = 80 − 60 = 20 km/hr = 20 × 5/18 = 50/9 m/s.
Total = 100 + 120 = 220 m.
Time = 220 ÷ (50/9) = 220 × 9/50 = **39.6 seconds**

---

#### Example 11: Find Train Length from Two Crossings

> A train crosses a pole in 12 sec and a 200 m platform in 20 sec. Find length and speed.

Speed = L/12 (crosses pole).
Speed = (L + 200)/20 (crosses platform).

$$\frac{L}{12} = \frac{L + 200}{20}$$
$$20L = 12L + 2400$$
$$8L = 2400 \Rightarrow L = 300 \text{ m}$$

Speed = 300/12 = 25 m/s = **90 km/hr**

---

#### Example 12: Train Crosses a Running Man

> A train 200 m long crosses a man running at 10 km/hr in same direction in 20 sec. Find train speed.

Relative speed = (S_train − 10) km/hr.
Convert: Relative speed in m/s = 200/20 = 10 m/s = 36 km/hr.
Train speed = 36 + 10 = **46 km/hr**

---

#### Example 13: Train Crosses a Man — Opposite Direction

> A 300 m train crosses a man walking at 6 km/hr opposite to train in 18 sec. Find train speed.

Relative speed = (S + 6) km/hr.
In m/s: 300/18 = 50/3 m/s = 50/3 × 18/5 = 60 km/hr.
Train speed = 60 − 6 = **54 km/hr**

---

#### Example 14: Find Time When Both Train Speed and Length Change

> Two trains of length 120 m and 150 m pass each other in 15 sec (opposite). The faster is twice the slower. Find individual speeds.

Total distance = 270 m. Time = 15 s. Relative speed = 270/15 = 18 m/s.
In km/hr = 18 × 18/5 = 64.8 km/hr.

S₁ + S₂ = 64.8, S₁ = 2S₂.
3S₂ = 64.8 → S₂ = 21.6 km/hr, S₁ = **43.2 km/hr**

---

## 7. BOAT & STREAM PROBLEMS

---

### 🔑 Core Concept:

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **u** = Speed of boat in still water | Boat's own speed |
| **v** = Speed of stream (current) | River's speed |
| **Downstream** | Boat + Current (same direction) |
| **Upstream** | Boat − Current (opposite direction) |

---

### The Four Key Formulas:

$$\boxed{D_s = u + v \quad \text{(Downstream speed)}}$$

$$\boxed{U_s = u - v \quad \text{(Upstream speed)}}$$

$$\boxed{u = \frac{D_s + U_s}{2} \quad \text{(Boat speed in still water)}}$$

$$\boxed{v = \frac{D_s - U_s}{2} \quad \text{(Stream speed)}}$$

---

### Memory Aid:

```
     DOWNSTREAM: Boat + Current → FASTER  (D_s = u + v)
     UPSTREAM:   Boat − Current → SLOWER  (U_s = u − v)

     Boat speed  = (Downstream + Upstream) / 2
     Stream speed = (Downstream − Upstream) / 2
```

---

### Examples — Boats & Streams:

#### Example 15: Basic Downstream/Upstream

> A boat goes 30 km downstream in 2 hrs and 18 km upstream in 3 hrs. Find boat speed and stream speed.

Downstream speed = 30/2 = 15 km/hr.
Upstream speed = 18/3 = 6 km/hr.

$$u = \frac{15 + 6}{2} = 10.5 \text{ km/hr}$$
$$v = \frac{15 - 6}{2} = 4.5 \text{ km/hr}$$

---

#### Example 16: Time for Round Trip

> Boat speed in still water = 12 km/hr. Stream = 4 km/hr. Time to go 48 km and return?

Downstream speed = 16 km/hr. Upstream speed = 8 km/hr.
Time downstream = 48/16 = 3 hrs.
Time upstream = 48/8 = 6 hrs.
**Total = 9 hours**

---

#### Example 17: Find Distance (Classic)

> A man rows 40 km downstream in 4 hrs. He returns in 8 hrs. Find stream speed and still water speed.

Downstream = 40/4 = 10 km/hr.
Upstream = 40/8 = 5 km/hr.
u = (10+5)/2 = **7.5 km/hr** | v = (10−5)/2 = **2.5 km/hr**

---

#### Example 18: Man Rows to Place and Back

> A man can row 8 km/hr in still water. Stream = 2 km/hr. How far can he go in 3 hrs so he can return in 3 hrs?

DS = 10, US = 6 km/hr.
Let distance = d. d/10 + d/6 = 6.
3d/30 + 5d/30 = 6.
8d/30 = 6 → d = 6 × 30/8 = **22.5 km**

---

#### Example 19: Swimmer in River

> A swimmer's speed in still water = 5 km/hr. River flows at 3 km/hr. Time to swim 8 km downstream and return?

DS = 8 km/hr. US = 2 km/hr.
T = 8/8 + 8/2 = 1 + 4 = **5 hours**

---

#### Example 20: Stream Speed From Different Distances

> Downstream: 20 km in 2 hrs. Upstream: 4 km in 2 hrs. Find stream speed.

DS = 10 km/hr. US = 2 km/hr. v = (10−2)/2 = **4 km/hr**

---

#### Example 21: Time Ratio Upstream to Downstream

> A boat goes downstream x km in t₁ hours and upstream x km in t₂ hours. Find ratio of stream speed to boat speed.

DS = x/t₁, US = x/t₂.
v/u = (DS − US)/(DS + US) = (x/t₁ − x/t₂)/(x/t₁ + x/t₂) = (t₂−t₁)/(t₂+t₁)

$$\frac{v}{u} = \frac{t_2 - t_1}{t_2 + t_1}$$

---

## 8. MEETING & CHASING PROBLEMS

---

### Scenario 1: Two People Walk Towards Each Other

$$\text{Meeting time} = \frac{D}{S_A + S_B}$$

$$\text{Meeting point from A} = S_A \times \text{Meeting time}$$

---

### Scenario 2: Two People Walk Same Direction (Chasing)

$$\text{Time for faster to catch slower} = \frac{\text{Head start distance}}{S_{fast} - S_{slow}}$$

---

### Scenario 3: They Start at Different Times

If B starts t hours after A, and A goes at S_A:
- Head start distance = S_A × t
- Time for B (at S_B) to catch A = (S_A × t) / (S_B − S_A)

---

### Examples — Meeting & Chasing:

#### Example 22: Meet on the Way

> A starts from P towards Q (180 km apart) at 40 km/hr. B starts from Q towards P at 50 km/hr at the same time. Where do they meet?

Time to meet = 180/(40+50) = 2 hrs.
Meeting point from P = 40 × 2 = **80 km from P** (or 100 km from Q).

---

#### Example 23: Chasing

> A thief runs at 6 km/hr. A policeman starts 10 minutes later at 8 km/hr. When does the policeman catch the thief?

Head start = 6 × (10/60) = 1 km.
Relative speed = 8 − 6 = 2 km/hr.
Time to catch = 1/2 = **0.5 hours = 30 minutes** after policeman starts.

---

#### Example 24: Multiple Meetings

> A and B run between two points P and Q (100 m). A at 10 m/s, B at 15 m/s. Same direction from P. At what distance from P do they first meet?

Wait — if same direction, A and B start from P. B is faster, so B laps A.

Total lapping distance = 100 m (they meet when relative distance = 100).
Time = 100/(15−10) = 20 s.
B has covered = 15 × 20 = 300 m (3 laps). Meeting point: 300 mod 100 = 0 m from P? Let's verify:
A covers 200 m = 2 laps, at P again. B at 300 m = 3 laps, at P also.

They meet at **P (starting point)** after 20 seconds.

---

#### Example 25: A Starts Late

> A and B are 350 km apart. A starts at 7 AM at 60 km/hr. B starts at 9 AM at 40 km/hr (towards A). When do they meet?

By 9 AM (2 hrs), A has covered 120 km. Remaining gap = 350 − 120 = 230 km.
Now both move towards each other: relative speed = 60 + 40 = 100 km/hr.
Time = 230/100 = 2.3 hrs after 9 AM = **11:18 AM**

## 9. CIRCULAR TRACK PROBLEMS

---

### 🔑 Core Rules:

| Starting from same point, same direction | First meet when faster covers **1 extra lap** |
| Starting from same point, opposite direction | First meet when combined distance = **1 lap** |

---

### Formulas:

**Same direction — first meeting time:**
$$T = \frac{L}{S_1 - S_2} \quad (S_1 > S_2)$$

**Opposite direction — first meeting time:**
$$T = \frac{L}{S_1 + S_2}$$

**Number of times they meet (same direction) in time T:**
$$= \frac{(S_1 - S_2) \times T}{L}$$

**Meet at starting point (same direction):**
$$= \text{LCM of } \left(\frac{L}{S_1}, \frac{L}{S_2}\right) = \text{LCM of individual lap times}$$

---

### Examples — Circular Track:

#### Example 26: First Meeting (Same Direction)

> A and B run on a 400 m circular track. A at 5 m/s, B at 3 m/s. Same direction from same point. When and where do they first meet?

Relative speed = 5 − 3 = 2 m/s.
Time = 400/2 = **200 seconds**.
Distance by A = 5 × 200 = 1000 m = 2.5 laps → 250 m from start.
**They meet 250 m from starting point.**

---

#### Example 27: First Meeting (Opposite Direction)

> A (5 m/s) and B (3 m/s) run in opposite directions on 400 m track. First meeting time?

Relative speed = 5 + 3 = 8 m/s.
Time = 400/8 = **50 seconds**

---

#### Example 28: All Three Meet

> A, B, C run on 600 m circular track. Speeds: 10, 15, 20 m/s. Same direction. When do all first meet?

Individual lap times: A = 60s, B = 40s, C = 30s.
LCM(60, 40, 30) = **120 seconds**

---

#### Example 29: How Many Times They Meet

> A (8 m/s) and B (5 m/s) run on 600 m track (same direction) for 10 minutes. How many times do they meet?

Meetings = (8−5) × 600 / 600 = 3 per meeting cycle... 

Meetings in T seconds = (S_A − S_B) × T / L = 3 × 600 / 600 = **3 meetings** per 600 seconds.
In 10 min = 600 sec: exactly 3 meetings. **3 times**

## 10. CLOCKS — SPEED OF HANDS

---

### 🔑 Clock Basics:

- Clock face = 360°
- 12 hours → hour hand completes 1 full circle = 360°
- Minute hand: 360° in 60 minutes = **6° per minute**
- Hour hand: 360° in 12×60 = 720 minutes = **0.5° per minute**
- Relative speed of minute hand over hour hand = 6 − 0.5 = **5.5° per minute**

---

### Key Formulas:

**Angle between hands at H hours M minutes:**

$$\theta = \left|30H - \frac{11M}{2}\right|$$

(Take the smaller angle if > 180°)

**Time for hands to coincide** (starting from 12):

$$t = \frac{60H}{11} \text{ minutes past } H \text{ o'clock}$$

**In 12 hours, hands coincide:** 11 times
**In 12 hours, hands are opposite (180°):** 11 times
**In 12 hours, hands are perpendicular (90°):** 22 times

---

### Examples — Clocks:

#### Example 30: Angle Between Hands

> Find angle between clock hands at 3:40.

$$\theta = \left|30 \times 3 - \frac{11 \times 40}{2}\right| = |90 - 220| = 130°$$

Angle = **130°**

---

#### Example 31: When Do Hands Coincide?

> Between 4 and 5 o'clock, when are the hands together?

$$t = \frac{60 \times 4}{11} = \frac{240}{11} = 21\frac{9}{11} \text{ min past 4}$$

**Hands coincide at 4:21 9/11**

---

#### Example 32: When Are Hands at 90°?

> Between 2 and 3, when are hands at right angles?

Hands at 90°:
$$30H - \frac{11M}{2} = \pm 90$$

H = 2:
$$60 - \frac{11M}{2} = 90 \Rightarrow M = -\frac{60}{11}$$ (negative, invalid)

$$60 - \frac{11M}{2} = -90 \Rightarrow \frac{11M}{2} = 150 \Rightarrow M = \frac{300}{11} = 27\frac{3}{11}$$

**Hands at 90° at 2:27 3/11**

---

#### Example 33: Gain/Loss of Faulty Clock

> A clock gains 5 minutes every hour. If set correctly at 8 AM, what time does it show at 8 PM?

Duration = 12 hours. Gain = 5 min/hr × 12 = 60 minutes.
Clock shows 8 PM + 60 min = **9 PM**

---

## 11. QUICK TRICKS & SHORTCUTS

---

### 🚀 TRICK 1: km/hr ↔ m/s Conversion

$$\text{km/hr} \times \frac{5}{18} = \text{m/s}$$
$$\text{m/s} \times \frac{18}{5} = \text{km/hr}$$

**Shortcut Check:** 18 km/hr = 5 m/s (memorise this pair!)

---

### 🚀 TRICK 2: Average Speed for Equal Distances

$$\text{Avg Speed} = \frac{2ab}{a+b}$$

Always use this — NEVER average the speeds directly for equal distances!

---

### 🚀 TRICK 3: Relative Speed Summary

Same direction → **Subtract** speeds
Opposite direction → **Add** speeds

---

### 🚀 TRICK 4: Train Crosses Pole vs Platform

- Pole: Distance = Train length only
- Platform: Distance = Train length + Platform length
- Never confuse them!

---

### 🚀 TRICK 5: Boat Speed and Stream Speed

$$u = \frac{D_s + U_s}{2}, \quad v = \frac{D_s - U_s}{2}$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK 6: If Late by T₁ at Speed S₁ and Early by T₂ at Speed S₂:

$$D = \frac{S_1 \times S_2 \times (T_1 + T_2)}{S_2 - S_1}$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK 7: Clock Angle Formula

$$\theta = \left|30H - \frac{11M}{2}\right|$$

If > 180°, take 360° − θ for smaller angle.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 8: Hands Coincide Every

$$\frac{720}{11} = 65\frac{5}{11} \text{ minutes}$$

In 12 hours → 11 times. In 24 hours → 22 times.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 9: Circular Track — First Meet

Same direction: T = Track / (S₁ − S₂)
Opposite: T = Track / (S₁ + S₂)

---

### 🚀 TRICK 10: Meeting at Starting Point — LCM of Lap Times

A's lap time = L/S_A. B's lap time = L/S_B.
Meet at start: **LCM of both lap times**.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 11: If Speed Increases by x%, Time Decreases by:

$$\text{Time decrease} = \frac{x}{100 + x} \times 100\%$$

Example: Speed up 25% → time reduces by 25/125 × 100 = **20%**

---

### 🚀 TRICK 12: If Speed Decreases by x%, Time Increases by:

$$\text{Time increase} = \frac{x}{100 - x} \times 100\%$$

Example: Speed down 20% → time increases by 20/80 × 100 = **25%**

---

### 🚀 TRICK 13: Catching Up — Head Start

Time to catch = Head start distance / Relative speed
= (Speed of A × Time advantage of A) / (Speed of B − Speed of A)

---

### 🚀 TRICK 14: Bird Flying Between Two Trains

Total bird distance = Bird speed × Time until trains meet
Time until trains meet = Gap / (Sum of train speeds)

---

### 🚀 TRICK 15: Escalator / Moving Walkway (Extension of Boat & Stream)

Going WITH escalator = (Person speed + Escalator speed)
Going AGAINST escalator = (Person speed − Escalator speed)
Same formulas as Boat & Stream!

---

## 12. COMMON MISTAKE ALERTS

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 1: Average Speed = (S₁+S₂)/2

**Wrong:** Average speed = (40+60)/2 = 50 km/hr (for equal distances)
**Right:** Average speed = 2×40×60/(40+60) = **48 km/hr**

The harmonic mean is ALWAYS less than arithmetic mean for equal-distance problems.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 2: Not Converting Units

Mixing km/hr and m/s in the same calculation → ALWAYS convert before solving.

**Wrong:** Time = 200m / 72 km/hr
**Right:** 72 km/hr = 20 m/s → Time = 200/20 = 10 s

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 3: Train Problem — Forgetting Own Length

A train crossing a platform must travel: **its own length + platform length**.
Many students use only the platform length.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 4: Relative Speed Direction Error

**Wrong:** Two trains approaching → relative speed = S₁ − S₂
**Right:** Approaching (opposite) → relative speed = **S₁ + S₂**

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 5: Boat — Confusing u and v

u = boat's OWN speed (in still water)
v = stream speed
DS = u+v, US = u−v

**Wrong:** US = v − u
**Right:** US = u − v (always ≥ 0, else boat can't go upstream)

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 6: Circular Track — Same vs Opposite

Same direction → Subtract speeds for relative speed.
Opposite → Add speeds.

Students often ADD when they should SUBTRACT for same direction.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 7: Clock — Angle > 180°

If formula gives 200°, the actual angle is 360° − 200° = **160°** (take the smaller one unless asked for reflex angle).

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 8: Meeting Point vs Meeting Time

Meeting TIME uses relative speed.
Meeting POINT requires finding distance each person travels = individual speed × meeting time.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 9: Late/Early Speed Problem

"Arrives 30 min late at speed S₁ and 20 min early at S₂" → the time difference is 30+20=50 min.
**Don't** subtract (30−20=10). The total buffer = late + early = **sum**.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 10: LCM for Circular Track

To find when ALL people meet at start → LCM of **individual lap TIMES** (not speeds).

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

## 🏆 TOP EXAM TIPS

1. **Convert units first** — never mix km/hr and m/s in one equation.
2. **Average speed for equal distances = 2ab/(a+b)** — write this on your answer sheet before starting.
3. **Train crosses platform** — distance = train length + platform length (NOT just platform).
4. **Relative speed:** Opposite = add, Same = subtract. Memorise with a visual.
5. **Boat problems:** Always find DS and US first; then use the two formulas.
6. **Circular track:** First meeting ≠ meeting at starting point. First meet uses relative speed; start meeting uses LCM.
7. **Clock formula** |30H − 11M/2| — practice this for 5 standard times before your exam.
8. **Late/Early problems:** Total time difference = late + early (NOT late − early).
9. **Chasing problems:** Time to catch = Head start / Relative speed.
10. **Average speed** — when in doubt, calculate Total Distance / Total Time directly.
