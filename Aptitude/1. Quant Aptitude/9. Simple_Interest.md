## 1. CORE DEFINITIONS & TERMINOLOGY

---

### 💰 Principal (P)
The **original sum of money** borrowed or invested.

> Also called: **Capital** or **Sum**

---

### 📈 Interest (I)
The **extra money** paid by the borrower to the lender for using the money.

> In Simple Interest, interest is calculated on the **original principal only** — it never changes.

---

### 🕐 Time (T)
The **duration** for which the money is borrowed/invested.

> Units: Years, Months, Days — must always be converted to **years** in the formula.

---

### 📊 Rate of Interest (R)
The **percentage of interest** charged per year on the principal.

> Written as: R% per annum (p.a.) means per year.

---

### 🏦 Amount (A)
The **total money** returned at the end = Principal + Interest

$$A = P + SI$$

---

### 🔑 Key Difference: Simple vs Compound Interest

| Feature | Simple Interest | Compound Interest |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| Interest calculated on | Original Principal only | Principal + accumulated interest |
| Interest changes each year? | NO — stays same | YES — grows each year |
| Formula | SI = PRT/100 | CI = P(1+R/100)ⁿ − P |

---

## 2. FUNDAMENTAL FORMULAS

---

### The One Formula You MUST Know:

$$\boxed{SI = \frac{P \times R \times T}{100}}$$

---

### Derived Formulas (All from the same base):

$$P = \frac{SI \times 100}{R \times T}$$

$$R = \frac{SI \times 100}{P \times T}$$

$$T = \frac{SI \times 100}{P \times R}$$

$$A = P + SI = P\left(1 + \frac{RT}{100}\right)$$

$$P = \frac{A \times 100}{100 + RT}$$

---

## 3. THE FORMULA TRIANGLE

> 🔑 Use this visual to instantly find any variable!

```
         ┌─────────────┐
         │  SI × 100   │
         └──────┬──────┘
                │
        ┌───────┴───────┐
        │               │
     ┌──┴──┐         ┌──┴──┐
     │  P  │    ×    │ R×T │
     └─────┘         └─────┘
```

**How to use:**
- Cover what you want to find
- What remains tells you what to do (multiply or divide)

| Want to Find | Cover It | Formula |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| SI | Cover SI | P × R × T / 100 |
| P | Cover P | SI × 100 / (R × T) |
| R | Cover R | SI × 100 / (P × T) |
| T | Cover T | SI × 100 / (P × R) |

## 4. BASIC TO ADVANCED EXAMPLES

---

### ─── BASIC LEVEL ───

#### Example 1: Find Simple Interest

> Find SI on ₹5000 at 8% per annum for 3 years.

**Solution:**
$$SI = \frac{5000 \times 8 \times 3}{100} = \frac{120000}{100} = ₹1200$$

---

#### Example 2: Find Amount

> Find the Amount when ₹8000 is invested at 10% p.a. for 4 years.

**Solution:**
$$SI = \frac{8000 \times 10 \times 4}{100} = ₹3200$$
$$A = 8000 + 3200 = ₹11200$$

**OR direct:**
$$A = 8000 \times \left(1 + \frac{10 \times 4}{100}\right) = 8000 \times 1.40 = ₹11200$$

---

#### Example 3: Find Principal

> SI = ₹960, Rate = 8% p.a., Time = 4 years. Find Principal.

**Solution:**
$$P = \frac{SI \times 100}{R \times T} = \frac{960 \times 100}{8 \times 4} = \frac{96000}{32} = ₹3000$$

---

#### Example 4: Find Rate

> SI = ₹450, P = ₹2500, T = 3 years. Find Rate.

**Solution:**
$$R = \frac{SI \times 100}{P \times T} = \frac{450 \times 100}{2500 \times 3} = \frac{45000}{7500} = 6\%$$

---

#### Example 5: Find Time

> SI = ₹720, P = ₹4000, R = 9% p.a. Find Time.

**Solution:**
$$T = \frac{SI \times 100}{P \times R} = \frac{720 \times 100}{4000 \times 9} = \frac{72000}{36000} = 2 \text{ years}$$

---

#### Example 6: Amount Given, Find Principal

> Amount after 3 years at 5% p.a. = ₹5750. Find Principal.

**Solution:**
$$A = P\left(1 + \frac{RT}{100}\right)$$
$$5750 = P\left(1 + \frac{5 \times 3}{100}\right) = P \times \frac{115}{100}$$
$$P = \frac{5750 \times 100}{115} = ₹5000$$

---

### ─── INTERMEDIATE LEVEL ───

#### Example 7: SI on Different Principals, Same Total SI

> A sum of money doubles itself in 8 years at SI. Find the rate of interest.

**Solution:**

Let P = 100. Amount = 200 (doubles). So SI = 100.

$$R = \frac{SI \times 100}{P \times T} = \frac{100 \times 100}{100 \times 8} = 12.5\%$$

> 🔥 **TRICK:** If money doubles in T years → R = 100/T

---

#### Example 8: Money Triples Itself

> At what rate will a sum triple itself in 20 years under SI?

**Solution:**

P = 100, A = 300, SI = 200.

$$R = \frac{200 \times 100}{100 \times 20} = 10\%$$

> 🔥 **TRICK:** If money becomes n times in T years → R = (n−1) × 100 / T

---

#### Example 9: In How Many Years Will a Sum Double?

> At 12.5% p.a. SI, in how many years will ₹3000 become ₹6000?

**Solution:**

SI = 6000 − 3000 = 3000.

$$T = \frac{3000 \times 100}{3000 \times 12.5} = \frac{100}{12.5} = 8 \text{ years}$$

> 🔥 **TRICK:** Time to double = 100/R

---

#### Example 10: Two Parts of a Sum

> A sum of ₹12,000 is split into two parts. One part is invested at 10% and the other at 15% p.a. Total SI after 2 years = ₹3300. Find each part.

**Solution:**

Let part at 10% = x. Then part at 15% = 12000 − x.

$$\frac{x \times 10 \times 2}{100} + \frac{(12000-x) \times 15 \times 2}{100} = 3300$$

$$0.20x + 0.30(12000-x) = 3300$$

$$0.20x + 3600 - 0.30x = 3300$$

$$-0.10x = -300 \Rightarrow x = 3000$$

**Parts: ₹3000 at 10% and ₹9000 at 15%**

---

#### Example 11: Different Time Periods

> ₹6000 is lent in two parts — one for 3 years at 5% and other for 5 years at 7%. Total interest = ₹2010. Find each part.

**Solution:**

Let first part = x. Second = 6000 − x.

$$\frac{x \times 5 \times 3}{100} + \frac{(6000-x) \times 7 \times 5}{100} = 2010$$

$$\frac{15x}{100} + \frac{35(6000-x)}{100} = 2010$$

$$15x + 210000 - 35x = 201000$$

$$-20x = -9000 \Rightarrow x = 450$$

**Parts: ₹450 at 5% for 3 years and ₹5550 at 7% for 5 years**

---

#### Example 12: Rate Changes Over Time

> ₹5000 is invested at 4% for first 2 years, 6% for next 3 years, and 8% for next 5 years. Find total SI.

**Solution:**

$$SI_1 = \frac{5000 \times 4 \times 2}{100} = ₹400$$

$$SI_2 = \frac{5000 \times 6 \times 3}{100} = ₹900$$

$$SI_3 = \frac{5000 \times 8 \times 5}{100} = ₹2000$$

$$\text{Total SI} = 400 + 900 + 2000 = ₹3300$$

---

#### Example 13: Finding Original Sum from Two Conditions

> A sum amounts to ₹3720 in 3 years and ₹4200 in 6 years at SI. Find the principal and rate.

**Solution:**

SI for 3 years (from year 3 to year 6) = 4200 − 3720 = **₹480**

Since SI is uniform each year:
SI per year = 480/3 = ₹160
SI for 3 years = ₹480

So: P + 480 = 3720 → **P = ₹3240**

$$R = \frac{480 \times 100}{3240 \times 3} = \frac{48000}{9720} = 4.94\% \approx 4\frac{17}{27}\%$$

Let me verify: SI = 3240 × R × 3/100 = 480 → R = 48000/9720 = 400/81 ≈ 4.94%

> **KEY INSIGHT:** In SI, the increase in amount is EQUAL every year. So difference of amounts for equal time = SI for that period.

---

#### Example 14: The "Extra Interest" Problem

> A person lends ₹5000 to A at 4% and ₹3000 to B. If he gets equal interest from both after 1 year, find the rate charged to B.

**Solution:**

SI from A = (5000 × 4 × 1)/100 = ₹200

For B: (3000 × R × 1)/100 = 200

$$R = \frac{200 \times 100}{3000} = 6.67\% \approx 6\frac{2}{3}\%$$

---

### ─── ADVANCED LEVEL ───

#### Example 15: Sum Lent at Two Different Rates — Difference in Interest

> A sum of money lent at SI becomes ₹2900 in 3 years and ₹3500 in 7 years. Find P and R.

**Solution:**

SI from year 3 to year 7 = 3500 − 2900 = ₹600 (for 4 years)
SI per year = 600/4 = ₹150
SI for 3 years = 150 × 3 = ₹450

P = 2900 − 450 = **₹2450**

$$R = \frac{150 \times 100}{2450} = \frac{15000}{2450} = \frac{300}{49} \approx 6.12\%$$

---

#### Example 16: Instalment Problem

> A person borrows ₹5000 and agrees to repay in 2 equal annual instalments at 4% SI. Find the instalment amount.

**Solution:**

Let instalment = x.

Present value of 1st instalment (paid after 1 year):
$$PV_1 = \frac{x}{1 + \frac{4 \times 1}{100}} = \frac{x}{1.04} = \frac{100x}{104}$$

Present value of 2nd instalment (paid after 2 years):
$$PV_2 = \frac{x}{1 + \frac{4 \times 2}{100}} = \frac{x}{1.08} = \frac{100x}{108}$$

Sum of PVs = Principal:
$$\frac{100x}{104} + \frac{100x}{108} = 5000$$

$$100x\left(\frac{1}{104} + \frac{1}{108}\right) = 5000$$

$$100x \times \frac{108 + 104}{104 \times 108} = 5000$$

$$100x \times \frac{212}{11232} = 5000$$

$$x = \frac{5000 \times 11232}{100 \times 212} = \frac{56160000}{21200} = ₹2650.94 \approx ₹2651$$

---

#### Example 17: Ratio Problem

> The ratio of SI on a certain sum for 5 years at 4% to that for 4 years at 5% is 1:1. Is this possible?

**Solution:**

$$\frac{P \times 4 \times 5}{100} : \frac{P \times 5 \times 4}{100} = 20P : 20P = 1:1 \checkmark$$

> **Insight:** RT product being the same → SI is always equal regardless of how R and T are distributed!

---

#### Example 18: Complex — SI Earned and Reinvested

> ₹4000 is invested for 3 years at 5% SI. The interest earned is then invested again for 4 years at 8% SI. Find total money accumulated.

**Solution:**

$$SI_1 = \frac{4000 \times 5 \times 3}{100} = ₹600$$

This ₹600 is now the principal for 4 years at 8%:
$$SI_2 = \frac{600 \times 8 \times 4}{100} = ₹192$$

Total = Original Principal + SI₁ + SI₂ = 4000 + 600 + 192 = **₹4792**

---

#### Example 19: Debt Repayment with SI

> A owes B ₹2000 due in 2 years and ₹3000 due in 5 years. If B agrees to settle now at 8% SI, how much should A pay now?

**Solution:**

Present value of ₹2000 due in 2 years:
$$PV_1 = \frac{2000}{1 + \frac{8 \times 2}{100}} = \frac{2000}{1.16} = ₹1724.14$$

Present value of ₹3000 due in 5 years:
$$PV_2 = \frac{3000}{1 + \frac{8 \times 5}{100}} = \frac{3000}{1.40} = ₹2142.86$$

**Total to pay now = ₹1724.14 + ₹2142.86 = ₹3867**

---

#### Example 20: Finding N when Amount Ratios are Given

> The SI on a sum for 3 years at R% equals the SI on the same sum for 5 years at (R−2)%. Find R.

**Solution:**

$$P \times R \times 3 = P \times (R-2) \times 5$$

$$3R = 5R - 10$$

$$2R = 10 \Rightarrow R = 5\%$$

## 5. TIME CONVERSIONS & UNIT HANDLING

---

### ⚠️ CRITICAL RULE: Time must always be in YEARS in the SI formula.

---

### Conversion Table:

| Given | Convert to Years |
|-------|----------------|
| Months | Months ÷ 12 |
| Days | Days ÷ 365 |
| Weeks | Weeks ÷ 52 |
| 6 months | 1/2 year |
| 4 months | 1/3 year |
| 3 months | 1/4 year |
| 2 months | 1/6 year |
| 146 days | 146/365 = 2/5 year |
| 73 days | 73/365 = 1/5 year |
| 36.5 days | 1/10 year |

---

#### Example 21: Time in Months

> Find SI on ₹3600 at 5% p.a. for 8 months.

**Solution:**

T = 8/12 = 2/3 years

$$SI = \frac{3600 \times 5 \times \frac{2}{3}}{100} = \frac{3600 \times 10}{300} = ₹120$$

---

#### Example 22: Time in Days

> Find SI on ₹7300 at 10% p.a. for 73 days.

**Solution:**

T = 73/365 = 1/5 year

$$SI = \frac{7300 \times 10 \times \frac{1}{5}}{100} = \frac{7300 \times 2}{100} = ₹146$$

---

#### Example 23: Mixed Units

> SI on ₹4800 at 6% p.a. for 2 years 4 months.

**Solution:**

T = 2 + 4/12 = 2 + 1/3 = 7/3 years

$$SI = \frac{4800 \times 6 \times \frac{7}{3}}{100} = \frac{4800 \times 14}{100} = ₹672$$

## 6. RATE OF INTEREST VARIATIONS

---

### Rate per annum vs per month vs per quarter:

| Given Rate | Per Year Equivalent |
|-----------|-------------------|
| R% per month | 12R% per annum |
| R% per quarter | 4R% per annum |
| R% per half-year | 2R% per annum |

---

#### Example 24: Monthly Rate

> Find SI on ₹5000 at 2% per month for 6 months.

**Solution:**

Use R = 2% and T = 6 months directly (since rate is per month):
$$SI = \frac{5000 \times 2 \times 6}{100} = ₹600$$

OR convert: R annually = 24%, T = 6/12 = 1/2 year:
$$SI = \frac{5000 \times 24 \times 0.5}{100} = ₹600 \checkmark$$

## 7. MULTIPLE PRINCIPALS / SPLIT INVESTMENTS

---

### Pattern 1: Same rate, same time, different principals

$$\text{Total SI} = \frac{(P_1 + P_2 + \ldots) \times R \times T}{100}$$

---

### Pattern 2: Different rates, different times, same principal

$$\text{Total SI} = P \times \frac{(R_1T_1 + R_2T_2 + \ldots)}{100}$$

---

### Pattern 3: Split sum to get equal interest

> If sum S is split into x and (S−x):

$$\frac{x \times R_1 \times T_1}{100} = \frac{(S-x) \times R_2 \times T_2}{100}$$

$$x \times R_1T_1 = (S-x) \times R_2T_2$$

$$\frac{x}{S-x} = \frac{R_2T_2}{R_1T_1}$$

---

#### Example 25: Equal Interest Condition

> ₹10,000 is divided into two parts. First at 5% for 4 years, second at 8% for 2 years. If SI from both are equal, find the parts.

**Solution:**

$$\frac{x}{10000-x} = \frac{8 \times 2}{5 \times 4} = \frac{16}{20} = \frac{4}{5}$$

$$5x = 4(10000-x) = 40000 - 4x$$

$$9x = 40000 \Rightarrow x = ₹4444.44$$

Parts: **₹4444.44 and ₹5555.56**

## 8. SI WITH INSTALMENTS

---

### Formula for Equal Annual Instalments:

If a loan of P is to be repaid in n equal annual instalments at R% SI:

$$\text{Instalment} = \frac{P \times 100}{n \times 100 + R \times \frac{n(n-1)}{2}}$$

> This formula comes from the present value approach. Memorise it for exams!

---

#### Example 26: 3 Equal Annual Instalments

> A man borrows ₹1820 and promises to repay in 3 equal annual instalments at 20% SI. Find each instalment.

**Solution:**

$$\text{Instalment} = \frac{1820 \times 100}{3 \times 100 + 20 \times \frac{3 \times 2}{2}} = \frac{182000}{300 + 60} = \frac{182000}{360} = ₹505.56$$

Let's verify using the direct method:
Let instalment = x.

After 1 year, amount owed with SI:
1820 × (1 + 20/100) = 1820 × 1.20 = 2184

Pay x → Balance = 2184 − x

After year 2: (2184 − x) × 1.20 = 2620.8 − 1.2x
Pay x → Balance = 2620.8 − 2.2x

After year 3: (2620.8 − 2.2x) × 1.20 = 3144.96 − 2.64x
Pay x → Balance = 0:

3144.96 − 2.64x − x = 0 → 3144.96 = 3.64x → x ≈ ₹864

> ⚠️ The two methods differ because the formula uses SI (not compound). Always re-read whether it's the **amount method** or **SI-on-original** approach.

**Using SI method (pure SI on original loan):**

Year 1 interest on 1820 = 1820 × 20/100 × 1 = 364
Year 2 interest = 364
Year 3 interest = 364
Total interest = 1092
Total to repay = 1820 + 1092 = 2912
Each instalment = 2912/3 ≈ **₹970.67**

---

#### Example 27: Exam-Style Instalment

> A borrowed ₹6000 from B at 10% SI per year. He repays ₹2000 at the end of each year. After 3 such payments, find the amount still owed.

**Solution:**

**Year 1:**
- Opening balance = ₹6000
- Interest = 6000 × 10% = ₹600
- Total = ₹6600
- Payment = ₹2000
- Balance = ₹4600

**Year 2:**
- Opening balance = ₹4600
- Interest = 4600 × 10% = ₹460
- Total = ₹5060
- Payment = ₹2000
- Balance = ₹3060

**Year 3:**
- Opening balance = ₹3060
- Interest = 3060 × 10% = ₹306
- Total = ₹3366
- Payment = ₹2000
- **Balance = ₹1366**

## 9. SI VS CI COMPARISON BASICS

---

> This section helps you relate SI with CI (often asked together).

### For the SAME P, R, T:

| Period | SI | CI |
|--------|----|----|
| 1 year | Same | Same |
| 2 years | SI = 2 × yearly SI | CI > SI |
| n years | SI = n × yearly SI | CI > SI always |

### Difference for 2 Years:

$$CI - SI = P \times \left(\frac{R}{100}\right)^2$$

### Difference for 3 Years:

$$CI - SI = P \times \left(\frac{R}{100}\right)^2 \times \left(3 + \frac{R}{100}\right)$$

---

#### Example 28: CI − SI Difference

> P = ₹10,000, R = 10%, T = 2 years. Find CI − SI.

$$CI - SI = 10000 \times \left(\frac{10}{100}\right)^2 = 10000 \times 0.01 = ₹100$$

**Verify:**
- SI = 10000 × 10 × 2/100 = ₹2000
- CI = 10000 × (1.10)² − 10000 = 12100 − 10000 = ₹2100
- Difference = ₹100 ✓

## 10. QUICK TRICKS & SHORTCUTS

---

### 🚀 TRICK 1: Doubling Time

$$T_{\text{double}} = \frac{100}{R}$$

Example: R = 8% → T = 100/8 = **12.5 years**

---

### 🚀 TRICK 2: Tripling Time

$$T_{\text{triple}} = \frac{200}{R}$$

Example: R = 10% → T = 200/10 = **20 years**

---

### 🚀 TRICK 3: Becoming n Times

$$T = \frac{(n-1) \times 100}{R}$$

OR

$$R = \frac{(n-1) \times 100}{T}$$

Example: Money becomes 5 times in 8 years → R = 4×100/8 = **50%**

---

### 🚀 TRICK 4: Equal R×T Product

If R₁ × T₁ = R₂ × T₂ → **SI is always equal** for the same principal.

Example: 5% for 4 years = 4% for 5 years (both give 20% of P as SI)

---

### 🚀 TRICK 5: Sum → Amount Ratio

$$\frac{A}{P} = 1 + \frac{RT}{100}$$

If you know A/P ratio, you can find RT directly.

Example: A = 1.6P → RT = 60 → if T = 4, then R = 15%

---

### 🚀 TRICK 6: Two Amounts at Different Times — Find P

$$P = \frac{A_2 T_1 - A_1 T_2}{T_1 - T_2}$$

Where A₁ is amount at time T₁ and A₂ is at T₂.

Wait — correct form:

$$\text{SI per year} = \frac{A_2 - A_1}{T_2 - T_1}$$

$$P = A_1 - SI_{\text{per year}} \times T_1$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK 7: Split Sum for Equal Interest

$$\frac{P_1}{P_2} = \frac{R_2 \times T_2}{R_1 \times T_1}$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK 8: Annual SI as Percentage of P

$$\text{SI per year} = \frac{R}{100} \times P$$

So if R = 12%, every year 12% of P is earned as interest — simple!

---

### 🚀 TRICK 9: Interest Rate Comparison

If someone says "interest doubles the principal in 10 years", that means:
SI = P → PRT/100 = P → RT = 100 → R = 10%

---

### 🚀 TRICK 10: "Find SP to pay at simple interest" — convert to SI

Any deferred payment problem converts to:
Present Value × (1 + RT/100) = Future Value

---

### 🚀 TRICK 11: Shortcut for SI When P, R, T Are All Multiples of 100

$$SI = \frac{P}{100} \times R \times T$$

Break P/100 first, then multiply.

Example: P = 4500, R = 6, T = 3
→ 45 × 6 × 3 = 45 × 18 = **₹810**

---

### 🚀 TRICK 12: Finding R When T = R (Rate = Time)

$$SI = \frac{P \times R^2}{100}$$

Example: P = ₹6000, R = T = 5:
SI = 6000 × 25/100 = **₹1500**

---

## 11. COMMON MISTAKE ALERTS

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 1: Using Months/Days Directly as Years

**Wrong:** P = 5000, R = 6%, T = 6 months → SI = 5000×6×6/100 = ₹1800
**Right:** T = 6/12 = 0.5 years → SI = 5000×6×0.5/100 = **₹150**

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 2: Confusing Amount with Interest

**Wrong:** "SI = ₹6000 and amount = ₹6000" (taking both same)
**Right:** Amount = P + SI. If A = ₹6000 and P = ₹4000 → SI = ₹2000

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 3: Applying Rate Directly Without /100

**Wrong:** SI = P × R × T (forgetting /100)
**Right:** SI = P × R × T / 100

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 4: Rate Per Month Not Converted

If rate is 2% per month and time is 1 year:
**Wrong:** SI = P × 2 × 1/100
**Right:** SI = P × 2 × 12/100 (since 1 year = 12 months) OR use T = 12 months

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 5: Instalment Direction Error

In instalment problems, be clear whether:
- SI is charged on the **remaining balance** each year (practical method), OR
- SI is charged on the **original loan** throughout (pure SI method)

These give DIFFERENT instalment amounts. Read the question carefully.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 6: Equal Interest ≠ Equal Principal

Just because two investments earn equal interest does NOT mean the principals are equal.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 7: Treating SI Like CI

In SI, interest earned in year 2 ≠ interest earned in year 1 + something.
In SI: **Year 1 interest = Year 2 interest = Year n interest** (always same).

## 13. SUMMARY CHEAT SHEET

---

```
CORE FORMULA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SI  = (P × R × T) / 100
P   = (SI × 100) / (R × T)
R   = (SI × 100) / (P × T)
T   = (SI × 100) / (P × R)
A   = P + SI = P × (1 + RT/100)

SPECIAL CASES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Sum doubles    → T = 100/R  or  R = 100/T
Sum triples    → T = 200/R  or  R = 200/T
Sum n-times    → T = (n−1)×100/R  or  R = (n−1)×100/T
R×T = constant → SI is same regardless of how R, T split

TIME UNITS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Months → divide by 12
Days   → divide by 365
Always convert to YEARS before using formula

CI − SI DIFFERENCE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
2 years: CI − SI = P(R/100)²
3 years: CI − SI = P(R/100)²(3 + R/100)

SPLIT SUM (Equal Interest)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
P₁/P₂ = (R₂ × T₂) / (R₁ × T₁)

TWO AMOUNTS AT DIFFERENT TIMES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
SI/year = (A₂ − A₁) / (T₂ − T₁)
P = A₁ − (SI/year) × T₁
```

### Memorise These Key Values:

| Condition | Formula |
|-----------|---------|
| Money doubles | R = 100/T |
| Money triples | R = 200/T |
| Money becomes n times | R = (n−1)×100/T |
| SI = P (interest = principal) | RT = 100 |
| SI per year | P × R/100 |

## 🏆 TOP EXAM TIPS

1. **Always convert time to years** — months ÷ 12, days ÷ 365.
2. **SI is always the same every year** — use this to solve two-amount problems.
3. **"Doubles in T years" → R = 100/T** — memorise this forever.
4. **Split sum problems** → set up a single equation with one variable.
5. **Instalment problems** → track balance year by year if formula confuses you.
6. **If R × T is equal for two situations, SI is always equal** (for same P).
7. **Amount − Principal = Interest** — never confuse Amount with Interest.
8. **Rate per month × 12 = Rate per year** — always convert to annual.
9. **In two-condition problems** (A₁ at T₁ and A₂ at T₂), SI per year = (A₂−A₁)/(T₂−T₁).
10. **For equal interest from two parts**, use ratio: P₁/P₂ = R₂T₂/R₁T₁.
