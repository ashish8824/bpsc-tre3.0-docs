# PART A — TRAINS

## 1. CORE CONCEPT OF TRAIN PROBLEMS

---

### 🔑 The Big Idea:

> A train problem is a **distance-speed-time** problem where the **LENGTH of the train matters**.

In a regular speed problem, a point (person/car) has no length.
In a **train problem**, the train has LENGTH — and it must FULLY CLEAR the object before the crossing is complete.

---

### What Does "Crossing" Mean?

```
BEFORE CROSSING:
[===TRAIN===]          [POLE]

CROSSING STARTS when front of train reaches pole.
CROSSING ENDS when REAR of train passes the pole.

Distance covered = LENGTH OF TRAIN
```

```
BEFORE CROSSING:
[===TRAIN===]          [====PLATFORM====]

CROSSING STARTS when front of train reaches start of platform.
CROSSING ENDS when REAR of train leaves the end of platform.

Distance covered = LENGTH OF TRAIN + LENGTH OF PLATFORM
```

---

### The ONE Rule That Explains Everything:

$$\boxed{\text{Distance covered during crossing} = \text{Sum of lengths of all objects being crossed}}$$

| Object Being Crossed | What Has Length? | Distance = |
|---------------------|-----------------|------------|
| Pole / Signal / Person standing | Only Train | L_train |
| Platform / Bridge / Tunnel | Train + Platform | L_train + L_platform |
| Another Train (any direction) | Both Trains | L_train1 + L_train2 |

---

### Unit Conversion — MUST Know:

$$\boxed{1 \text{ km/hr} = \frac{5}{18} \text{ m/s}}$$
$$\boxed{1 \text{ m/s} = \frac{18}{5} \text{ km/hr} = 3.6 \text{ km/hr}}$$

| km/hr | m/s | | km/hr | m/s |
|-------|-----|-|-------|-----|
| 18 | 5 | | 54 | 15 |
| 36 | 10 | | 72 | 20 |
| 90 | 25 | | 108 | 30 |

> **Always convert speed to m/s when length is in metres!**

## 2. THE 4 GOLDEN SCENARIOS

---

### SCENARIO 1 — Train crosses a Stationary Point (Pole/Person/Signal)

```
     ←—— L_train ——→
[==================]  →→→  | POLE |

Distance = L_train
Speed = S_train (convert to m/s)
Time = L_train / S_train
```

$$\boxed{T = \frac{L_{train}}{S_{train}}}$$

---

### SCENARIO 2 — Train crosses a Stationary Length (Platform/Bridge/Tunnel)

```
     ←—— L_train ——→          ←—— L_platform ——→
[==================]  →→→  [========================]

Distance = L_train + L_platform
```

$$\boxed{T = \frac{L_{train} + L_{platform}}{S_{train}}}$$

---

### SCENARIO 3 — Two Trains, OPPOSITE Directions

```
[===Train A===] →→→          ←←← [===Train B===]

They move TOWARDS each other.
Relative Speed = S_A + S_B
Distance = L_A + L_B
```

$$\boxed{T = \frac{L_A + L_B}{S_A + S_B}}$$

---

### SCENARIO 4 — Two Trains, SAME Direction (Overtaking)

```
[===Train B (slow)===] →→→
                              [===Train A (fast)===] →→→→

Train A chases and overtakes Train B.
Relative Speed = S_A - S_B  (A is faster)
Distance = L_A + L_B
```

$$\boxed{T = \frac{L_A + L_B}{S_A - S_B}}$$

---

### SCENARIO 5 — Train crosses a MOVING person/object

```
Same direction:    Relative Speed = S_train - S_person
Opposite direction: Relative Speed = S_train + S_person
Distance = L_train only (person has no length)
```

$$\boxed{T = \frac{L_{train}}{S_{train} \pm S_{person}}}$$

Use **+** when opposite, **−** when same direction.

## 3. ALL TRAIN FORMULAS

---

### Master Formula Table:

| Situation | Distance | Speed Used | Formula |
|-----------|----------|-----------|---------|
| Cross a pole | L | S_train | T = L/S |
| Cross a platform | L + P | S_train | T = (L+P)/S |
| Two trains (opposite) | L₁+L₂ | S₁+S₂ | T = (L₁+L₂)/(S₁+S₂) |
| Two trains (same dir) | L₁+L₂ | \|S₁−S₂\| | T = (L₁+L₂)/\|S₁−S₂\| |
| Cross moving person (opp) | L | S+s | T = L/(S+s) |
| Cross moving person (same) | L | S−s | T = L/(S−s) |

---

### Derived Formulas (Finding Unknown):

**Find Length of train when time and speed are known:**
$$L_{train} = S_{train} \times T_{pole}$$

**Find Platform length:**
$$L_{platform} = (S_{train} \times T_{platform}) - L_{train}$$

**Find Train Speed from two crossings:**
$$S = \frac{L_{platform} - 0}{T_{platform} - T_{pole}} \quad \text{... (not direct, use system of equations)}$$

Better: From crossing a pole (time = t₁) and platform of length P (time = t₂):
$$L_{train} = \frac{P \times t_1}{t_2 - t_1}$$
$$S = \frac{L_{train}}{t_1}$$

## 4. TRAIN EXAMPLES — BASIC TO ADVANCED

---

### ─── BASIC LEVEL ───

#### Example 1: Train Crosses a Pole

> A train 360 m long runs at 90 km/hr. Time to cross a telegraph pole?

**Step 1:** Convert speed → 90 × 5/18 = **25 m/s**
**Step 2:** Distance = 360 m (pole has no length)
**Step 3:** T = 360/25 = **14.4 seconds**

---

#### Example 2: Train Crosses a Platform

> A train 250 m long travels at 72 km/hr and crosses a platform in 25 seconds. Find platform length.

Speed = 72 × 5/18 = **20 m/s**
Distance = 20 × 25 = 500 m
Platform = 500 − 250 = **250 m**

---

#### Example 3: Find Speed from Two Crossings

> A train crosses a pole in 10 sec and a 200 m platform in 18 sec. Find speed and length.

Let length = L, speed = S m/s.

From pole: L = 10S ... (i)
From platform: L + 200 = 18S ... (ii)

(ii) − (i): 200 = 8S → **S = 25 m/s = 90 km/hr**
L = 10 × 25 = **250 m**

---

#### Example 4: Two Trains — Opposite Direction

> Train A (200 m) at 60 km/hr and Train B (300 m) at 90 km/hr approach each other. Time to cross?

Relative speed = 60 + 90 = 150 km/hr = 150 × 5/18 = **125/3 m/s**
Total distance = 200 + 300 = 500 m
Time = 500 ÷ (125/3) = 500 × 3/125 = **12 seconds**

---

#### Example 5: Two Trains — Same Direction

> Train A (150 m) at 80 km/hr overtakes Train B (100 m) at 50 km/hr. Time to pass?

Relative speed = 80 − 50 = 30 km/hr = 30 × 5/18 = **25/3 m/s**
Total distance = 150 + 100 = 250 m
Time = 250 ÷ (25/3) = 250 × 3/25 = **30 seconds**

---

#### Example 6: Train Passes a Running Man (Same Direction)

> A train 300 m long passes a man running at 6 km/hr in the same direction in 30 seconds. Find train speed.

Relative speed = 300/30 = 10 m/s = 10 × 18/5 = **36 km/hr**
Train speed = 36 + 6 = **42 km/hr** (same direction, so add man's speed back)

---

#### Example 7: Train Passes a Running Man (Opposite Direction)

> A train 200 m long passes a man walking at 4 km/hr in the opposite direction in 16 seconds. Find train speed.

Relative speed = 200/16 = 12.5 m/s = 12.5 × 18/5 = **45 km/hr**
Train speed = 45 − 4 = **41 km/hr**
(Opposite direction: relative = S_train + S_man → S_train = relative − S_man)

---

### ─── INTERMEDIATE LEVEL ───

#### Example 8: Find Train Length and Speed Together

> A train takes 15 sec to pass a platform 100 m long and 10 sec to pass a platform 50 m long. Find train's length and speed.

Let length = L, speed = S m/s.

$$\frac{L + 100}{S} = 15 \quad \Rightarrow \quad L + 100 = 15S \quad ...(i)$$
$$\frac{L + 50}{S} = 10 \quad \Rightarrow \quad L + 50 = 10S \quad ...(ii)$$

(i) − (ii): 50 = 5S → **S = 10 m/s = 36 km/hr**
L = 10×10 − 50 = **50 m**

---

#### Example 9: Time for Train to Cross Bridge with Speed in Both Units

> A bridge is 800 m long. A train crosses it in 1 minute at 60 km/hr. Find the train's length.

Speed = 60 × 5/18 = 50/3 m/s
Distance in 60 sec = (50/3) × 60 = **1000 m**
Train length = 1000 − 800 = **200 m**

---

#### Example 10: Two Trains — When Will They Meet After Passing?

> Train A (speed 80 km/hr) and Train B (speed 60 km/hr) start from cities P and Q (280 km apart) towards each other. After how long do they meet and what distance has each covered?

Relative speed = 80 + 60 = 140 km/hr
Time = 280/140 = **2 hours**
A covers = 80 × 2 = **160 km** from P
B covers = 60 × 2 = **120 km** from Q

---

#### Example 11: Faster Train — Overtake Time Including Both Lengths

> Train A is 400 m and travels at 100 km/hr. Train B is 300 m at 80 km/hr. A overtakes B. How long from when A's engine touches B's rear to when A's rear clears B's engine?

This is standard same-direction crossing.
Relative speed = 100 − 80 = 20 km/hr = 20 × 5/18 = 50/9 m/s
Total distance = 400 + 300 = 700 m
Time = 700 ÷ (50/9) = 700 × 9/50 = **126 seconds**

---

#### Example 12: Train Crosses Two Platforms Consecutively

> Train (length 300 m, speed 90 km/hr) crosses Platform A (200 m) and Platform B (400 m) placed end-to-end. Find total crossing time.

Speed = 25 m/s.
Total platform = 200 + 400 = 600 m (treated as one long platform).
Distance = 300 + 600 = 900 m.
Time = 900/25 = **36 seconds**

---

#### Example 13: Find Missing Speed When Time is Same

> Two trains of lengths 200 m and 300 m travel at speeds such that when going opposite, they cross in 10 sec; same direction in 50 sec. Find speeds.

Let speeds = S₁ and S₂ m/s.
Opposite: (S₁ + S₂) = 500/10 = 50 m/s
Same: (S₁ − S₂) = 500/50 = 10 m/s

$$S_1 = \frac{50+10}{2} = 30 \text{ m/s} = 108 \text{ km/hr}$$
$$S_2 = \frac{50-10}{2} = 20 \text{ m/s} = 72 \text{ km/hr}$$

---

### ─── ADVANCED LEVEL ───

#### Example 14: Three Crossings — Find Both Length and Speed

> A train crosses:
> - A standing man in 8 seconds
> - A 264 m bridge in 20 seconds
> Find length and speed.

Let L = length, S = speed.
8S = L ... (i)
20S = L + 264 ... (ii)

(ii) − (i): 12S = 264 → **S = 22 m/s = 79.2 km/hr**
L = 8 × 22 = **176 m**

---

#### Example 15: Relative Speed — Trains Running and Meeting Time

> Train P leaves city A at 8 AM going at 60 km/hr. Train Q leaves city B (300 km from A) at 9 AM at 90 km/hr towards A. When do they meet?

At 9 AM (when Q starts), P has covered 60 km. Remaining gap = 240 km.
Relative speed = 60 + 90 = 150 km/hr.
Time = 240/150 = 1.6 hours = **1 hr 36 min** after 9 AM = **10:36 AM**

---

#### Example 16: Speeds Given as Ratio

> Two trains of equal length cross each other in 10 sec (opposite) and 30 sec (same direction). Find length of each train.

Let speed = a and b m/s. Length = L each.

Opposite: 2L/(a+b) = 10 → 2L = 10(a+b) ... (i)
Same: 2L/(a−b) = 30 → 2L = 30(a−b) ... (ii)

From (i) and (ii): 10(a+b) = 30(a−b) → a+b = 3a−3b → 4b = 2a → **a = 2b**

Let b = k: a = 2k.
From (i): 2L = 10(3k) = 30k → L = 15k.
But we need actual lengths — we need one more value. If given that one train is 150 m:
L = 150 m, 15k = 150 → k = 10.
a = 20 m/s, b = 10 m/s. ✓

**This type of question usually gives one extra piece of info to find exact lengths.**

---

#### Example 17: A Train Passes Poles at Given Intervals

> A train passes two successive kilometre posts in 40 seconds and 50 seconds. Find the train's length.

Between consecutive km posts = 1000 m.

When train passes 1st post: it takes 40 seconds — this means the train crosses the POST (length = train length) in 40 sec.

Wait — "passes a km post" = crosses a pole = L/S = 40s ... this would mean same time.

Actually "passes successive km posts" means:
- It takes 40 sec to go from one post to the next (covering 1 km = 1000 m) — but then why two times?

Re-interpretation: The train takes 40 sec to pass the first pole and 50 sec to pass the second pole. But that doesn't make sense either if speed is constant.

**Correct interpretation:** Time to pass 1st post = 40 sec (the front-to-rear crossing = L/S). Time BETWEEN passing first post and second post = 50 sec (front-front or rear-rear = 1000/S).

50 = 1000/S → S = 20 m/s.
L = S × 40 = 20 × 40 = **800 m**

---

#### Example 18: Man on Train Sees Another Train

> A man on a train (travelling at 60 km/hr) sees a train going in the OPPOSITE direction at 90 km/hr. The train passes him in 8 seconds. Find the length of the other train.

Relative speed = 60 + 90 = 150 km/hr = 150 × 5/18 = 125/3 m/s
Length of other train = (125/3) × 8 = **1000/3 ≈ 333.33 m**

---

#### Example 19: Complex — Two Trains from Same Station

> Two trains start from the same station at 6 AM and 7 AM at 40 and 60 km/hr. The second train runs on a parallel track. When does the second train overtake the first?

By 7 AM, Train 1 has covered 40 km (head start).
Relative speed = 60 − 40 = 20 km/hr.
Time = 40/20 = 2 hours after 7 AM = **9 AM**

Distances: Train 1 from 6AM to 9AM = 3 hrs × 40 = 120 km.
Train 2 from 7AM to 9AM = 2 hrs × 60 = 120 km. ✓

## 5. SPECIAL TRAIN CASES

---

### Case 1: Train crosses a man standing on a platform

> The train only needs to pass the STANDING MAN (a point), not the platform.
> Distance = **L_train only**

---

### Case 2: Train length = Platform length

> Time to cross platform = 2 × Time to cross a pole
> (Since distance doubles but speed is same)

---

### Case 3: Same speed trains going opposite

> If S₁ = S₂, relative speed = 2S.
> They cross each other in exactly half the time they'd take if one were stationary.

---

### Case 4: One train stopped on track

> Crossing a stopped train = same as crossing a platform of the same length.
> T = (L₁ + L₂) / S_moving

---

### Case 5: Tunnel crossing

> Treat tunnel exactly like a platform/bridge.
> T = (L_train + L_tunnel) / S_train

## 6. CORE CONCEPT OF BOATS & STREAMS

---

### 🔑 The Big Idea:

> A boat in a river experiences TWO forces:
> 1. Its **own engine/rowing force** → gives it speed in **still water**
> 2. The **river current** → adds or subtracts from effective speed

---

### The Core Analogy:

```
DOWNSTREAM (Going WITH current — river helps):
   Boat  →→→  +  Current  →→→  =  FASTER  →→→
   Effective speed = Boat speed + Current speed

UPSTREAM (Going AGAINST current — river resists):
   Boat  →→→  −  Current  ←←←  =  SLOWER  →
   Effective speed = Boat speed − Current speed
```

---

### Standard Notation:

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **u** | Speed of boat in **still water** (own speed) |
| **v** | Speed of **stream/current** |
| **D_s** | **Downstream** speed = u + v |
| **U_s** | **Upstream** speed = u − v |

---

### Always Remember:

```
u > v always (otherwise boat can't go upstream — it gets swept away)
D_s > U_s always (downstream is always faster)
D_s + U_s = 2u (add them → 2 × boat speed)
D_s − U_s = 2v (subtract → 2 × current speed)
```

---

## 7. ALL BOATS & STREAMS FORMULAS

---

### The 4 Core Formulas:

$$\boxed{D_s = u + v \quad \text{(Downstream speed)}}$$

$$\boxed{U_s = u - v \quad \text{(Upstream speed)}}$$

$$\boxed{u = \frac{D_s + U_s}{2} \quad \text{(Boat speed in still water)}}$$

$$\boxed{v = \frac{D_s - U_s}{2} \quad \text{(Stream/Current speed)}}$$

---

### Derived Formulas:

**Time downstream for distance d:**
$$T_{down} = \frac{d}{u + v}$$

**Time upstream for distance d:**
$$T_{up} = \frac{d}{u - v}$$

**Round trip time (same distance d both ways):**
$$T_{total} = \frac{d}{u+v} + \frac{d}{u-v} = \frac{2ud}{u^2 - v^2}$$

**If time ratio upstream:downstream = n:1 (same distance):**
$$\frac{u+v}{u-v} = n \quad \Rightarrow \quad u = v \cdot \frac{n+1}{n-1}$$

---

### If a Swimmer Crosses a River:

When a swimmer crosses (perpendicular to river flow):
- Speed across = u (swimmer's speed)
- Speed of drift = v (current)
- **Actual speed** = √(u² + v²)
- **Width** = u × time
- **Drift** = v × time

## 8. BOATS & STREAMS EXAMPLES — BASIC TO ADVANCED

---

### ─── BASIC LEVEL ───

#### Example 20: Find Boat Speed and Stream Speed

> A boat goes 40 km downstream in 4 hours and 24 km upstream in 6 hours. Find the speed of the boat in still water and the speed of the stream.

Downstream speed = 40/4 = **10 km/hr**
Upstream speed = 24/6 = **4 km/hr**

$$u = \frac{10 + 4}{2} = 7 \text{ km/hr (boat speed)}$$
$$v = \frac{10 - 4}{2} = 3 \text{ km/hr (stream speed)}$$

---

#### Example 21: Time for Round Trip

> Boat speed in still water = 15 km/hr. Stream = 5 km/hr. Find time to go 60 km and return.

DS = 15 + 5 = 20 km/hr → time down = 60/20 = 3 hrs
US = 15 − 5 = 10 km/hr → time up = 60/10 = 6 hrs
**Total time = 9 hours**

---

#### Example 22: Find Distance when Times are Given

> A man rows at 8 km/hr in still water. Stream speed = 2 km/hr. He rows downstream for 3 hours. How far did he go?

DS = 8 + 2 = 10 km/hr
Distance = 10 × 3 = **30 km**

---

#### Example 23: Find Speed of Stream

> A boat travels 20 km upstream in 2.5 hours and 20 km downstream in 1.25 hours. Find stream speed.

US = 20/2.5 = 8 km/hr
DS = 20/1.25 = 16 km/hr
Stream = (16−8)/2 = **4 km/hr**

---

#### Example 24: When Boat Speed and Stream Speed Given — Find Time

> A motorboat whose speed in still water is 18 km/hr takes 1 hour more to go 24 km upstream than downstream. Find stream speed.

Let stream speed = v.
Time upstream = 24/(18−v). Time downstream = 24/(18+v).

$$\frac{24}{18-v} - \frac{24}{18+v} = 1$$

$$24\left[\frac{(18+v)-(18-v)}{(18-v)(18+v)}\right] = 1$$

$$24 \times \frac{2v}{324 - v^2} = 1$$

$$48v = 324 - v^2$$

$$v^2 + 48v - 324 = 0$$

$$(v+54)(v-6) = 0 \Rightarrow v = 6 \text{ km/hr}$$

**Stream speed = 6 km/hr**

---

### ─── INTERMEDIATE LEVEL ───

#### Example 25: How Far Can a Man Go and Return in Given Time?

> A man rows 10 km/hr in still water. Stream = 2 km/hr. How far downstream can he go and come back in 6 hours total?

DS = 12 km/hr, US = 8 km/hr.
Let distance = d.

$$\frac{d}{12} + \frac{d}{8} = 6$$

$$\frac{2d + 3d}{24} = 6 \Rightarrow 5d = 144 \Rightarrow d = 28.8 \text{ km}$$

**He can go 28.8 km downstream and return.**

---

#### Example 26: Upstream:Downstream Time Ratio

> A boat takes 3 times as long to row upstream as downstream for the same distance. Stream = 4 km/hr. Find boat speed.

$$\frac{T_{up}}{T_{down}} = 3 \Rightarrow \frac{u+v}{u-v} = 3$$

$$u + v = 3(u - v) = 3u - 3v$$

$$4v = 2u \Rightarrow u = 2v = 2 \times 4 = \textbf{8 km/hr}$$

---

#### Example 27: Two Boats Meet

> Boat A travels DS at 12 km/hr and Boat B travels US at 8 km/hr. They start from opposite ends of a 100 km river. After how long do they meet?

Relative speed = DS speed of A + US speed of B... 

Wait — both boats have different stream effects.
Let stream = v.
Boat A (DS) = u_A + v = 12. Boat B (US) = u_B − v = 8.

When they move towards each other on the river:
If A goes DS and B goes US (towards each other):
Relative closing speed = 12 + 8 = 20 km/hr.
Time = 100/20 = **5 hours**

> **Key insight:** Regardless of stream, closing speed = DS speed of one + US speed of other (when moving towards each other).

---

#### Example 28: How Many Times Will They Meet?

> A boat makes a round trip between two points 60 km apart. DS speed = 15 km/hr, US = 10 km/hr. Another boat makes the same trip at DS = 12 km/hr and US = 8 km/hr. How many times do they meet in one complete trip of each?

This is complex — typically solved using time ratios. For standard exams:

Time for A to complete round trip = 60/15 + 60/10 = 4 + 6 = 10 hrs
Time for B to complete round trip = 60/12 + 60/8 = 5 + 7.5 = 12.5 hrs
LCM of 10 and 12.5 = 50 hrs.
In 50 hrs: A completes 5 trips, B completes 4 trips.
Meetings = 5 + 4 − 1 = **8 times** (approximate for exam purposes).

---

#### Example 29: Swimmer Crossing a River

> A swimmer crosses a 400 m wide river. He can swim at 5 m/s in still water. Current = 3 m/s. Find:
> a) Time to cross
> b) Drift (how far downstream he ends up)

He swims perpendicular to the bank.
Time = width/swimmer speed = 400/5 = **80 seconds**
Drift = current × time = 3 × 80 = **240 m downstream**
Actual distance travelled = √(400² + 240²) = √(160000 + 57600) = √217600 = **466.7 m**

---

#### Example 30: Minimum Drift — Swimmer Heading Upstream at Angle

> Swimmer speed = 5 m/s, current = 3 m/s, river width = 400 m. At what angle should he swim to reach directly opposite?

He must aim upstream at angle θ where sin θ = v/u = 3/5.
θ = arcsin(3/5) = **37°** upstream from perpendicular.

Effective crossing speed = √(u²−v²) = √(25−9) = √16 = **4 m/s**
Time = 400/4 = **100 seconds** (takes longer but no drift)

---

### ─── ADVANCED LEVEL ───

#### Example 31: Boat Speed from Distances and Common Time

> A and B are two points on a river. A boat going from A→B→A takes 10 hours total. Downstream (A to B) takes 4 hours. Find speed of current if distance AB = 48 km.

DS time = 4 hrs → US time = 10 − 4 = 6 hrs.
DS = 48/4 = 12 km/hr. US = 48/6 = 8 km/hr.
Stream speed = (12−8)/2 = **2 km/hr**

---

#### Example 32: Boat and Speed Changes

> When speed of stream doubles, a boat's upstream speed halves. Find ratio of boat speed to stream speed.

Let boat speed = u, stream = v.
Normal US = u − v.
When stream doubles: new US = u − 2v = (u−v)/2.

$$u - 2v = \frac{u-v}{2}$$
$$2u - 4v = u - v$$
$$u = 3v$$

$$\frac{u}{v} = \frac{3}{1} \rightarrow \text{Ratio} = \mathbf{3:1}$$

---

#### Example 33: Distance Downstream When Upstream Takes Twice as Long

> A boat's speed is 5 km/hr in still water. It goes downstream for 1 hour and upstream for 3 hours, covering a total of 15 km. Find stream speed.

Let stream = v. DS = 5+v, US = 5−v.
$(5+v)(1) + (5−v)(3) = 15$
$5+v + 15 − 3v = 15$
$20 − 2v = 15$
$2v = 5 \Rightarrow v = 2.5$ km/hr

**Stream speed = 2.5 km/hr**

---

#### Example 34: Speed Upstream = Half Speed Downstream

> A boat's upstream speed is half its downstream speed. If the boat's speed in still water is 12 km/hr, find stream speed.

$U_s = D_s / 2$
$u - v = (u + v)/2$
$2u - 2v = u + v$
$u = 3v$
$12 = 3v \Rightarrow v = 4$ km/hr

**Stream speed = 4 km/hr**
DS = 16, US = 8. Check: 8 = 16/2 ✓

---

#### Example 35: Complex — Find Which Boat is Faster

> Boat A: still water speed = 10 km/hr, stream = 2 km/hr. Boat B: still water speed = 8 km/hr, stream = 4 km/hr. Both need to go 40 km upstream. Which is faster and by how much?

Boat A upstream = 10 − 2 = 8 km/hr → Time = 40/8 = 5 hrs
Boat B upstream = 8 − 4 = 4 km/hr → Time = 40/4 = 10 hrs

**Boat A is faster by 5 hours.**

---

## 9. SPECIAL BOAT CASES

### Case 1: Boat goes downstream, comes back — finds net time

$$T_{round} = \frac{2ud}{u^2 - v^2}$$

---

### Case 2: Upstream time = k × Downstream time (same distance)

$$u = v \cdot \frac{k+1}{k-1}$$

---

### Case 3: Man rows in still lake (v = 0)

DS = US = u. All times are just distance/u.

---

### Case 4: Boat cannot go upstream (v ≥ u)

If stream speed ≥ boat speed → boat can't go upstream.
Net upstream speed = 0 or negative → not possible.

---

### Case 5: Speed of boat in still water from average speed

Average speed of round trip:
$$\bar{S} = \frac{2 \times D_s \times U_s}{D_s + U_s} = \frac{2(u+v)(u-v)}{2u} = \frac{u^2-v^2}{u}$$

## 10. QUICK TRICKS & SHORTCUTS — TRAINS

---

### 🚀 TRICK T1: Pole vs Platform Instant Rule

Pole → Only train length.
Platform → Train + Platform length.
**Never mix these up.**

---

### 🚀 TRICK T2: Unit Conversion Pairs to Memorise

| 18 km/hr = 5 m/s | 36 km/hr = 10 m/s |
| 54 km/hr = 15 m/s | 72 km/hr = 20 m/s |
| 90 km/hr = 25 m/s | 108 km/hr = 30 m/s |

---

### 🚀 TRICK T3: Two Trains Cross Time Formula

Opposite: T = (L₁+L₂)/(S₁+S₂)
Same: T = (L₁+L₂)/|S₁−S₂|

---

### 🚀 TRICK T4: Find Both L and S from Two Platform Crossings

$$S = \frac{P_2 - P_1}{T_2 - T_1}$$

Where P₁, P₂ = platform lengths and T₁, T₂ = corresponding crossing times.

$$L = S \times T_1 - P_1$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK T5: Train Crossing Moving Man

Same direction: Effective speed = S_train − S_man
Opposite direction: Effective speed = S_train + S_man
Distance = L_train only.

---

### 🚀 TRICK T6: Speed Ratio from Crossing Times

If two trains cross each other in time T₁ (opposite) and T₂ (same), and total length = constant:
$$S_1 + S_2 = \frac{L}{T_1} \quad \text{and} \quad S_1 - S_2 = \frac{L}{T_2}$$

Solve for S₁ and S₂.

---

### 🚀 TRICK T7: Equal Length Trains

If L₁ = L₂ = L:
Opposite: T = 2L/(S₁+S₂)
Same: T = 2L/|S₁−S₂|

---

### 🚀 TRICK T8: When Train Crosses Pole and Platform

$$L_{train} = \frac{L_{platform} \times T_{pole}}{T_{platform} - T_{pole}}$$

$$S = \frac{L_{platform}}{T_{platform} - T_{pole}}$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK T9: Man on Train Observing Another Train

If man is on Train A (speed S_A) and sees Train B:
- Train B going opposite → Relative = S_A + S_B
- Train B going same direction → Relative = S_A − S_B (if S_B > S_A, appears to go backward)

Length of B = Relative speed × observation time.

---

### 🚀 TRICK T10: Finding Overtake Starting Point

If A starts t hours before B, and both start from same point:
Head start = S_A × t.
Time for B to catch A = Head start / (S_B − S_A).

## 11. QUICK TRICKS & SHORTCUTS — BOATS & STREAMS

---

### 🚀 TRICK B1: The Four Core Formulas (Must Memorise)

$$D_s = u + v \quad U_s = u - v$$
$$u = \frac{D_s + U_s}{2} \quad v = \frac{D_s - U_s}{2}$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK B2: Upstream k Times Longer Than Downstream

$$u = v \cdot \frac{k+1}{k-1}$$

Example: Upstream takes 3× → u = v × 4/2 = 2v.

---

### 🚀 TRICK B3: Round Trip Formula

$$T = \frac{d}{u+v} + \frac{d}{u-v} = \frac{2ud}{u^2-v^2}$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK B4: When Both Boats Travel Toward Each Other

Closing speed = DS speed of one + US speed of other.
(Or: effective relative speed = sum of their effective speeds in that direction.)

---

### 🚀 TRICK B5: If Upstream speed = (1/n) × Downstream speed

$$\frac{u-v}{u+v} = \frac{1}{n} \Rightarrow \frac{u}{v} = \frac{n+1}{n-1}$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK B6: Equal Time Upstream and Downstream

If same time t is spent going DS and US:
Total distance = (DS + US) × t / 2... wait:
Total = DS×t + US×t = (DS+US)×t = 2u×t.
**Distance covered each way is proportional to the speed.**

---

### 🚀 TRICK B7: Swimmer River Crossing

Perpendicular crossing:
- Time = Width / Swimmer speed (ignore current for time)
- Drift = Current speed × Time

To cross WITH no drift (reach exactly opposite):
- Head angle = arcsin(v/u) upstream
- Effective speed = √(u²−v²)

---

### 🚀 TRICK B8: Speed of Boat from Common Time Points

If boat takes same time going d₁ downstream and d₂ upstream:
$$\frac{d_1}{u+v} = \frac{d_2}{u-v} \Rightarrow \frac{d_1}{d_2} = \frac{u+v}{u-v}$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK B9: Average Speed for Round Trip

$$\bar{v}_{round} = \frac{u^2 - v^2}{u}$$

This is ALWAYS less than u (boat's still water speed).

---

### 🚀 TRICK B10: Distance When Total Time Is Given

$$d = \frac{T \times (u+v)(u-v)}{2u} = \frac{T(u^2-v^2)}{2u}$$

## 12. COMMON MISTAKE ALERTS

---

### ⚠️ TRAINS — MISTAKE 1: Not Adding Train's Own Length

**Wrong:** A train crossing a 200 m platform travels 200 m.
**Right:** Distance = Train length + 200 m.

---

### ⚠️ TRAINS — MISTAKE 2: Not Converting km/hr to m/s

When length is in metres, speed MUST be in m/s.
**Wrong:** Time = 300 m / 54 km/hr
**Right:** 54 km/hr = 15 m/s → Time = 300/15 = 20 sec

---

### ⚠️ TRAINS — MISTAKE 3: Relative Speed Direction Error

**Wrong (Opposite trains):** Relative = S₁ − S₂
**Right:** Relative = S₁ **+** S₂ (opposite = add)

**Wrong (Same direction):** Relative = S₁ + S₂
**Right:** Relative = S₁ **−** S₂ (same = subtract)

---

### ⚠️ TRAINS — MISTAKE 4: Crossing a Man on Platform

The man stands ON the platform but is a POINT.
Train only needs to cover its OWN length to pass the man.
**NOT** Train + Platform.

---

### ⚠️ TRAINS — MISTAKE 5: Ignoring One Train's Length

In two-train problems: Distance = L₁ + L₂ (BOTH lengths).
**Wrong:** Distance = only the longer train's length.

---

### ⚠️ BOATS — MISTAKE 1: Confusing u and v

**u = boat's OWN speed** (in still water)
**v = STREAM speed**

Beginners often mix these up. Always define clearly at start.

---

### ⚠️ BOATS — MISTAKE 2: DS Formula Reversed

**Wrong:** DS = u − v (incorrect — going WITH current should be FASTER)
**Right:** DS = u + v (downstream = boat + current)

Memory: **D**ownstream = **D**own = boat **D**rops the upstream resistance = goes **faster** = **u+v**

---

### ⚠️ BOATS — MISTAKE 3: Averaging Speeds for Round Trip

**Wrong:** Average speed = (DS + US)/2
**Right:** Average speed = 2×DS×US/(DS+US) OR use Total D/Total T

---

### ⚠️ BOATS — MISTAKE 4: Upstream vs. Time to Upstream

"Time to go upstream" → Use UPSTREAM SPEED = u−v in denominator.
"Time to go downstream" → Use DOWNSTREAM SPEED = u+v in denominator.

---

### ⚠️ BOATS — MISTAKE 5: Assuming v = 0 for Still Lake

In a still lake, v = 0.
DS = US = u. No adjustment needed.
**BUT** if given "calm water" rate vs actual rate, check if there's a current before assuming v = 0.

---

### ⚠️ BOATS — MISTAKE 6: Round Trip Time Formula Misuse

$$T = \frac{2ud}{u^2 - v^2}$$

This formula applies ONLY when BOTH legs are equal distance d.
Don't use it when distances are different.

## 🏆 TOP EXAM TIPS

**TRAINS:**
1. **Convert km/hr → m/s FIRST** before any calculation when lengths are in metres.
2. **The distance crossed = sum of all object lengths** — train's own + whatever it crosses.
3. **Pole vs Platform confusion** is the #1 trap. Pole = train length only. Platform = train + platform.
4. **Two trains opposite = add speeds; same direction = subtract speeds.** Write this on your paper before starting.
5. When asked to find both **length and speed**, use two crossing events and form two equations.

**BOATS & STREAMS:**
6. **Always define u and v at the start** of every problem. Don't mix them up mid-solution.
7. **DS = u+v (faster), US = u−v (slower)** — downstream the river HELPS, upstream it RESISTS.
8. **Average speed for round trip ≠ (DS+US)/2** — use 2ud/(u²−v²) or Total D / Total T.
9. **When upstream time = k × downstream time** → Use u = v(k+1)/(k−1).
10. **River crossing (swimming)**: Time = width/swim speed. Drift = current × time. These are INDEPENDENT.
