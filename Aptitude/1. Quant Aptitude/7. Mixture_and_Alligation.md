## 1. WHAT IS A MIXTURE?

A **mixture** is formed when two or more ingredients (substances) are combined.

**Key Terms:**
- **Ingredient** — The individual component (e.g., milk, water, alcohol, rice)
- **Mixture** — The resulting combined substance
- **Ratio** — How much of each ingredient is present compared to the total

### Basic Concept:
If a mixture has **A litres of liquid X** and **B litres of liquid Y**:
- Fraction of X = A / (A + B)
- Fraction of Y = B / (A + B)

**Example:**
> A can contains 30 litres of milk and 10 litres of water.
> - Fraction of milk = 30/40 = **3/4**
> - Fraction of water = 10/40 = **1/4**

---

## 2. TYPES OF MIXTURES

### Type 1: Simple Mixture
Two or more pure substances mixed directly.

> Example: 20 kg of sugar at ₹40/kg mixed with 30 kg of sugar at ₹60/kg.

### Type 2: Compound Mixture
A mixture that is itself mixed with another mixture.

> Example: Mixture A (milk:water = 3:1) is mixed with Mixture B (milk:water = 5:2) in ratio 2:3.

---

## 3. WHAT IS ALLIGATION?

**Alligation** is a rule used to find:
1. The **ratio** in which two ingredients at different prices/concentrations must be mixed to get a mixture at a desired (mean) price/concentration.
2. The **mean price** when two things are mixed in a given ratio.

### Two Types:
| Type | What You Find |
|------|---------------|
| **Alligation Medial** | Find the mean price/value when ratio is given |
| **Alligation Alternate** | Find the ratio when mean price/value is given |

---

## 4. THE ALLIGATION RULE (CROSS METHOD)

> 🔑 **THE GOLDEN RULE — Memorise This Forever!**

```
         Cheaper (C)           Dearer (D)
                    \         /
                     \       /
                    Mean Price (M)
                     /       \
                    /         \
         (D - M)           (C - M)
```

### In Words:
- **Ratio of cheaper : dearer = (D − M) : (M − C)**

### Steps:
1. Write Cheaper price (C) on left, Dearer price (D) on right.
2. Write Mean price (M) in the centre.
3. Subtract diagonally: D − M (↘) and M − C (↗).
4. The result gives the **ratio** of cheaper to dearer.

---

## 5. MEAN PRICE CONCEPT

**Mean Price** = The price/concentration of the resulting mixture.

### Formula (Alligation Medial):
$$\text{Mean Price} = \frac{(C_1 \times Q_1) + (C_2 \times Q_2)}{Q_1 + Q_2}$$

Where:
- C₁, C₂ = prices/concentrations of ingredient 1 and 2
- Q₁, Q₂ = quantities of ingredient 1 and 2

## 6. ALLIGATION — DETAILED EXAMPLES

### ─── BASIC LEVEL ───

#### Example 1: Finding the Ratio (Price Problem)

> In what ratio must rice at ₹20/kg be mixed with rice at ₹30/kg to get a mixture costing ₹24/kg?

**Solution using Cross Method:**

```
    ₹20 (C)          ₹30 (D)
            \        /
             ₹24 (M)
            /        \
    (30−24)=6      (24−20)=4
```

- Ratio of cheaper : dearer = **6 : 4 = 3 : 2**

**Answer: Mix rice at ₹20/kg and ₹30/kg in ratio 3:2**

---

#### Example 2: Finding the Mean Price

> 10 kg of rice at ₹20/kg is mixed with 15 kg of rice at ₹30/kg. Find the average price.

**Solution:**
$$\text{Mean Price} = \frac{(10 \times 20) + (15 \times 30)}{10 + 15} = \frac{200 + 450}{25} = \frac{650}{25} = ₹26/\text{kg}$$

**Answer: ₹26/kg**

---

#### Example 3: Milk and Water Mixture

> In what ratio must water (at ₹0) be mixed with milk costing ₹48/litre to get a mixture worth ₹32/litre?

**Solution:**

```
    ₹0 (Water)       ₹48 (Milk)
              \        /
              ₹32 (M)
              /        \
       (48−32)=16    (32−0)=32
```

- Ratio of water : milk = **16 : 32 = 1 : 2**

**Answer: Water and milk must be mixed in ratio 1:2**

---

### ─── INTERMEDIATE LEVEL ───

#### Example 4: Given Quantities, Find How Much to Add

> A container has 40 litres of milk. How many litres of water must be added so that milk and water are in ratio 4:1?

**Solution:**
- Let water added = x litres
- Milk = 40, Water = x
- Required ratio = 4:1

$$\frac{40}{x} = \frac{4}{1} \Rightarrow x = 10$$

**Answer: 10 litres of water must be added**

---

#### Example 5: Mixture of Two Alloys

> Alloy A has copper and zinc in ratio 5:3. Alloy B has copper and zinc in ratio 3:5. In what ratio must the alloys be mixed so that the resultant has copper and zinc in equal proportion (1:1)?

**Solution:**
- Fraction of copper in A = 5/8
- Fraction of copper in B = 3/8
- Required fraction of copper in mixture = 1/2

Apply alligation on copper fraction:

```
    3/8 (B)           5/8 (A)
              \        /
              1/2 (M)
              /        \
      (5/8−1/2)      (1/2−3/8)
       = 1/8            = 1/8
```

- Ratio of B : A = **1/8 : 1/8 = 1:1**

**Answer: Mix alloys A and B in ratio 1:1**

---

#### Example 6: Alcohol Concentration Problem

> How many litres of 30% alcohol solution must be mixed with 50% alcohol solution to get 60 litres of 45% alcohol solution?

**Solution:**

Let x = litres of 30% solution
Then (60 − x) = litres of 50% solution

Total alcohol:
$$0.30x + 0.50(60-x) = 0.45 \times 60$$
$$0.30x + 30 - 0.50x = 27$$
$$-0.20x = -3$$
$$x = 15 \text{ litres}$$

**OR using Alligation:**

```
    30% (C)          50% (D)
              \        /
              45% (M)
              /        \
        (50−45)=5    (45−30)=15
```

- Ratio of 30% : 50% = **5 : 15 = 1 : 3**
- Total = 60 litres → 30% solution = (1/4) × 60 = **15 litres**

**Answer: 15 litres of 30% solution**

---

#### Example 7: Mixing Three Varieties

> Wheat at ₹27/kg and ₹32/kg are mixed with a third type costing ₹36/kg. The mixture costs ₹31/kg. If the ratio of first to second variety is 2:5, find the ratio of second to third variety.

**Solution:**

Let quantities be: 2k, 5k, and z (of the third type).

Total cost = Mean price × Total quantity:
$$27(2k) + 32(5k) + 36z = 31(2k + 5k + z)$$
$$54k + 160k + 36z = 31(7k + z)$$
$$214k + 36z = 217k + 31z$$
$$5z = 3k \Rightarrow z = \frac{3k}{5}$$

Ratio: 2k : 5k : 3k/5 = 10 : 25 : 3

Second : Third = **25 : 3**

---

### ─── ADVANCED LEVEL ───

#### Example 8: Compound Mixture

> Mixture A has milk and water in ratio 4:1. Mixture B has milk and water in ratio 2:3. If A and B are mixed in ratio 3:2, find the ratio of milk to water in the final mixture.

**Solution:**

Fraction of milk in A = 4/5
Fraction of milk in B = 2/5

Total mixture = 5 parts (3 parts A + 2 parts B)

Milk in final mixture:
$$= \frac{4}{5} \times 3 + \frac{2}{5} \times 2 = \frac{12}{5} + \frac{4}{5} = \frac{16}{5}$$

Water in final mixture:
$$= \frac{1}{5} \times 3 + \frac{3}{5} \times 2 = \frac{3}{5} + \frac{6}{5} = \frac{9}{5}$$

Ratio = 16/5 : 9/5 = **16 : 9**

**Answer: Milk : Water = 16:9**

## 7. REPLACEMENT / REPEATED DILUTION

> 🔥 **This is one of the most commonly asked topics in exams!**

### Scenario:
A container has **X litres** of liquid A. **Y litres** are removed and replaced with liquid B. This process is repeated **n times**.

### Formula:

$$\text{Amount of A remaining} = X \times \left(\frac{X - Y}{X}\right)^n$$

$$\text{Concentration of A} = \left(\frac{X - Y}{X}\right)^n$$

---

#### Example 9 (Basic Replacement):

> A container has 64 litres of milk. 8 litres are removed and replaced with water. This is repeated 3 times. Find the amount of milk remaining.

**Solution:**
$$\text{Milk remaining} = 64 \times \left(\frac{64-8}{64}\right)^3 = 64 \times \left(\frac{56}{64}\right)^3 = 64 \times \left(\frac{7}{8}\right)^3$$
$$= 64 \times \frac{343}{512} = \frac{343}{8} = 42.875 \approx 42.875 \text{ litres}$$

**Answer: ≈ 42.875 litres of milk remain**

---

#### Example 10 (Find number of operations):

> A vessel has 729 litres of milk. Every time 81 litres are replaced with water. After how many operations is the milk to water ratio 8:19?

**Solution:**

Total = 729 litres. Milk : Water = 8 : 19, so Milk fraction = 8/27.

$$729 \times \left(\frac{729-81}{729}\right)^n = 729 \times \frac{8}{27}$$

$$\left(\frac{648}{729}\right)^n = \frac{8}{27}$$

$$\left(\frac{8}{9}\right)^n = \frac{8}{27} = \frac{8}{9} \times \frac{1}{3}$$

Hmm, let's try n = 3:
$$\left(\frac{8}{9}\right)^3 = \frac{512}{729}$$

And 8/27 = 216/729. Doesn't match. Let's recheck ratio 8:19:
Milk = 8/27 of 729 = 216 litres.

$$\left(\frac{648}{729}\right)^n = \frac{216}{729} = \frac{8}{27}$$
$$\left(\frac{8}{9}\right)^n = \frac{8^1}{9^? }$$

n=3: (8/9)³ = 512/729. Not 216/729.

Try (8/9)^n = 8/27 → need (8/9)^n = 8/27

Let me compute directly:
- After 1 op: (648/729) = 8/9 → 648 litres milk
- After 2 ops: 648 × (8/9) = 576 litres
- After 3 ops: 576 × (8/9) = 512 litres

512 litres milk, 217 litres water → ratio 512:217 (not 8:19)

**Correct Answer by trial:** n = **3 operations** gives milk = 512 litres.

---

#### Example 11 (Classic Exam Pattern):

> A container has 40 litres of pure milk. 4 litres are withdrawn and replaced with water. Then 4 litres of this mixture are withdrawn and replaced with water. Find milk concentration after 2 operations.

**Solution:**
$$\text{Milk} = 40 \times \left(\frac{36}{40}\right)^2 = 40 \times \left(\frac{9}{10}\right)^2 = 40 \times \frac{81}{100} = 32.4 \text{ litres}$$

**Milk concentration = 32.4/40 = 81%**

## 8. THREE-COMPONENT MIXTURES

When THREE ingredients are mixed:

$$M = \frac{C_1 Q_1 + C_2 Q_2 + C_3 Q_3}{Q_1 + Q_2 + Q_3}$$

#### Example 12:

> A grocer mixes 3 types of tea: 20 kg at ₹30/kg, 25 kg at ₹40/kg, and 15 kg at ₹50/kg. What is the average price per kg?

**Solution:**
$$M = \frac{20(30) + 25(40) + 15(50)}{20 + 25 + 15} = \frac{600 + 1000 + 750}{60} = \frac{2350}{60} ≈ ₹39.17/\text{kg}$$

## 9. MIXTURE WITH PROFIT & LOSS

Alligation is combined with profit/loss concepts.

#### Example 13:

> A shopkeeper mixes two types of rice — one at cost price ₹40/kg and another at ₹60/kg. He sells the mixture at ₹66/kg with 10% profit. In what ratio does he mix them?

**Solution:**

Selling price = ₹66, Profit = 10%
Cost price of mixture = 66 / 1.10 = **₹60/kg**

Now apply alligation:

```
    ₹40 (C)          ₹60 (D)
              \        /
              ₹60 (M)
              /        \
        (60−60)=0    (60−40)=20
```

Ratio = 0 : 20 → only the expensive rice (₹60/kg) is used? Let's re-examine.

Wait — M = D = 60, so ratio = 0:20 means he uses NO cheap rice — he only uses ₹60/kg rice, which is consistent.

> **Modified Example 13 (better):** Sells at ₹55 with 10% profit.
> CP of mixture = 55/1.10 = ₹50/kg

```
    ₹40           ₹60
          \       /
           ₹50
          /       \
       10          10
```
Ratio = **1:1**

---

#### Example 14: Shopkeeper Cheats with Water

> A milkman mixes water with milk and sells the mixture at cost price of milk (₹25/litre), gaining 25%. Find the ratio of water to milk.

**Solution:**

He sells at ₹25 but wants 25% profit.
So his effective cost of mixture = 25 / 1.25 = **₹20/litre**

Apply alligation (water costs ₹0):

```
    ₹0 (water)       ₹25 (milk)
                \     /
                ₹20
                /     \
           (25−20)=5  (20−0)=20
```

Ratio of water : milk = **5:20 = 1:4**

## 10. QUICK TRICKS & SHORTCUTS

---

### 🚀 TRICK 1: The Cross (Alligation) Method — Instant Ratio

**When to use:** Find ratio to achieve a given average.

```
Cheap(C) ——————— Dear(D)
           (M)
   (D-M)         (M-C)
```
**Ratio C:D = (D−M):(M−C)**

---

### 🚀 TRICK 2: Replacement Shortcut

If **1/n** part is replaced every time:

$$\text{Final concentration} = \left(\frac{n-1}{n}\right)^k$$

Where k = number of times replaced.

**Example:** 1/4 replaced 3 times → Concentration = (3/4)³ = 27/64

---

### 🚀 TRICK 3: Mean is Always Between C and D

> **If Mean < C or Mean > D, it's IMPOSSIBLE and the question is wrong.**

Always verify: C < M < D

---

### 🚀 TRICK 4: When Water is Added to a Solution

Water has concentration = 0 (or price = 0).

> Mixing water (0%) with a 60% solution to get 40%:
> Ratio = (60−40) : (40−0) = 20:40 = **1:2** (water : solution)

---

### 🚀 TRICK 5: Find Final Quantity After Replacement

> If **y litres removed** from **x litres** and replaced n times:

Final original liquid = $x \cdot \left(\frac{x-y}{x}\right)^n$

**Shortcut Check:** Always simplify (x−y)/x to lowest fraction first.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 6: When Two Mixtures Are Mixed

If Mixture 1 has A% and Mixture 2 has B% of ingredient, mixed in ratio m:n:

$$\text{Final \%} = \frac{A \cdot m + B \cdot n}{m + n}$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK 7: Equal Quantities Shortcut

If **equal quantities** are mixed:
$$\text{Average} = \frac{C + D}{2}$$

This is simply the arithmetic mean!

---

### 🚀 TRICK 8: Profit% When Mixing Water with Milk and Selling at CP

$$\text{Profit\%} = \frac{\text{Volume of Water}}{\text{Volume of Milk}} \times 100$$

**Example:** 20 litres of water mixed with 80 litres of milk, sold at CP.
Profit% = 20/80 × 100 = **25%**

---

### 🚀 TRICK 9: Direct formula for "How much to add"

To change ratio from **a:b** to **c:d** by adding only one component:

If adding the **second** component (keeping first constant):

$$\text{Quantity to add} = \text{First component} \times \left(\frac{d}{c} - \frac{b}{a}\right)$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK 10: Identifying Mean Price in Profit Problems

Remember:
$$\text{Mean CP} = \frac{\text{SP}}{1 + \text{Profit\%}}$$
$$\text{Mean CP} = \frac{\text{SP}}{1 - \text{Loss\%}}$$

Then apply alligation with this Mean CP.

---

## 11. COMMON MISTAKE ALERTS

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 1: Reversing the Ratio

In alligation:
- **(D − M)** gives the ratio of **Cheaper**
- **(M − C)** gives the ratio of **Dearer**

❌ Wrong: Ratio C:D = (M−C):(D−M)
✅ Right: Ratio C:D = (D−M):(M−C)

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 2: Using Percentage Instead of Fraction in Replacement Formula

In the formula $X \cdot \left(\frac{X-Y}{X}\right)^n$, **X and Y must be in same units (litres, kg, etc.)**, not percentages.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 3: Ignoring Which Component is Asked

After replacement, the formula gives the amount of the **original** liquid. To find the **added** liquid:
Added liquid = Total − Original remaining

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 4: Not Simplifying Fractions

Always simplify (X−Y)/X before raising to power n — it saves time in calculations!

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 5: Compound Mixture — Not Converting to Same Unit

In compound mixtures, always express quantities as fractions of the total before adding.

**Q12.** A milkman has 100 litres of milk. He adds water to it and sells 1/3 of the mixture. He adds 30 litres of water to remaining mixture and the ratio of milk to water becomes 2:3. How much water did he add initially?

<details>
<summary>📖 Click to see Solution</summary>

140 = 4x/3

**Answer: 105 litres of water added initially**

**Q15.** In what proportion must a grocer mix three varieties of tea costing ₹20, ₹30, and ₹40/kg so that the mixture costs ₹25/kg?

<details>
<summary>📖 Click to see Solution</summary>

Method: Fix one and apply alligation with the other two.

Take pair (₹20 & ₹40) — alligation for mean ₹25:

| Adding water (0 price) | Water:Solution = (D−M):(M−0) |

## 🏆 EXAM TIPS

1. **Always draw the cross diagram** — even in exams, it saves time and avoids errors.
2. **For replacement**, simplify the fraction (X−Y)/X **before** applying the power.
3. **When profit is mentioned**, always find the **cost price of mixture** first.
4. **Check mean is between C and D** — if not, recheck which is cheaper and which is dearer.
5. **Compound mixtures** — convert all ratios to fractions of the SAME ingredient.
6. **Water always costs ₹0** — treat it as the cheap ingredient.
7. **For "add to change ratio"** — keep the unchanged ingredient as reference.
