## 1. THE BIG PICTURE — WHAT IS MENSURATION 3D?

---

### 🔑 Definition:

> **3D Mensuration** deals with **solid (three-dimensional) figures** that have length, width, AND height (depth). We measure their **surface areas** and **volumes**.

---

### Two Key Measurements in 3D:

| Measurement | What It Measures | Unit |
|------------|-----------------|------|
| **Surface Area** | Total area of all outer surfaces | cm², m² |
| **Volume** | Total space enclosed inside the solid | cm³, m³, litres |

---

### Surface Area — Two Types:

| Type | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Lateral / Curved Surface Area (LSA/CSA)** | Only the side surfaces (not top and bottom) |
| **Total Surface Area (TSA)** | ALL surfaces including top and bottom |

```
TSA = LSA + Area of base(s)
```

---

### 3D Shapes Overview:

```
                      3D SOLIDS
                          │
        ┌─────────────────┼─────────────────┐
        │                 │                 │
   POLYHEDRA         CURVED SOLIDS      COMBINED
        │                 │
   ┌────┴────┐        ┌───┴───┐
   │         │        │       │
 Cuboid   Prism   Cylinder  Sphere
  Cube   Pyramid   Cone   Hemisphere

```

## 2. CORE TERMINOLOGY — 3D SHAPES

---

### 📦 Face
A flat surface of a 3D solid.
> Cube has 6 faces, all squares.

### 📐 Edge
A line segment where two faces meet.
> Cube has 12 edges.

### 📌 Vertex
A corner point where edges meet.
> Cube has 8 vertices.

### 🎯 Slant Height (l)
The distance along the sloped surface from the base edge to the apex of a cone/pyramid.
> Different from vertical height h!

### 📏 Lateral Surface Area (LSA)
Surface area of the SIDES only (excluding top and bottom bases).

### 📦 Total Surface Area (TSA)
Surface area of ALL faces including bases.

### 💧 Volume
The space enclosed inside a 3D solid.

## 3. CUBOID (RECTANGULAR BOX)

---

### Shape Diagram:

```
         h (height)
        ╔═══════════╗
       ╱│           │╱|
      ╔═══════════╗  │
      ║           ║  │ b (breadth)
      ║           ║  │
      ╚═══════════╝ ╱
      └──────────╯
         l (length)
```

### Formulas — Cuboid (l = length, b = breadth, h = height):

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| **Volume** | l × b × h |
| **LSA** | 2h(l + b) |
| **TSA** | 2(lb + bh + hl) |
| **Diagonal** | √(l² + b² + h²) |

---

### Important Special Cases:

- If l = b = h → it becomes a **Cube**
- If h = 0 → it becomes a **Rectangle** (2D)

---

### Examples — Cuboid:

#### Example 1: Basic Cuboid

> A box has l = 12 cm, b = 8 cm, h = 5 cm. Find volume, LSA, TSA.

$$V = 12 \times 8 \times 5 = \textbf{480 cm³}$$

$$\text{LSA} = 2 \times 5 \times (12+8) = 10 \times 20 = \textbf{200 cm²}$$

$$\text{TSA} = 2(12 \times 8 + 8 \times 5 + 5 \times 12) = 2(96 + 40 + 60) = 2 \times 196 = \textbf{392 cm²}$$

---

#### Example 2: Diagonal of Cuboid

> Room dimensions: 10 m × 6 m × 4 m. Find the length of the longest rod that can fit.

$$\text{Diagonal} = \sqrt{10^2 + 6^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{100+36+16} = \sqrt{152} = 2\sqrt{38} \approx \textbf{12.33 m}$$

---

#### Example 3: Finding Dimension from Volume and Others

> Volume = 1200 cm³, l = 20 cm, b = 15 cm. Find h.

$$h = \frac{V}{l \times b} = \frac{1200}{300} = \textbf{4 cm}$$

---

#### Example 4: Cuboid with Cost of Painting

> A room 12 m × 10 m × 6 m. Cost of painting all 4 walls and ceiling at ₹5/m². Find cost.

LSA (4 walls) = 2 × 6 × (12+10) = 12 × 22 = 264 m²
Ceiling = 12 × 10 = 120 m²
Total = 264 + 120 = 384 m²
Cost = 384 × 5 = **₹1920**

## 4. CUBE

---

### Shape Diagram:

```
     ┌───────┐
    /│      /│ a
   ┌─┴─────┐ │
   │ │     │ │
 a │ └─────┤─┘ a
   │/      │/
   └───────┘
        a
```

### Formulas — Cube (side = a):

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| **Volume** | a³ |
| **LSA** | 4a² |
| **TSA** | 6a² |
| **Diagonal (space)** | a√3 |
| **Face diagonal** | a√2 |
| **Number of faces** | 6 |
| **Number of edges** | 12 |
| **Number of vertices** | 8 |

---

### Examples — Cube:

#### Example 5: Basic Cube

> Cube of side 7 cm. Find volume, LSA, TSA.

$$V = 7^3 = \textbf{343 cm³}$$

$$\text{LSA} = 4 \times 49 = \textbf{196 cm²}$$

$$\text{TSA} = 6 \times 49 = \textbf{294 cm²}$$

---

#### Example 6: Cube from Volume

> Volume = 512 cm³. Find side, TSA, diagonal.

$$a = \sqrt[3]{512} = \textbf{8 cm}$$

$$\text{TSA} = 6 \times 64 = \textbf{384 cm²}$$

$$\text{Diagonal} = 8\sqrt{3} \approx \textbf{13.86 cm}$$

---

#### Example 7: Small Cubes from Big Cube

> A cube of side 12 cm is cut into small cubes of side 2 cm. How many small cubes are formed?

$$n = \left(\frac{12}{2}\right)^3 = 6^3 = \textbf{216 cubes}$$

---

#### Example 8: Painted Cube Problem

> A cube of side 4 cm is painted on all faces. It is then cut into 1 cm³ cubes. Find:
> (a) Cubes with 3 faces painted
> (b) Cubes with 2 faces painted
> (c) Cubes with 1 face painted
> (d) Cubes with 0 faces painted

Total small cubes = 4³ = 64

(a) **Corners** → 3 faces painted: always **8** (corners of big cube)
(b) **Edges (not corners)** → 2 faces: each edge has (n-2) = 2. 12 edges × 2 = **24**
(c) **Face centres** → 1 face: each face has (n-2)² = 4. 6 × 4 = **24**
(d) **Inner cubes** → 0 faces: (n-2)³ = 2³ = **8**

**Verify:** 8 + 24 + 24 + 8 = **64** ✓

## 5. CYLINDER

---

### Shape Diagram:

```
    ____
   /    \   ← circular base radius r
  │  r   │
  │      │ h (height)
  │      │
   \____/
```

### Formulas — Cylinder (r = radius, h = height):

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| **Volume** | πr²h |
| **CSA (Curved)** | 2πrh |
| **TSA** | 2πr(r + h) |
| **Base Area** | πr² |

> TSA = CSA + 2 × Base Area = 2πrh + 2πr²

---

### Hollow Cylinder (outer radius R, inner radius r, height h):

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| **Volume** | π(R² − r²)h |
| **CSA** | 2π(R + r)h |
| **TSA** | 2π(R + r)(h + R − r) |

---

### Examples — Cylinder:

#### Example 9: Basic Cylinder

> Cylinder: r = 7 cm, h = 10 cm. Find Volume, CSA, TSA.

$$V = \frac{22}{7} \times 49 \times 10 = 22 \times 70 = \textbf{1540 cm³}$$

$$\text{CSA} = 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times 10 = 2 \times 22 \times 10 = \textbf{440 cm²}$$

$$\text{TSA} = 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times (7+10) = 44 \times 17 = \textbf{748 cm²}$$

---

#### Example 10: Finding Radius from Volume

> Volume of cylinder = 1386 cm³, height = 9 cm. Find radius.

$$\pi r^2 h = 1386 \Rightarrow \frac{22}{7} \times r^2 \times 9 = 1386$$

$$r^2 = \frac{1386 \times 7}{22 \times 9} = \frac{9702}{198} = 49 \Rightarrow r = \textbf{7 cm}$$

---

#### Example 11: Hollow Cylinder

> Hollow cylinder: R = 10 cm, r = 7 cm, h = 20 cm. Find Volume.

$$V = \pi(R^2 - r^2)h = \frac{22}{7}(100-49)(20) = \frac{22}{7} \times 51 \times 20 = \textbf{3205.71 cm³}$$

---

#### Example 12: Cylinder Formed by Rolling a Rectangle

> A rectangular sheet 44 cm × 14 cm is rolled along the 14 cm side. Find volume of cylinder formed.

When rolled along 14 cm: circumference = 44 cm → r = 44/(2π) = 44 × 7/(2 × 22) = 7 cm.
Height h = 14 cm.

$$V = \pi r^2 h = \frac{22}{7} \times 49 \times 14 = 22 \times 98 = \textbf{2156 cm³}$$

## 6. CONE

---

### Shape Diagram:

```
        ●  ← Apex
       /|\
      / | \
     /  |  \
    /   |h  \  l = slant height
   /    |    \
  /_____|_____\
        r        ← base circle radius r
```

### Key Relationship:

$$\boxed{l^2 = r^2 + h^2 \Rightarrow l = \sqrt{r^2 + h^2}}$$

---

### Formulas — Cone (r = base radius, h = height, l = slant height):

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| **Volume** | (1/3)πr²h |
| **CSA** | πrl |
| **TSA** | πr(r + l) = πrl + πr² |
| **Slant height** | l = √(r² + h²) |

> Volume of cone = (1/3) × Volume of cylinder with same r, h.

---

### Examples — Cone:

#### Example 13: Basic Cone

> Cone: r = 7 cm, h = 24 cm. Find slant height, Volume, CSA, TSA.

$$l = \sqrt{7^2 + 24^2} = \sqrt{49+576} = \sqrt{625} = \textbf{25 cm}$$

$$V = \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 49 \times 24 = \frac{1}{3} \times 22 \times 7 \times 24 = \frac{3696}{3} = \textbf{1232 cm³}$$

$$\text{CSA} = \frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times 25 = 22 \times 25 = \textbf{550 cm²}$$

$$\text{TSA} = \frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times (7+25) = 22 \times 32 = \textbf{704 cm²}$$

---

#### Example 14: Cone from CSA and Radius

> CSA of cone = 4070 cm², r = 35 cm. Find slant height and height.

$$\pi r l = 4070 \Rightarrow \frac{22}{7} \times 35 \times l = 4070$$

$$110l = 4070 \Rightarrow l = \textbf{37 cm}$$

$$h = \sqrt{l^2 - r^2} = \sqrt{37^2 - 35^2} = \sqrt{1369-1225} = \sqrt{144} = \textbf{12 cm}$$

---

#### Example 15: Volumes of Cone and Cylinder

> A cylinder and cone have same base and height. Ratio of volumes?

$$\frac{V_{cone}}{V_{cylinder}} = \frac{\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h}{\pi r^2 h} = \textbf{1:3}$$

## 7. SPHERE AND HEMISPHERE

---

### Shape Diagrams:

```
SPHERE:              HEMISPHERE:
    ____                 ____
   /    \               /    \
  |  ●  |              |  ●   |
  |  r  |              |      |
   \____/              └──────┘ (flat base)
                         r
```

---

### Formulas — Sphere (radius = r):

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| **Volume** | (4/3)πr³ |
| **Surface Area** | 4πr² |
| **Diameter** | 2r |

---

### Formulas — Hemisphere (radius = r):

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| **Volume** | (2/3)πr³ |
| **CSA (curved only)** | 2πr² |
| **TSA (curved + flat)** | 3πr² |

> Hemisphere TSA = 2πr² (curved) + πr² (flat circular base) = 3πr²

---

### Relationship — Sphere, Hemisphere, Cylinder, Cone:

$$V_{sphere} = 2 \times V_{hemisphere}$$

$$V_{sphere} = \frac{2}{3} \times V_{cylinder} \text{ (same r, h=2r)}$$

---

### Examples — Sphere/Hemisphere:

#### Example 16: Basic Sphere

> Sphere of radius 7 cm. Find Surface Area and Volume.

$$SA = 4 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 49 = 4 \times 22 \times 7 = \textbf{616 cm²}$$

$$V = \frac{4}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 343 = \frac{4 \times 22 \times 49}{3} = \frac{4312}{3} \approx \textbf{1437.33 cm³}$$

---

#### Example 17: Hemisphere

> Hemisphere of radius 21 cm. Find CSA, TSA, Volume.

$$\text{CSA} = 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 441 = 2 \times 22 \times 63 = \textbf{2772 cm²}$$

$$\text{TSA} = 3 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 441 = 3 \times 22 \times 63 = \textbf{4158 cm²}$$

$$V = \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 9261 = \frac{2 \times 22 \times 1323}{3} = \frac{58212}{3} = \textbf{19404 cm³}$$

---

#### Example 18: Sphere from Surface Area

> Surface area of a sphere = 1386 cm². Find radius and volume.

$$4\pi r^2 = 1386 \Rightarrow \frac{22}{7} \times r^2 = \frac{1386}{4} = 346.5$$

$$r^2 = \frac{346.5 \times 7}{22} = \frac{2425.5}{22} = 110.25 \Rightarrow r = \textbf{10.5 cm}$$

$$V = \frac{4}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times (10.5)^3 = \frac{4}{3} \times \frac{22}{7} \times 1157.625 = \textbf{4851 cm³}$$

---

#### Example 19: Volume Ratio — Sphere to Cylinder

> A sphere is placed inside a cylinder that just fits (r of sphere = r of cylinder, h = 2r). Find ratio of their volumes.

$$V_{sphere} = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3, \quad V_{cylinder} = \pi r^2 \times 2r = 2\pi r^3$$

$$\frac{V_{sphere}}{V_{cylinder}} = \frac{4\pi r^3/3}{2\pi r^3} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3}$$

Ratio = **2:3**

## 8. PRISM (GENERAL)

---

### Definition:

A **prism** is a solid with two identical, parallel polygonal bases connected by rectangular faces.

```
        Base (any polygon)
       ┌────────┐
      /│        │\
     / │        │ \
    /  │        │  \  h = height
   /   └────────┘   \
  └────────────────────┘
        same Base
```

---

### Formulas — General Prism:

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| **Volume** | Base Area × Height |
| **LSA** | Perimeter of Base × Height |
| **TSA** | LSA + 2 × Base Area |

---

### Special Case — Triangular Prism:

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| **Volume** | (½ × base × height_triangle) × Length |
| **LSA** | (sum of 3 triangle sides) × Length |
| **TSA** | LSA + 2 × triangle area |

---

### Example — Prism:

#### Example 20: Triangular Prism

> Triangular prism: equilateral triangle base of side 6 cm, length = 12 cm.

Base area = (√3/4) × 36 = 9√3 cm²
Volume = 9√3 × 12 = **108√3 ≈ 187.06 cm³**
LSA = 3 × 6 × 12 = **216 cm²**
TSA = 216 + 2 × 9√3 = 216 + 18√3 ≈ **247.18 cm²**

## 9. PYRAMID (GENERAL)

---

### Definition:

A **pyramid** has a polygonal base and triangular faces that meet at an **apex**.

```
            ●  ← Apex
           /|\
          / | \
         /  |h \
        /   |   \
       /    |    \
      /_____|_____\
           base (polygon)
```

---

### Formulas — General Pyramid:

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| **Volume** | (1/3) × Base Area × Height |
| **LSA** | (1/2) × Perimeter of Base × Slant Height |
| **TSA** | LSA + Base Area |

---

### Special Case — Square Pyramid (base a, height h, slant height l):

$$l = \sqrt{h^2 + (a/2)^2}$$

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| **Volume** | (1/3) × a² × h |
| **LSA** | 2al |
| **TSA** | a² + 2al |

---

### Example — Square Pyramid:

#### Example 21: Square Pyramid

> Square base 12 cm, height 8 cm. Find slant height, volume, TSA.

$$l = \sqrt{8^2 + 6^2} = \sqrt{64+36} = \sqrt{100} = \textbf{10 cm}$$

$$V = \frac{1}{3} \times 144 \times 8 = \frac{1152}{3} = \textbf{384 cm³}$$

$$\text{LSA} = \frac{1}{2} \times 48 \times 10 = \textbf{240 cm²}$$

$$\text{TSA} = 144 + 240 = \textbf{384 cm²}$$

## 10. FRUSTUM OF A CONE

---

### What is a Frustum?

When a cone is cut by a plane parallel to its base, the lower portion is called a **frustum**.

```
     ________  ← Top circle (radius r)
    /        \
   /          \
  /            \
 /    height h  \
/________________\  ← Bottom circle (radius R)
         R
```

---

### Key Relationship:

$$\text{Slant height of frustum} = l = \sqrt{h^2 + (R-r)^2}$$

---

### Formulas — Frustum (R = bottom radius, r = top radius, h = height, l = slant height):

| Property | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| **Volume** | (π h/3)(R² + r² + Rr) |
| **CSA** | π(R + r)l |
| **TSA** | π(R + r)l + π(R² + r²) |
| **Slant height** | l = √[h² + (R−r)²] |

---

### Examples — Frustum:

#### Example 22: Basic Frustum

> Frustum: R = 5 cm, r = 3 cm, h = 4 cm. Find slant height, volume, CSA.

$$l = \sqrt{4^2 + (5-3)^2} = \sqrt{16+4} = \sqrt{20} = 2\sqrt{5} \approx \textbf{4.47 cm}$$

$$V = \frac{\pi \times 4}{3}(25 + 9 + 15) = \frac{4\pi}{3} \times 49 = \frac{196\pi}{3} \approx \textbf{205.33 cm³}$$

$$\text{CSA} = \pi(5+3)(2\sqrt{5}) = 16\pi\sqrt{5} \approx \textbf{112.25 cm²}$$

---

#### Example 23: Frustum from Cone

> A cone of height 12 cm and radius 9 cm is cut parallel to base at height 8 cm from base. Find the frustum dimensions.

The cutting plane is 8 cm from base → 4 cm from apex.
At 4 cm from apex: r = 9 × (4/12) = **3 cm** (by similar triangles).
Frustum: R = 9, r = 3, h = 8 cm.
l = √[8² + (9−3)²] = √[64+36] = √100 = **10 cm**

## 11. COMBINED / COMPOSITE 3D SOLIDS

---

### Strategy:

```
STEP 1: Identify each component shape
STEP 2: Calculate Volume/SA of each separately
STEP 3: For Volume → ADD all volumes
STEP 4: For Surface Area → ADD visible surfaces only
         (subtract surfaces that are internal/hidden)
```

---

### Examples:

#### Example 24: Cylinder with Conical Top

> A toy is made of a cylinder (r=3, h=4) topped with a cone (r=3, h=4). Find Volume and Surface Area.

Slant height of cone: l = √(3² + 4²) = 5 cm.

Volume:
$$V_{cyl} = \pi \times 9 \times 4 = 36\pi$$
$$V_{cone} = \frac{1}{3}\pi \times 9 \times 4 = 12\pi$$
$$V_{total} = 48\pi \approx \textbf{150.86 cm³}$$

Surface Area (visible):
- Base circle of cylinder: πr² = 9π
- Curved surface of cylinder: 2πrh = 24π
- Curved surface of cone: πrl = 15π
- (The flat circle between cone and cylinder is INTERNAL — not counted)

$$SA = 9\pi + 24\pi + 15\pi = 48\pi \approx \textbf{150.86 cm²}$$

---

#### Example 25: Sphere on Cylinder (Capsule)

> A medicine capsule: cylinder (r=3.5, h=7) with hemisphere on each end. Find Volume.

$$V_{cyl} = \pi(3.5)^2 \times 7 = \pi \times 12.25 \times 7 = 85.75\pi$$

$$V_{2 \text{ hemispheres}} = \frac{4}{3}\pi(3.5)^3 = \frac{4}{3}\pi \times 42.875 = \frac{171.5\pi}{3} = 57.17\pi$$

$$V_{total} = 85.75\pi + 57.17\pi = 142.92\pi \approx \textbf{449 cm³}$$

---

#### Example 26: Cone on Hemisphere

> A solid is made of a hemisphere (r=7) and a cone (r=7, h=24) on top. Find TSA.

l = √(7² + 24²) = √(49+576) = √625 = 25 cm.

TSA = Curved surface of hemisphere + Curved surface of cone
(No flat bases visible — hemisphere's flat part is where cone sits)

$$TSA = 2\pi r^2 + \pi r l = 2\pi(49) + \pi(7)(25) = 98\pi + 175\pi = 273\pi \approx \textbf{857.5 cm²}$$

---

#### Example 27: Cube with Conical Hole

> From a cube of side 7 cm, a cone of base radius 3 cm and height 7 cm is drilled. Find the remaining volume.

$$V_{cube} = 7^3 = 343 \text{ cm}^3$$

$$V_{cone} = \frac{1}{3} \times \pi \times 9 \times 7 = \frac{63\pi}{3} = 21\pi \approx 65.97 \text{ cm}^3$$

$$V_{remaining} = 343 - 65.97 \approx \textbf{277.03 cm}^3$$

---

## 12. MELTING & RECASTING PROBLEMS

---

### The Golden Rule:

> **When a solid is melted and recast into another shape, the VOLUME is preserved.**

$$\boxed{V_{original} = V_{new}}$$

---

### Examples:

#### Example 28: Sphere Melted into Cylinders

> A solid sphere of radius 6 cm is melted and recast into cylinders of radius 3 cm and height 4 cm. How many cylinders?

$$V_{sphere} = \frac{4}{3}\pi(6)^3 = \frac{4}{3}\pi \times 216 = 288\pi$$

$$V_{one\ cylinder} = \pi(3)^2 \times 4 = 36\pi$$

$$n = \frac{288\pi}{36\pi} = \textbf{8 cylinders}$$

---

#### Example 29: Cone Melted into Sphere

> A solid cone with r = 6 cm and h = 27 cm is melted. Find radius of sphere formed.

$$V_{cone} = \frac{1}{3}\pi(36)(27) = 324\pi$$

$$\frac{4}{3}\pi R^3 = 324\pi \Rightarrow R^3 = \frac{324 \times 3}{4} = 243 \Rightarrow R = \sqrt[3]{243} = 3\sqrt[3]{9} \approx \textbf{6.24 cm}$$

Actually: 243 = 3⁵. R = 3^(5/3) ≈ 6.24 cm. Let's verify: if R = 6 exactly: 4/3 × 6³ = 4/3 × 216 = 288 ≠ 324.

For exact answer: R³ = 243, R = ∛243 = **3∛9 cm** (leave in surd form for exam).

---

#### Example 30: Multiple Small Spheres from Big Sphere

> A metallic sphere of radius R = 12 cm is melted into small spheres of radius r = 3 cm. How many?

$$n = \frac{V_{big}}{V_{small}} = \frac{(4/3)\pi R^3}{(4/3)\pi r^3} = \frac{R^3}{r^3} = \frac{12^3}{3^3} = \frac{1728}{27} = \textbf{64 spheres}$$

---

#### Example 31: Wire from Metal Sphere

> A sphere of radius 3 cm is drawn into a cylindrical wire of diameter 0.2 cm. Find length of wire.

r_wire = 0.1 cm.
V_sphere = (4/3)π(3)³ = 36π cm³.

$$\pi(0.1)^2 \times h = 36\pi \Rightarrow 0.01h = 36 \Rightarrow h = \textbf{3600 cm = 36 m}$$

---

#### Example 32: Cuboid Melted into Cubes

> A cuboid 27 cm × 24 cm × 20 cm is melted into small cubes of side 4 cm. How many?

$$V_{cuboid} = 27 \times 24 \times 20 = 12960 \text{ cm}^3$$

$$V_{small\ cube} = 4^3 = 64 \text{ cm}^3$$

## 13. WATER LEVEL / VOLUME PROBLEMS

---

### Key Concept:

> When an object is submerged in water, the **rise in water level = volume of object displaced**.

---

### Formula:

$$\text{Volume of object} = \text{Base area of container} \times \text{Rise in water level}$$

---

### Examples:

#### Example 33: Ball Dropped in Cylinder

> A ball of radius 4.2 cm is dropped into a cylindrical vessel of radius 6 cm full to brim. Find volume of water overflowed.

Volume overflowed = Volume of ball = (4/3)π(4.2)³
= (4/3) × (22/7) × 74.088
= (4/3) × (22/7) × 74.088 ≈ **310.46 cm³**

---

#### Example 34: Rise in Water Level

> A metal sphere of radius 3 cm is dropped into a cylindrical tub of radius 6 cm. Find rise in water level.

$$V_{sphere} = \frac{4}{3}\pi(3)^3 = 36\pi \text{ cm}^3$$

$$\pi r_{tub}^2 \times h_{rise} = 36\pi$$

$$36 h_{rise} = 36 \Rightarrow h_{rise} = \textbf{1 cm}$$

---

#### Example 35: Conical Vessel Poured Into Cylinder

> A conical vessel (r=12, h=8) is full of water. It is poured into a cylinder of radius 6 cm. Find height of water in cylinder.

$$V_{cone} = \frac{1}{3}\pi(144)(8) = 384\pi$$

$$\pi(36)h = 384\pi \Rightarrow h = \frac{384}{36} = \frac{32}{3} \approx \textbf{10.67 cm}$$

---

#### Example 36: Pipe Filling Tank

> A pipe of inner diameter 4 cm flows water at 10 m/s. How long to fill a tank of 1000 litres?

r = 2 cm = 0.02 m.
Volume per second = π(0.02)² × 10 = 0.0004π m³/s = 0.4π litres/s ≈ 1.257 litres/s.

Time = 1000/1.257 ≈ **795.8 seconds ≈ 13.26 minutes**

## 14. QUICK TRICKS & SHORTCUTS

---

### 🚀 TRICK 1: Volume of Cone = (1/3) × Cylinder

Same base and height: V_cone = (1/3)V_cylinder.
Always remember this ratio: Cone : Cylinder = **1:3**

---

### 🚀 TRICK 2: Sphere to Cylinder Ratio (Archimedes' Result)

When sphere fits exactly in cylinder (h = 2r):
$$V_{sphere} : V_{cylinder} = 2:3$$
$$SA_{sphere} : CSA_{cylinder} = 2:3 \quad \text{(wait: } 4\pi r^2 : 2\pi r \times 2r = 4\pi r^2 : 4\pi r^2 = 1:1)$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK 3: Number of Small Spheres from Big Sphere

$$n = \left(\frac{R}{r}\right)^3$$

If radii ratio = k:1, then number = k³.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 4: Number of Small Cubes from Big Cube

$$n = \left(\frac{a}{b}\right)^3$$

Where a = big cube side, b = small cube side.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 5: Painted Cube Shortcut

For a cube of side n cut into unit cubes:
- **3 faces painted (corners):** Always **8**
- **2 faces painted (edges, not corners):** 12(n−2)
- **1 face painted (face centre):** 6(n−2)²
- **0 faces painted (inner):** (n−2)³

---

### 🚀 TRICK 6: Diagonal of Cube = a√3

Space diagonal of cube = side × √3.
Face diagonal = side × √2.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 7: CSA of Cylinder = Rectangle Unrolled

If you unroll a cylinder, you get a rectangle of dimensions (2πr) × h.
CSA = 2πrh (the rectangle area).

---

### 🚀 TRICK 8: Wire from Sphere — Volume Preserved

$$\frac{4}{3}\pi R^3 = \pi r^2 L \Rightarrow L = \frac{4R^3}{3r^2}$$

Where R = sphere radius, r = wire radius, L = wire length.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 9: % Change in Volume When Dimension Changes

If radius of sphere changes by x%:
Volume changes by (3x + 3x²/100 + x³/10000)% ≈ **3x%** (approx for small x)

If all sides of cuboid change by x%:
Volume changes by approximately **3x%**.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 10: Hemisphere TSA = 3πr²

Don't forget: TSA includes the flat circular base too!
- CSA of hemisphere = 2πr²
- Flat base = πr²
- TSA = 3πr²

---

### 🚀 TRICK 11: Slant Height of Cone — Pythagoras

l = √(r² + h²). This is just Pythagoras on the right triangle formed by r, h, l.
- If r = 3, h = 4 → l = 5
- If r = 5, h = 12 → l = 13

Use Pythagorean triplets for quick answers!

---

### 🚀 TRICK 12: Volume Preserved = Key to All Melting Problems

Always write: V₁ = V₂. Then substitute formulas and solve.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 13: Water Rise Formula

$$h_{rise} = \frac{V_{object}}{A_{base}}$$

Where A_base = πr² for cylindrical container.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 14: Cylinder from Rectangle

When a rectangle (l × b) is rolled:
- Along length l → Circumference = l → r = l/(2π), height = b
- Along breadth b → Circumference = b → r = b/(2π), height = l

---

### 🚀 TRICK 15: Frustum Slant Height — Don't Confuse with Cone Slant

Frustum l = √[h² + (R−r)²] ← uses difference of radii, NOT just r or R.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 16: Quick TSA of Common Shapes

| Shape | TSA Quick Formula |
|-------|-----------------|
| Cube (a) | 6a² |
| Cylinder (r,h) | 2πr(r+h) |
| Sphere (r) | 4πr² |
| Hemisphere (r) | 3πr² |
| Cone (r,l) | πr(r+l) |

---

### 🚀 TRICK 17: Inversion Ratio — Cylinder, Cone, Sphere Same Radius and Height

Fill ratios in a cylinder:
$$V_{cone} : V_{hemisphere} : V_{cylinder} = 1 : 1 : \frac{3}{2}$$

More precisely for same r, with h = r for cone/hemisphere:
$$\frac{1}{3}\pi r^3 : \frac{2}{3}\pi r^3 : \pi r^3 = 1:2:3$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK 18: Surface Area Ratio (Same Shape)

If linear dimension scaled by k:
- LSA scales by k²
- Volume scales by k³

## 15. COMMON MISTAKE ALERTS

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 1: Cone Volume Missing the 1/3

**Wrong:** V = πr²h
**Right:** V = **(1/3)**πr²h

The 1/3 in cone volume is the most commonly forgotten factor.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 2: Hemisphere TSA — Missing the Flat Base

**Wrong:** TSA = 2πr²
**Right:** TSA = 2πr² + πr² = **3πr²**

CSA = 2πr² (only curved). TSA = 3πr² (curved + flat circular base).

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 3: Slant Height vs Height in Cone

**Height (h):** Vertical distance from apex to base centre.
**Slant height (l):** Distance along the slant surface from apex to base edge.

l = √(r² + h²). Using h where l is needed (or vice versa) in CSA = πrl gives wrong answer.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 4: Cylinder TSA — Forgetting Both Circular Ends

**Wrong:** TSA = CSA + πr² (only one base)
**Right:** TSA = CSA + **2**πr² = 2πrh + 2πr²

A cylinder has TWO circular ends (top and bottom).

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 5: Frustum — Not Using (R−r) for Slant Height

**Wrong:** l = √(h² + R²)
**Right:** l = √(h² + **(R−r)**²)

The slant height of frustum uses the DIFFERENCE of radii.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 6: Volume Units — Not Cubing

If dimensions are in cm, volume is in **cm³** (cubic centimetres).
Perimeter → cm, Area → cm², Volume → **cm³**.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 7: Composite Shapes — Counting Hidden Surfaces

When two solids are joined, the surfaces touching each other are NOT visible.
**Do NOT** include internal/joined surfaces in TSA calculation.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 8: Melting — Not Equating Volumes Correctly

"Melted and recast" → Volume is conserved.
**Wrong:** Setting surface areas equal.
**Right:** Setting **volumes** equal: V₁ = V₂.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 9: Painted Cube — Off-By-One Error

For a cube of side n units cut into unit cubes:
Inner cubes = **(n−2)³** NOT n³ − something vague.
Edges (2 painted) = **12(n−2)** NOT 12(n−1).

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 10: Water Level Rise — Using Wrong Container Area

Rise in water level = V_object / Area_of_container_base.
**Always use the container's cross-sectional area, NOT the object's.**

| Problem Type | Key Approach |
|-------------|-------------|
| Melting and recasting | Equate volumes: V₁ = V₂ |

| Water level rise | h_rise = V_object / Area_base |

| Frustum slant height | l = √(h²+(R-r)²) |
| Wire from sphere | L = 4R³/(3r²) |
| Composite shapes volume | Add all component volumes |
| Composite shapes SA | Add only VISIBLE surfaces |

## 🏆 TOP EXAM TIPS

1. **Cone volume = (1/3)πr²h** — the 1/3 is the #1 forgotten factor. Write it first before anything else.
2. **Hemisphere TSA = 3πr²** (not 2πr²) — the flat circular base MUST be included.
3. **Slant height of cone** = √(r²+h²). Use Pythagorean triplets (3-4-5, 5-12-13) for instant answers.
4. **Melting problems**: ALWAYS equate volumes. Never equate surface areas.
5. **Composite solids**: Add volumes of all parts. For surface area, subtract hidden/internal faces.
6. **Painted cube**: Corners = 8 (always!). Then use the formulas with (n-2).
7. **Water rise problems**: V_object = π × r_container² × h_rise. Isolate h_rise.
8. **Volume units** — check if answer needs to be in cm³, litres, or m³. Convert accordingly.
9. **Frustum slant height** uses (R-r), not just R or r alone. Most common calculation error.
10. **For "how many small from big"** — just divide volumes. The π cancels, making calculation clean.
