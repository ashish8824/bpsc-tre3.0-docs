## 1. CORE DEFINITIONS & TERMINOLOGY

---

### 💰 Cost Price (CP)
The price at which an article is **purchased** (or manufactured).

> Includes: purchase price + all overhead costs (transport, labour, etc.) unless stated otherwise.

---

### 💵 Selling Price (SP)
The price at which an article is **sold**.

---

### 📈 Profit (Gain)
When SP > CP → **Profit = SP − CP**

---

### 📉 Loss
When SP < CP → **Loss = CP − SP**

---

### 🏷️ Marked Price (MP) / List Price
The price **printed/tagged** on the article before any discount.

> Also called: **MRP (Maximum Retail Price)** in everyday language.

---

### 🎫 Discount
A reduction given on the **Marked Price**.

> Discount is always calculated on **Marked Price**, NEVER on Cost Price.

$$\text{Discount} = \text{MP} - \text{SP}$$

---

### 🧮 Profit% and Loss%
**Always calculated on Cost Price (CP).**

$$\text{Profit\%} = \frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{CP}} \times 100$$

$$\text{Loss\%} = \frac{\text{Loss}}{\text{CP}} \times 100$$

---

### 📊 Discount%
**Always calculated on Marked Price (MP).**

$$\text{Discount\%} = \frac{\text{Discount}}{\text{MP}} \times 100$$

---

## 2. FUNDAMENTAL FORMULAS

---

### Group 1: Basic Relationships

| Formula | Expression |
|---------|-----------|
| Profit | SP − CP |
| Loss | CP − SP |
| SP (with profit) | CP × (1 + Profit%/100) |
| SP (with loss) | CP × (1 − Loss%/100) |
| CP (from profit) | SP / (1 + Profit%/100) |
| CP (from loss) | SP / (1 − Loss%/100) |
| Profit% | (Profit/CP) × 100 |
| Loss% | (Loss/CP) × 100 |

---

### Group 2: Marked Price & Discount

| Formula | Expression |
|---------|-----------|
| SP after discount | MP × (1 − Discount%/100) |
| MP (when SP & discount% known) | SP / (1 − Discount%/100) |
| Discount | MP − SP |
| Discount% | (Discount/MP) × 100 |

---

### Group 3: The Combined Formula (Most Important!)

$$\text{SP} = \text{CP} \times \frac{(100 + \text{Profit\%})}{100}$$

$$\text{SP} = \text{MP} \times \frac{(100 - \text{Discount\%})}{100}$$

$$\text{CP} \times \frac{(100 + \text{Profit\%})}{100} = \text{MP} \times \frac{(100 - \text{Discount\%})}{100}$$

> 🔑 **This combined formula is the key to most exam questions!**

---

### Group 4: Multiplying Factor (MF) — The Speed Shortcut

Instead of percentage calculations, use **Multiplying Factor**:

| Situation | Multiplying Factor |
|-----------|-------------------|
| 20% profit | 1.20 |
| 20% loss | 0.80 |
| 15% discount | 0.85 |
| 10% increase then 10% decrease | 1.10 × 0.90 = 0.99 |

> **SP = CP × MF** (profit) or **SP = MP × MF** (discount)

## 3. PROFIT & LOSS EXAMPLES

---

### ─── BASIC LEVEL ───

#### Example 1: Simple Profit Calculation

> A man buys a watch for ₹1200 and sells it for ₹1500. Find profit and profit%.

**Solution:**
- Profit = SP − CP = 1500 − 1200 = **₹300**
- Profit% = (300/1200) × 100 = **25%**

---

#### Example 2: Simple Loss Calculation

> A bike is purchased for ₹45,000 and sold for ₹36,000. Find loss%.

**Solution:**
- Loss = 45000 − 36000 = ₹9000
- Loss% = (9000/45000) × 100 = **20%**

---

#### Example 3: Find SP given CP and Profit%

> CP of an article = ₹800. Sell at 15% profit. Find SP.

**Solution:**
$$\text{SP} = 800 \times \frac{115}{100} = 800 \times 1.15 = ₹920$$

---

#### Example 4: Find CP given SP and Profit%

> A book is sold for ₹364 at a profit of 40%. Find CP.

**Solution:**
$$\text{CP} = \frac{364}{1.40} = ₹260$$

---

#### Example 5: Find CP given SP and Loss%

> A shirt is sold for ₹510 at a loss of 15%. Find CP.

**Solution:**
$$\text{CP} = \frac{510}{0.85} = ₹600$$

---

### ─── INTERMEDIATE LEVEL ───

#### Example 6: Two Items, Find Overall Profit/Loss

> A trader buys two articles for ₹5000 each. He sells one at 20% profit and the other at 20% loss. What is his overall profit or loss?

**Solution:**
- SP₁ = 5000 × 1.20 = ₹6000
- SP₂ = 5000 × 0.80 = ₹4000
- Total CP = ₹10,000 | Total SP = ₹10,000
- **Neither profit nor loss (0%)**

> ⚠️ When EQUAL CP items are sold at equal % profit and loss → **Always 0% net result**

---

#### Example 7: Two Items at Same SP — Classic Trap!

> Two articles are each sold for ₹1200. On one, 20% profit is made; on the other, 20% loss. Find overall profit or loss%.

**Solution:**
- CP₁ = 1200/1.20 = ₹1000 → Profit = ₹200
- CP₂ = 1200/0.80 = ₹1500 → Loss = ₹300
- Total CP = ₹2500, Total SP = ₹2400
- **Net Loss = ₹100**

$$\text{Loss\%} = \frac{100}{2500} \times 100 = 4\%$$

> 🔥 **SHORTCUT:** When same SP, same % profit & loss:
> $$\text{Loss\%} = \frac{(\text{common \%})^2}{100} = \frac{20^2}{100} = 4\%$$
> **Always a LOSS!**

---

#### Example 8: Profit on CP = x% of SP

> A man sells goods at a profit of 20% on his selling price. Find profit% on CP.

**Solution:**

Let SP = 100. Profit = 20% of SP = 20.
So CP = 100 − 20 = 80.

$$\text{Profit\% on CP} = \frac{20}{80} \times 100 = 25\%$$

---

#### Example 9: By What % Should SP be Increased?

> A shopkeeper sells at 10% loss. By what % should he increase his SP to gain 5%?

**Solution:**

Let CP = 100.
- Current SP = 90 (10% loss)
- Required SP = 105 (5% profit)
- Increase needed = 105 − 90 = 15

$$\text{Required increase\%} = \frac{15}{90} \times 100 = 16.\overline{6}\% ≈ 16.67\%$$

---

#### Example 10: Cost After Successive Price Changes

> CP of an item increases by 20%. By how much % must a shopkeeper reduce his selling so that profit% remains the same (currently 25%)?

**Solution:**

Let original CP = 100, SP = 125 (25% profit).
New CP = 120.
To maintain 25% profit: New SP = 120 × 1.25 = 150.
But original SP = 125. So SP must **increase** to 150.

> (The question assumes the shopkeeper cannot raise price — then he must absorb the loss.)

If SP stays 125: New profit% = (125−120)/120 × 100 = 5/120 × 100 = **4.17%**

---

### ─── ADVANCED LEVEL ───

#### Example 11: By Selling 33 Items, Gain = CP of 11 Items

> By selling 33 articles, a trader gains the CP of 11 articles. Find profit%.

**Solution:**

Let CP of 1 article = ₹1.
Total CP of 33 = ₹33.
Profit = CP of 11 = ₹11.
SP of 33 = 33 + 11 = ₹44.

$$\text{Profit\%} = \frac{11}{33} \times 100 = 33.\overline{3}\% ≈ 33.33\%$$

---

#### Example 12: If SP of x articles = CP of y articles

> SP of 8 oranges = CP of 10 oranges. Find profit%.

**Solution:**

Let CP of 1 orange = ₹1.
CP of 10 = ₹10 = SP of 8.
So SP of 1 orange = 10/8 = ₹1.25.
Profit per orange = 1.25 − 1 = ₹0.25.

$$\text{Profit\%} = \frac{0.25}{1} \times 100 = 25\%$$

> 🔥 **SHORTCUT:** Profit% = $\frac{y - x}{x} \times 100 = \frac{10-8}{8} \times 100 = 25\%$

---

#### Example 13: Selling Part at Profit, Part at Loss

> A man buys 100 apples at ₹10 each. He sells 60 at 20% profit and the rest at 10% loss. Find overall profit or loss%.

**Solution:**

- Total CP = 100 × 10 = ₹1000
- SP of 60 = 60 × 10 × 1.20 = ₹720
- SP of 40 = 40 × 10 × 0.90 = ₹360
- Total SP = ₹1080
- Profit = ₹80

$$\text{Profit\%} = \frac{80}{1000} \times 100 = 8\%$$

---

#### Example 14: Find SP for Desired Profit after a Loss

> A merchant loses 10% by selling at ₹450. At what price should he sell to gain 10%?

**Solution:**

CP = 450/0.90 = ₹500.
Required SP = 500 × 1.10 = **₹550**

## 4. DISCOUNT CONCEPTS & EXAMPLES

---

### Core Relationship:

```
MP  →  Apply Discount%  →  SP  →  Compare with CP  →  Profit/Loss
```

---

#### Example 15: Simple Discount

> MP = ₹500, Discount = 10%. Find SP.

$$\text{SP} = 500 \times 0.90 = ₹450$$

---

#### Example 16: Find MP given SP and Discount%

> A shirt is sold for ₹680 after 15% discount. Find MP.

$$\text{MP} = \frac{680}{0.85} = ₹800$$

---

#### Example 17: Find Profit% after Discount

> CP = ₹400, MP = ₹600, Discount = 20%. Find profit%.

**Solution:**
- SP = 600 × 0.80 = ₹480
- Profit = 480 − 400 = ₹80
- Profit% = (80/400) × 100 = **20%**

---

#### Example 18: Find MP to Allow Discount and Still Make Profit

> A shopkeeper wants 25% profit after giving 10% discount. If CP = ₹720, find MP.

**Solution:**

Required SP = 720 × 1.25 = ₹900.
MP × 0.90 = 900.

$$\text{MP} = \frac{900}{0.90} = ₹1000$$

---

#### Example 19: Find Discount% given CP, MP, and Profit%

> CP = ₹500, MP = ₹700, Profit = 12%. Find discount%.

**Solution:**

SP = 500 × 1.12 = ₹560.
Discount = 700 − 560 = ₹140.

$$\text{Discount\%} = \frac{140}{700} \times 100 = 20\%$$

---

## 5. SUCCESSIVE DISCOUNTS

---

### What is it?
When **two or more discounts** are applied one after another on the reduced price.

> ⚠️ **Successive discounts are NOT additive!**
> 10% + 10% ≠ 20% discount

### Formula:

For discounts d₁% and d₂%:

$$\text{Net Discount\%} = d_1 + d_2 - \frac{d_1 \times d_2}{100}$$

For three discounts:

$$\text{Final SP} = \text{MP} \times \frac{(100-d_1)}{100} \times \frac{(100-d_2)}{100} \times \frac{(100-d_3)}{100}$$

---

#### Example 20: Two Successive Discounts

> MP = ₹2000. Successive discounts of 20% and 10%. Find SP.

**Method 1 (Step by Step):**
- After 1st discount: 2000 × 0.80 = ₹1600
- After 2nd discount: 1600 × 0.90 = **₹1440**

**Method 2 (Formula):**
Net discount = 20 + 10 − (20×10)/100 = 30 − 2 = **28%**
SP = 2000 × 0.72 = **₹1440** ✓

---

#### Example 21: Three Successive Discounts

> MP = ₹5000. Discounts of 10%, 20%, 25%. Find SP.

$$\text{SP} = 5000 \times 0.90 \times 0.80 \times 0.75$$
$$= 5000 \times 0.54 = ₹2700$$

---

#### Example 22: Single Equivalent Discount

> Two successive discounts of 30% and 20%. Find single equivalent discount.

$$\text{Equivalent Discount} = 30 + 20 - \frac{30 \times 20}{100} = 50 - 6 = 44\%$$

---

#### Example 23: Which is better — 40% or 25% + 20%?

**Option A:** 40% off → Pay 60% of MP
**Option B:** 25% then 20% → Pay 75% × 80% = 60% of MP

> **Both are exactly the same!**

---

## 6. MARKED PRICE PROBLEMS

---

### The Master Formula:

$$\frac{\text{MP}}{\text{CP}} = \frac{100 + \text{Profit\%}}{100 - \text{Discount\%}}$$

---

#### Example 24: Find the Ratio MP:CP

> A shopkeeper marks 40% above CP and gives 15% discount. Find MP:CP and profit%.

**Solution:**

MP = 1.40 × CP → MP/CP = 7/5

SP = MP × 0.85 = 1.40 × 0.85 × CP = 1.19 × CP

Profit% = **19%**

---

#### Example 25: What % above CP to Mark for Target Profit after Discount?

> To gain 20% after 20% discount, by what % above CP should MP be marked?

**Solution:**

SP = CP × 1.20 (to gain 20%)
SP = MP × 0.80 (after 20% discount)

$$\text{MP} = \frac{1.20 \times \text{CP}}{0.80} = 1.50 \times \text{CP}$$

Mark **50% above CP**.

---

#### Example 26: Using the Master Formula

> Discount = 16%, Profit = 26%. Find the % above CP the article was marked.

$$\frac{\text{MP}}{\text{CP}} = \frac{100 + 26}{100 - 16} = \frac{126}{84} = \frac{3}{2} = 1.50$$

MP is **50% above CP**.

---

## 7. DISHONEST DEALER / FALSE WEIGHT PROBLEMS

---

### Scenario:
A dishonest dealer claims to sell at CP but uses a **false weight** (sells less quantity).

### Formula:

$$\text{Profit\%} = \frac{\text{True Weight} - \text{False Weight}}{\text{False Weight}} \times 100$$

Or equivalently:

$$\text{Profit\%} = \frac{\text{Error}}{\text{True Value} - \text{Error}} \times 100$$

---

#### Example 27: Basic False Weight

> A dishonest dealer claims to sell goods at CP but uses 900g weight instead of 1000g. Find profit%.

**Solution:**
$$\text{Profit\%} = \frac{1000 - 900}{900} \times 100 = \frac{100}{900} \times 100 = 11.11\%$$

---

#### Example 28: False Weight + Markup

> A shopkeeper marks 25% above CP and gives 10% discount, but uses a 800g weight instead of 1000g. Find true profit%.

**Solution:**

Let CP of 1000g = ₹100. He actually gives only 800g, so cost to him = ₹80.
He sells at: 100 × 1.25 × 0.90 = ₹112.50 (for what he claims is 1000g).

Profit = 112.50 − 80 = ₹32.50

$$\text{Profit\%} = \frac{32.50}{80} \times 100 = 40.625\%$$

---

#### Example 29: Both Buy and Sell False

> A trader uses 900g while buying (claims 1000g paid) and 1100g while selling (claims 1000g received). Find his profit%.

**Solution:**

He buys 1100g paying for 1000g (buys more).
He sells 900g receiving money for 1000g (sells less).

Effective: Buys 1100g, sells 900g.

If CP of 1000g = ₹100:
- Pays for 1000g = ₹100, gets 1100g
- CP per gram = 100/1100

Sells 900g as 1000g → receives ₹100
Actual cost of 900g = 900 × (100/1100) = 900/11 × 10 = ₹81.82

Profit% = (100 − 81.82)/81.82 × 100 ≈ **22.2%**

> **Quick Formula:** Profit% = [(1100×1000 − 900×1000)/(900×1000)] × 100
> = (1100−900)/900 × 100 = 200/900 × 100 = **22.22%**

---

## 8. CP & SP WITH OVERHEAD / ADDITIONAL COSTS

---

### Rule: Overhead costs are ADDED to CP.

$$\text{Effective CP} = \text{Purchase Price} + \text{Overhead Costs}$$

---

#### Example 30: Transport Costs

> A man buys 10 kg of sugar at ₹30/kg. He spends ₹50 on transport. If he sells all at ₹35/kg, find profit%.

**Solution:**
- Purchase cost = 10 × 30 = ₹300
- Overhead = ₹50
- Total CP = ₹350
- SP = 10 × 35 = ₹350
- **Profit = 0 (No profit, No loss)**

---

#### Example 31: Manufacturing Cost

> A manufacturer spends ₹1500 on raw material, ₹800 on labour, and ₹200 on overhead per unit. He sells at ₹3000. Find profit%.

**Solution:**
- Total CP = 1500 + 800 + 200 = ₹2500
- SP = ₹3000
- Profit% = (500/2500) × 100 = **20%**

---

## 9. SELLING MULTIPLE ITEMS

---

### Scenario: Same SP, different profit% and loss%

> ⚠️ **GOLDEN RULE:** When two items have the SAME SP, and one earns x% profit while other earns x% loss — there is **ALWAYS a net loss.**

$$\text{Net Loss\%} = \frac{x^2}{100}$$

---

#### Example 32: Classic Same-SP Problem

> A man sold two horses at ₹5000 each. On one he gains 25% and on another he loses 25%. Find overall profit or loss.

**Solution:**

- CP₁ = 5000/1.25 = ₹4000
- CP₂ = 5000/0.75 = ₹6666.67
- Total CP = ₹10666.67
- Total SP = ₹10000
- Loss = ₹666.67

$$\text{Loss\%} = \frac{25^2}{100} = \frac{625}{100} = 6.25\%$$

---

### Scenario: Buying N items for price of M, or Selling

#### Example 33: Buy 5, Get 1 Free

> A shopkeeper gives one article free for every 5 articles purchased. What is his effective discount%?

**Solution:**

Customer pays for 5, gets 6.
Discount = price of 1 article out of 6.

$$\text{Discount\%} = \frac{1}{6} \times 100 = 16.67\%$$

---

#### Example 34: Selling More/Less for the Quoted Price

> A vendor buys 12 oranges for ₹1 and sells 9 oranges for ₹1. Find profit%.

**Solution:**

Let's compute per orange:
- CP per orange = 1/12
- SP per orange = 1/9

$$\text{Profit\%} = \frac{\frac{1}{9} - \frac{1}{12}}{\frac{1}{12}} \times 100 = \frac{\frac{4-3}{36}}{\frac{1}{12}} \times 100 = \frac{\frac{1}{36}}{\frac{1}{12}} \times 100 = \frac{12}{36} \times 100 = 33.33\%$$

---

## 10. QUICK TRICKS & SHORTCUTS

---

### 🚀 TRICK 1: The Multiplying Factor Method

Avoid lengthy formula — just multiply:

| Profit% | Multiply CP by | Loss% | Multiply CP by |
|---------|---------------|-------|---------------|
| 10% | 1.10 | 10% | 0.90 |
| 20% | 1.20 | 20% | 0.80 |
| 25% | 1.25 | 25% | 0.75 |
| 33.33% | 4/3 | 33.33% | 2/3 |
| 50% | 1.50 | 50% | 0.50 |

---

### 🚀 TRICK 2: Same SP — Always Loss Formula

$$\text{Net Loss\%} = \frac{x^2}{100}$$

Where x = common profit% = loss%

---

### 🚀 TRICK 3: SP of x = CP of y → Profit%

$$\text{Profit\%} = \frac{y - x}{x} \times 100$$

$$\text{Loss\%} = \frac{x - y}{x} \times 100$$

(Use profit if y > x, loss if y < x)

---

### 🚀 TRICK 4: Gain on Selling = CP of N Items

> If by selling x items, gain = CP of y items:
$$\text{Profit\%} = \frac{y}{x} \times 100$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK 5: Master Ratio Formula

$$\frac{\text{MP}}{\text{CP}} = \frac{100 + \text{Profit\%}}{100 - \text{Discount\%}}$$

**Find any unknown when 3 of the 4 values are given.**

---

### 🚀 TRICK 6: False Weight Profit

$$\text{Profit\%} = \frac{\text{Error}}{(\text{True Weight}) - \text{Error}} \times 100$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK 7: Successive Discount Equivalent

$$\text{Net Discount\%} = a + b - \frac{ab}{100}$$

---

### 🚀 TRICK 8: Finding SP when Two Conditions are Given

If selling at price A gives x% profit and price B gives y% loss:

$$\frac{A}{B} = \frac{100 + x}{100 - y}$$

Use this to find CP directly.

---

### 🚀 TRICK 9: Discount on Discount ≠ Sum of Discounts

20% + 10% discount ≠ 30% discount
Actual = 20 + 10 − (20×10/100) = **28% discount**

---

### 🚀 TRICK 10: Profit% on SP → Profit% on CP

If profit = x% of SP:
$$\text{Profit\% on CP} = \frac{x}{100 - x} \times 100$$

Example: Profit = 20% of SP → Profit on CP = 20/80 × 100 = **25%**

---

### 🚀 TRICK 11: Quick Check for Marked Price

**Mark up % above CP** to maintain profit after discount:

$$\text{Mark up\%} = \frac{100 + \text{Profit\%}}{100 - \text{Discount\%}} - 1 \times 100$$

Example: Profit 20%, Discount 20%:
MP/CP = 120/80 = 1.50 → Mark up = **50%**

---

### 🚀 TRICK 12: Cashback vs Discount

If an item has **10% discount** vs **10% cashback on SP**:
- Discount of 10%: Pay 90% of MP upfront ✅ Better
- Cashback of 10% on SP: Pay SP, get 10% back = effectively 90% of SP, but SP may already = MP

> **Discount on MP is always better than same % cashback on SP!**

---

### 🚀 TRICK 13: Finding CP when Profit% and Loss% are Given for Two Conditions

> If article sold at ₹A → x% profit; sold at ₹B → y% loss:
$$\text{CP} = \frac{100A + 100B}{(100+x) + (100-y)} \cdot \text{(varies)}$$

Better to use: CP × (1 + x/100) = A and solve directly.

---

## 11. COMMON MISTAKE ALERTS

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 1: Profit% on SP Instead of CP

**Wrong:** Profit% = (Profit/SP) × 100
**Right:** Profit% = (Profit/CP) × 100

> Unless the question **explicitly** says "profit on selling price."

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 2: Discount% on CP Instead of MP

**Wrong:** Discount% = (Discount/CP) × 100
**Right:** Discount% = (Discount/MP) × 100

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 3: Adding Successive Discounts

**Wrong:** 20% + 10% = 30% net discount
**Right:** 20% + 10% − 2% = 28% net discount

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 4: Same SP Same % → Assuming No Net Effect

When same SP with equal profit% and loss% → Always NET LOSS of x²/100 %.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 5: Forgetting Overhead in CP

Always add transport, tax, repair costs to CP before calculating profit%.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 6: Finding MP When Only Profit% and Discount% Are Given

You CANNOT find MP in absolute value without knowing CP or SP.
You CAN find the **ratio** MP:CP using the master formula.

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 7: False Weight Formula Confusion

$$\text{Profit\%} = \frac{\text{True} - \text{False}}{\text{False}} \times 100 \quad \text{(denominator = FALSE weight)}$$

Many students accidentally put True weight in denominator — that's WRONG.

Profit% = (SP − CP)/CP × 100:
He gets 900g worth of money = ₹100. His cost for 900g = 900×(100/1100) = 900/11 × 100/100 = ₹81.82
Profit = ₹18.18 on ₹81.82.

**Short formula:** Profit% = [(1+10/100)/(1−10/100) − 1] × 100 = (1.1/0.9 − 1) × 100 = (0.2/0.9) × 100 ≈ **22.22%**

</details>

**Q19.** A trader uses a weighing balance that shows 1200g when actual weight is 1000g (he buys using this balance) and shows 800g when actual weight is 1000g (he sells using this). He buys at ₹5/kg and sells at ₹6/kg. Find his profit%.

<details>
<summary>📖 Solution</summary>

While buying: He pays for 1000g but gets 1200g (balance reads 1000g when actual = 1200g).
While selling: He charges for 1000g but gives only 800g.

Let CP per actual gram = 5/1000 (since he's paying ₹5 per kg).

For ₹5 he gets 1200g (not 1000g).
Cost per actual gram = 5/1200.

He charges ₹6 per kg (1000g label), but actually gives 800g.
Revenue per 800g = 6 × (800/1000) = ₹4.80

But let's compute per deal:
- Pay ₹5 → get 1200g
- Sell 1200g → charges for 1200/800 × 1000g at ₹6/kg = 1200/800 × 6 = ₹9

Profit = 9 − 5 = ₹4. Profit% = (4/5) × 100 = **80%**

</details>

**Q25.** A dealer marks his goods at x% above CP and allows y% discount. If he makes a profit of z%, find the relationship.

<details>
<summary>📖 Solution</summary>

This is the **general form** of the master formula:

$$\frac{(100+x)}{100} \times \frac{(100-y)}{100} = \frac{(100+z)}{100}$$

$$(100+x)(100-y) = 100(100+z)$$

For example: x=40%, y=20%, then:
(140)(80)/100 = 112 → profit = 12%. (**z = 12%**)

</details>

## 🏆 TOP EXAM TIPS

1. **Always identify what is given** — is it CP, SP, or MP? Start from there.
2. **For discount problems** — remember discount is always on MP, never CP.
3. **For profit%** — always on CP, unless question says "on SP."
4. **When two items are sold at the same SP** with equal profit and loss — net result is always a LOSS = x²/100%.
5. **Use the Master Ratio Formula** (MP/CP) to solve most marked-price questions in one step.
6. **False weight**: denominator is ALWAYS the false weight (what the cheat actually uses).
7. **Successive discounts** — multiply factors; do not add percentages.
8. **For chain transactions (A sells to B sells to C)** — multiply all factors together.
9. **If "profit on SP" is given**, convert to "profit on CP" using: p/(100−p) × 100.
10. **Read carefully** — "by how much must SP be raised" vs "by what %" are different questions.
