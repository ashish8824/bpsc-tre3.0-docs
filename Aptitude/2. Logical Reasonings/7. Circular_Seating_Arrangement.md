## 1. THE BIG PICTURE — WHAT IS CIRCULAR SEATING?

**Circular Seating Arrangement** questions involve people sitting around a circular table or in a circular formation. You must determine the exact position of each person relative to the others.

> **Golden Rule #1:** In circular arrangements, there is NO fixed start point. Always FIX one person and arrange others relative to them.

> **Golden Rule #2:** Left and right depend entirely on which direction a person FACES. This changes everything compared to linear.

## 2. HOW CIRCULAR DIFFERS FROM LINEAR

---

### Key Differences Table:

```
┌─────────────────────┬──────────────────────┬──────────────────────┐
│ ASPECT              │ LINEAR               │ CIRCULAR             │
├─────────────────────┼──────────────────────┼──────────────────────┤
│ Fixed reference     │ Left end = position1 │ No fixed start point │
│                     │ Right end = positionN│ (circular, no ends)  │
├─────────────────────┼──────────────────────┼──────────────────────┤
│ Total arrangements  │ N! ways              │ (N−1)! ways          │
│                     │                      │ (one position fixed) │
├─────────────────────┼──────────────────────┼──────────────────────┤
│ Left/Right          │ Always fixed         │ Depends on FACING    │
│                     │ (left = lower pos)   │ direction (in/out)   │
├─────────────────────┼──────────────────────┼──────────────────────┤
│ "Between" persons   │ Count linearly       │ Count in SHORTER arc │
│                     │ one way only         │ (can go either way)  │
├─────────────────────┼──────────────────────┼──────────────────────┤
│ Solving method      │ Fill positions 1→N   │ Fix one person, then │
│                     │                      │ place others around  │
├─────────────────────┼──────────────────────┼──────────────────────┤
│ Ends                │ Two ends exist       │ No ends (wraps)      │
└─────────────────────┴──────────────────────┴──────────────────────┘
```

## 3. LEFT & RIGHT RULES — THE MOST CRITICAL CONCEPT

> This is where 90% of mistakes happen. Master this section completely.

---

### Facing INWARD (Toward Centre) — Standard:

```
When a person FACES THE CENTRE (most common exam type):

                    [A]
                   /   \
               [F]       [B]
               |    ●    |
               [E]       [C]
                   \   /
                    [D]

A faces the centre (↓ toward centre).
A's LEFT  = clockwise direction   = B's side
A's RIGHT = anti-clockwise direction = F's side

MEMORY AID for INWARD facing:
"When facing inward: LEFT = CLOCKWISE, RIGHT = ANTI-CLOCKWISE"

More precisely: imagine YOU are sitting at position A, facing the centre.
Your left hand points CLOCKWISE (to B).
Your right hand points ANTI-CLOCKWISE (to F).
```

---

### Facing OUTWARD (Away from Centre):

```
When a person FACES OUTSIDE (away from centre):

                    [A] ← faces outward (up, away from centre)
                   /   \
               [F]       [B]
               |    ●    |
               [E]       [C]
                   \   /
                    [D]

A faces outside (↑ away from centre).
A's LEFT  = anti-clockwise direction = F's side
A's RIGHT = clockwise direction   = B's side

MEMORY AID for OUTWARD facing:
"When facing outward: LEFT = ANTI-CLOCKWISE, RIGHT = CLOCKWISE"
(Opposite of inward!)
```

---

### The Master Left/Right Rule:

```
╔═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║  FACING INWARD  (toward centre):                              ║
║  Clockwise neighbour     = person to your LEFT                ║
║  Anti-clockwise neighbour = person to your RIGHT              ║
║                                                               ║
║  FACING OUTWARD (away from centre):                           ║
║  Clockwise neighbour     = person to your RIGHT               ║
║  Anti-clockwise neighbour = person to your LEFT               ║
╚═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

SUPER SHORTCUT:
Inward facing  → LEFT  = Clockwise
Outward facing → RIGHT = Clockwise
(Clockwise changes side when facing flips)
```

---

### Why This Works — Visual Proof:

```
Imagine a clock face. You sit AT the 12 position.

FACING INWARD (facing toward 6 o'clock / centre):
  Your left hand → points to 3 o'clock = CLOCKWISE direction ✓
  Your right hand → points to 9 o'clock = ANTI-CLOCKWISE ✓

FACING OUTWARD (facing away from centre, toward 12 direction outside):
  Your left hand → points to 9 o'clock = ANTI-CLOCKWISE ✓
  Your right hand → points to 3 o'clock = CLOCKWISE ✓
```

---

### Practical Application — Position Clue Conversion:

```
Given: 8 persons around a circle (positions 1–8 clockwise).
Person A is at position 3, facing inward.

"B is to the immediate LEFT of A"
→ A faces inward → left = clockwise
→ Clockwise from A(3) = position 4
→ B is at position 4 ✓

"C is to the immediate RIGHT of A"
→ A faces inward → right = anti-clockwise
→ Anti-clockwise from A(3) = position 2
→ C is at position 2 ✓
```

## 4. CLUE LANGUAGE — EVERY TYPE DECODED

---

### 4.1 — Immediate Neighbor Clues:

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────┬─────────────────────────────────┐
│ CLUE                                     │ MEANING                         │
├──────────────────────────────────────────┼─────────────────────────────────┤
│ "B is to the immediate left of A"        │ B is directly next to A,        │
│ (all face inward)                        │ in the CLOCKWISE direction       │
│                                          │ B_pos = A_pos + 1 (clockwise)   │
├──────────────────────────────────────────┼─────────────────────────────────┤
│ "B is to the immediate right of A"       │ B is directly next to A,        │
│ (all face inward)                        │ in ANTI-CLOCKWISE direction      │
│                                          │ B_pos = A_pos - 1 (clockwise)   │
├──────────────────────────────────────────┼─────────────────────────────────┤
│ "A and B are immediate neighbors"        │ |A_pos - B_pos| = 1 in circle   │
│                                          │ (adjacent seats)                │
├──────────────────────────────────────────┼─────────────────────────────────┤
│ "A and B are not neighbors"              │ Not adjacent seats              │
│                                          │ At least 1 person between them  │
└──────────────────────────────────────────┴─────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

### 4.2 — Opposite Position Clues:

```
"A sits exactly opposite B" (in a circle of N persons, N must be EVEN):
→ A and B are separated by N/2 positions.
→ If A is at position X, B is at position X + N/2 (clockwise).

Examples:
6-person circle: opposite = 3 positions away.
8-person circle: opposite = 4 positions away.
10-person circle: opposite = 5 positions away.
12-person circle: opposite = 6 positions away.
```

---

### 4.3 — "Between" Clues in Circular Arrangement:

```
"N persons are sitting between A and B"

In a circle, "between" can mean going EITHER way (clockwise or anti-clockwise).
The clue usually means the SHORTER arc (fewer persons between them).
If equal both ways → N = (Total - 2) / 2 persons between each way.

FORMULA:
Persons between (clockwise arc) = |pos_A - pos_B| - 1 (if going clockwise from A to B)
OR
Persons between (anti-clockwise arc) = Total - |pos_A - pos_B| - 1

When clue says "N between A and B":
The arc with N persons between them = |pos_A - pos_B| = N + 1 (clockwise)
OR Total - (N+1) positions apart (anti-clockwise).
```

---

### 4.4 — "K seats to the left/right" Clues:

```
"B is 2nd to the left of A" (facing inward):
→ Left = clockwise → B is 2 positions clockwise from A
→ B_pos = A_pos + 2 (clockwise)

"B is 3rd to the right of A" (facing inward):
→ Right = anti-clockwise → B is 3 positions anti-clockwise from A
→ B_pos = A_pos - 3 (clockwise numbering)
```

---

### 4.5 — Direction of Facing Clues (Mixed Facing):

```
"A faces the centre" → A is an inward-facing person.
"A faces outside"    → A is an outward-facing person.
"A and B face each other" → They sit opposite AND face each other
                          (one faces inward, other faces outward)
                          OR they face each other across the table.
"A and B face the same direction" → Both inward or both outward.
```

---

### 4.6 — The "Neighbour of Neighbour" Chain:

```
Clue chain example:
"B is to the immediate left of A"
"C is to the immediate left of B"
"D is to the immediate left of C"

→ Going clockwise: A, B, C, D are in consecutive seats!
→ Order clockwise: A → B → C → D (each one step clockwise from previous)

This creates a BLOCK of 4 consecutive persons: [A][B][C][D] clockwise.
```

## 5. THE FIXING METHOD — YOUR PRIMARY WEAPON

> In a circular arrangement, positions have no absolute reference. We fix one person and place all others relative to them.

---

### Why We Fix One Person:

```
In a linear row: Position 1, 2, 3... are fixed reference points.
In a circle: Rotating the entire arrangement gives a "different" solution
             that is actually the same arrangement!

To avoid counting duplicates, we FIX one person at a reference position
(call it "top" or "position 1") and arrange others relative to them.

This reduces the problem from N! to (N-1)! arrangements.
```

---

### The Fixing Method — Step by Step:

```
STEP 1: Count total persons (N).

STEP 2: Draw a circle with N seats.
        Label seats 1, 2, 3... N going CLOCKWISE.
        Mark seat 1 at the TOP.

STEP 3: Find the person who appears in the MOST clues
        (or the one whose exact position is given) → FIX this person at seat 1.

STEP 4: Apply clues to fill in other positions relative to the fixed person.

STEP 5: When stuck, try multiple cases (usually 2 cases: clockwise or anti-clockwise).

STEP 6: Verify ALL clues against final arrangement.
```

---

### Standard Circle Diagram for 6 Persons:

```
        [Pos 1] ← Fix one person here (at top)
       /        \
[Pos 6]          [Pos 2]
  |      CENTRE    |
[Pos 5]          [Pos 3]
       \        /
        [Pos 4]

Positions 1→2→3→4→5→6→1 go CLOCKWISE.
Positions 1→6→5→4→3→2→1 go ANTI-CLOCKWISE.
```

### Standard Circle Diagram for 8 Persons:


## 6. TYPE 1 — ALL FACE CENTRE (INWARD)

> Most common type. Everyone faces the centre of the table.

---

### Complete Worked Example 1 — 6 Persons, Face Centre:

```
PROBLEM:
Six persons A, B, C, D, E, F sit around a circular table, all facing the centre.
(i)   A sits immediately to the right of B.
(ii)  D sits 2nd to the left of A.
(iii) C and F are immediate neighbours of each other.
(iv)  F sits 3rd to the right of D.
(v)   E is not an immediate neighbour of A.

Find the arrangement.

SOLUTION:

Step 1: Draw a 6-seat circle. Fix A at position 1 (top).

              [A=1]
           /         \
        [6]             [2]
        |      CENTRE    |
        [5]             [3]
           \         /
              [4]

All face INWARD.
Inward facing: LEFT = clockwise, RIGHT = anti-clockwise.

Step 2: Clue (i): A sits immediately to the RIGHT of B.
"Right of B" (inward) = anti-clockwise from B.
So A is anti-clockwise from B = B is clockwise from A.
B is 1 step CLOCKWISE from A → B at position 2.

              [A=1]
           /         \
        [6]             [B=2]
        |      CENTRE    |
        [5]             [3]
           \         /
              [4]

Step 3: Clue (ii): D sits 2nd to the LEFT of A.
"Left of A" (inward) = clockwise from A.
D is 2 steps clockwise from A → D at position 3.

              [A=1]
           /         \
        [6]             [B=2]
        |      CENTRE    |
        [5]             [D=3]
           \         /
              [4]

Step 4: Clue (iv): F sits 3rd to the RIGHT of D.
"Right of D" (inward) = anti-clockwise from D.
F is 3 steps anti-clockwise from D(3):
Position 3 → anti-clockwise → 2 → 1 → 6
F at position 6.

              [A=1]
           /         \
        [F=6]           [B=2]
        |      CENTRE    |
        [5]             [D=3]
           \         /
              [4]

Step 5: Clue (iii): C and F are immediate neighbours.
F at position 6. Neighbours of 6 = positions 5 and 1(=A).
Position 1 = A (taken). So C at position 5.

              [A=1]
           /         \
        [F=6]           [B=2]
        |      CENTRE    |
        [C=5]           [D=3]
           \         /
              [4]

Step 6: Remaining person: E at position 4.

              [A=1]
           /         \
        [F=6]           [B=2]
        |      CENTRE    |
        [C=5]           [D=3]
           \         /
              [E=4]

Step 7: Clue (v): E not immediate neighbour of A.
E at 4. A at 1. Neighbours of A(1) = positions 2(B) and 6(F).
E is at 4, not adjacent to A. ✓

VERIFICATION:
(i)   A(1) immediately right of B(2): B→right(anti-CW) = pos1 = A ✓
(ii)  D(3) is 2nd left(CW) of A(1): A→CW: 1→2→3 = D ✓
(iii) C(5) and F(6): |5-6|=1, adjacent ✓
(iv)  F(6) is 3rd right(anti-CW) of D(3): D(3)→anti-CW: 3→2→1→6 = F ✓
(v)   E(4) not neighbour of A(1): neighbours of 1 = {2,6} ≠ {4} ✓

FINAL ARRANGEMENT (clockwise from top):
A(1) → B(2) → D(3) → E(4) → C(5) → F(6) → back to A

Or in circle:
              [A]
           /      \
        [F]          [B]
        |    CENTRE   |
        [C]          [D]
           \      /
              [E]
```

### Worked Example 2 (Clean) — 8 Persons:


## 8. TYPE 3 — MIXED FACING (SOME IN, SOME OUT)

> Some persons face centre; others face outside. This is the most challenging type.

### Key Rules for Mixed Facing:

```
RULE 1: For each person, identify their facing direction FIRST.
RULE 2: Apply the left/right rule based on THEIR facing direction.
RULE 3: "A faces B" = A and B look toward each other (one faces inward,
```

## 14. MASTER TRICKS & SHORTCUTS

---

### 🚀 TRICK 1 — Fix the Most Constrained Person

```
The person who appears in the MOST clues (or whose absolute position is given) 
→ FIX this person first.
Alternatively: Fix the person who is "opposite" someone → place both immediately.

Example: "A is opposite B, and A is 2nd to the left of C"
→ Fix A, place B opposite, then find C.
```

---

### 🚀 TRICK 2 — Left/Right One-Line Memory Rule

```
INWARD:  LEFT = CLOCKWISE        (L for inward = Like Left+CLockwise, both start differently but...)
OUTWARD: LEFT = ANTI-CLOCKWISE   

SUPER MEMORY: "Inward Left Clicks" (I-L-C)
I = Inward, L = Left, C = Clockwise → When facing inward, Left = Clockwise.
Everything else is the opposite.
```

---

### 🚀 TRICK 3 — Opposite Position Formula

```
In N-person circle (N must be EVEN for exact opposite):
Position of opposite of person at position X = X + N/2 (mod N, starting from 1).

N=6: opposite gap = 3.   N=8: gap = 4.   N=10: gap = 5.   N=12: gap = 6.

QUICK: Count N/2 seats away in either direction = the opposite seat.
```

---

### 🚀 TRICK 4 — "Between" in Circular — Count Both Arcs

```
"N persons between A and B" in a circle can be satisfied TWO ways:
Arc 1 (clockwise from A to B): has N persons between.
Arc 2 (anti-clockwise from A to B): has (Total - N - 2) persons between.

When a clue says "N between A and B": the INTENDED arc is the one with N persons.
The other arc has (Total - N - 2) persons.

Always check BOTH interpretations if the first leads to contradiction.
```

---

### 🚀 TRICK 5 — The "Two Cases" Strategy

```
Many clues give two possible placements (e.g., "P is to the left OR right of Q").
SYSTEMATICALLY try Case 1 and Case 2.
Only ONE case will satisfy ALL remaining clues.

Track cases as:
Case 1: X at position A → solve → check all clues.
Case 2: X at position B → solve → check all clues.
One will succeed; the other will contradict.
```

---

### 🚀 TRICK 6 — Neighbor Chain Building

```
When you have consecutive "immediate left/right" clues:
"B left of A, C left of B, D left of C"
→ Build the BLOCK: A-B-C-D (clockwise if left=CW for inward)
→ Place this entire block as a unit.

This reduces 3 separate clues into 1 block placement.
```

---

### 🚀 TRICK 7 — Opposite + Neighbor Elimination

```
"A opposite B" AND "C adjacent to A" AND "D adjacent to B":
→ Draw A with B directly across.
→ C is one of A's two neighbors.
→ D is one of B's two neighbors.
→ C and D are in specific zones of the circle, which can be narrowed down further.
```

---

### 🚀 TRICK 8 — Gender Pattern Lock

```
If no two same-gender persons are adjacent in an N-person circle:
→ N must be EVEN for alternating to work.
→ Males at ODD positions (1,3,5...) OR EVEN positions (2,4,6...).
→ Once gender of any one person is fixed at one position,
   ALL other positions' genders are determined instantly!
```

---

### 🚀 TRICK 9 — The "Not Adjacent" Shortcut

```
Apply "not adjacent" clues LAST (after all positive clues placed).
These are ELIMINATION clues, not PLACEMENT clues.

When two people remain for two positions:
Check which arrangement satisfies "X not adjacent to Y."
Only ONE arrangement will work.
```

---

### 🚀 TRICK 10 — Count Remaining Seats Check

```
After EVERY placement, do this 10-second check:
Persons placed + Persons remaining = Total persons?
Seats filled + Seats empty = Total seats?
These MUST ALWAYS be equal. If not, you made a mistake.
```

---

### 🚀 TRICK 11 — Verify by Neighbors

```
Final arrangement verification shortcut:
For each person, state their two neighbors (left and right).
Check EVERY neighbor-based clue against this list.
This takes 30 seconds but catches ALL mistakes.
```

---

### 🚀 TRICK 12 — The "Facing" Shortcut for Mixed Problems

```
When some face in and some face out:
First, determine WHO faces in and who faces out.
Then, group clues:
- Clues for inward-facers: LEFT=CW, RIGHT=anti-CW.
- Clues for outward-facers: LEFT=anti-CW, RIGHT=CW.
Handle each group separately, then combine.
```

---

### 🚀 TRICK 13 — Circular Position Numbering Shortcut

```
Always number positions 1 to N clockwise from the TOP.
Position 1 = TOP (where you fix the first person).
Position 2 = One step CW from top.
...
Position N = One step anti-CW from top (= last CW position).

This consistent numbering prevents confusion in complex puzzles.
```

---

### 🚀 TRICK 14 — The "Relative Position" Table

```
Before starting ANY circular puzzle, quickly note:
"Who is mentioned with who" to find the most central person (appears in most clues).
Fix THAT person.

Count mentions:
A: clues i, ii, iii → 3 mentions
B: clues i, iv → 2 mentions
C: clue ii only → 1 mention
→ Fix A first!
```

---

### 🚀 TRICK 15 — Double-Check with "Alternate Path"

```
After finding the arrangement, verify using an ALTERNATE PATH:
Instead of going clockwise, trace anti-clockwise and confirm:
- "X is 3rd to the right of Y" going anti-clockwise should give the same answer as clockwise.
- If any clue gives a DIFFERENT result when traced differently → ERROR.
```

## 15. STEP-BY-STEP ATTACK STRATEGY

---

### The Universal 9-Step Method:

```
STEP 1: READ the entire puzzle ONCE completely.
         Note: Total persons, facing direction (in/out/mixed), any special setup.

STEP 2: DRAW the circle.
         N seats, numbered 1→N clockwise. Position 1 at TOP.

STEP 3: NOTE the facing direction for all (or relevant) persons.
         This determines LEFT/RIGHT convention.

STEP 4: IDENTIFY the most anchored person:
         → Who has an exact/opposite position given?
         → Who appears in the most clues?
         FIX that person at position 1.

STEP 5: APPLY priority clues:
         Priority A: "Exactly opposite" → place immediately.
         Priority B: "Immediately to left/right" of fixed person → place next.
         Priority C: "Kth to left/right" → count and place.
         Priority D: "Between" clues → find the gap.

STEP 6: AFTER EACH PLACEMENT, scan ALL remaining clues:
         Does any clue now become solvable? Apply it immediately.

STEP 7: When STUCK:
         → Use "not adjacent" clues to eliminate possibilities.
         → Try Case 1 and Case 2 for ambiguous placements.
         → Check if remaining empty seats + remaining persons are consistent.

STEP 8: FILL IN the last remaining persons using process of elimination.

STEP 9: VERIFY EVERY CLUE against the final arrangement.
         Go clue by clue. Mark each clue ✓ when satisfied.
```

---

### Decision Flowchart When Stuck:

```
Stuck?
   │
   ├─ Any "exactly opposite" clue unused? → Place both immediately.
   │
   ├─ Any "immediate neighbor" clue of an already-placed person? → Apply it.
   │
   ├─ "Either left OR right" ambiguity? → Try BOTH cases systematically.
   │
   ├─ Only 2 persons left for 2 seats? → Try both options, use negative clues.
   │
   └─ Gender/profession pattern? → Apply alternating pattern to fill remaining.
```

## 16. COMMON MISTAKE ALERTS

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 1 — Not Drawing the Circle First

```
Wrong: Trying to solve mentally or with just text.
Right: ALWAYS draw a circle with numbered seats before reading the clues.
       The circle is your workspace. Use it.
```

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 2 — Applying Wrong Left/Right Rule

```
Wrong: Using "left = lower position" (linear rule) in circular arrangements.
Right: 
  Inward facing  → LEFT = CLOCKWISE
  Outward facing → LEFT = ANTI-CLOCKWISE
  Check facing direction BEFORE interpreting any left/right clue.
```

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 3 — Using Linear "Opposite" in Circles

```
Wrong: "Opposite of position 3 in a 6-seat circle = position 3+1=4."
Right: Opposite of position X = position X + N/2.
       Opposite of 3 in 6-seat circle = 3 + 3 = 6.
```

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 4 — Between Count Error in Circles

```
Wrong: "2 persons between A and B means positions differ by 2."
Right: "2 persons between means positions differ by 3" (same as linear!).
       Formula: |pos_A - pos_B| = N_between + 1 (for the shorter arc).
       In circular: always check BOTH arcs.
```

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 5 — Forgetting Wrap-Around

```
Wrong: "Position 6 + 1 = position 7" in a 6-seat circle.
Right: "Position 6 + 1 = position 1" (wraps around).
       Always use modular arithmetic: (pos + 1 - 1) mod N + 1.
       Or just count around the circle diagram.
```

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 6 — Treating Circle as Linear

```
Wrong: "A is at position 1, so A is at the left end."
Right: In a circle, there is NO left end or right end.
       Position 1 is just a reference; it connects to both position 2 AND position N.
```

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 7 — Not Fixing One Person

```
Wrong: Trying to place people in "absolute" positions without fixing anyone.
Right: Always fix ONE person at position 1 (the reference point).
       Every other person is placed RELATIVE to this fixed person.
```

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 8 — Mixed Facing Left/Right Confusion

```
Wrong: Applying inward left/right rules to outward-facing persons.
Right: For each person, identify their facing direction separately.
       Apply the correct rule for EACH person individually.
```

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 9 — "Faces" Clue Misinterpretation

```
"A faces B" in circular context usually means:
→ They are directly across the table from each other (opposite seats).
NOT: A is looking at B while B looks elsewhere.

In some mixed-facing puzzles, "A faces B" can mean they look at each other
(one faces in, one faces out, same column/position).
Context determines which meaning applies.
```

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 10 — Not Verifying ALL Clues

```
Wrong: Stopping after placing all persons without checking every clue.
Right: Go through EVERY SINGLE CLUE and verify ✓ before answering questions.
       Missing even ONE clue can mean your entire arrangement is wrong.
```

## 🏆 TOP 10 EXAM TIPS

1. **Draw the circle BEFORE reading any clue.** The moment you see "circular table," draw a circle with N numbered seats. This is non-negotiable. Your circle diagram is your answer sheet.

2. **Left = Clockwise for inward-facing persons.** This is the single most important fact in circular seating. When everyone faces the centre (most common), CW = their left. Anti-CW = their right. Memorize: "Inward Left Clicks" (I-L-C).

3. **Fix one person at the top (position 1) always.** Never try to solve without fixing a reference. Fix the person who appears in the most clues, or the one whose position is given by "opposite" or an exact clue.

4. **Place "exactly opposite" pairs first.** These two placements are FREE information. Once you fix person A, their opposite is automatically placed. Use this to immediately fill 2 seats.

5. **Check BOTH arcs for "between" clues.** "3 persons between A and B" can be satisfied going clockwise OR anti-clockwise. When one gives a conflict, try the other before concluding no solution exists.

6. **Scan all remaining clues after EVERY placement.** Each new person placed may unlock 2–3 other clues. Chain placements are faster than isolated ones. Never stop to "think" without scanning all unused clues first.

7. **Use negative clues only after exhausting positive clues.** "Not adjacent" and "not at position X" are elimination tools, not placement tools. Save them for the final 2–3 persons.

8. **For mixed-facing puzzles, label each person's facing direction on the diagram.** Write ↓ (inward) or ↑ (outward) next to each person's name as you place them. Then apply the correct left/right rule for THAT person's direction.

9. **In double-circle problems, handle each circle separately first.** Solve the outer circle fully, then use "faces" clues to link the inner circle. Don't intermix the two circles' clues prematurely.

10. **Verify every single clue against your final arrangement.** List all clues and tick them one by one. This 30-second check catches 100% of errors before you answer the questions. One wrong clue check → potentially 5 wrong answers. Always verify.
