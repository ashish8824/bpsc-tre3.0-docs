## 1. THE BIG PICTURE — WHAT ARE BLOOD RELATIONS?

**Blood Relations** questions test your ability to identify the relationship between two people based on given information about family connections.

> **Golden Rule:** Always draw a family tree. Never try to solve in your head. A drawn tree eliminates ALL confusion.

---

### What You Need to Know Before Starting:

**Two Types of Relations in a Family:**
```
1. BLOOD RELATIONS  → Related by birth (parent, child, sibling, grandparent)
2. MARRIAGE RELATIONS → Related by marriage (husband, wife, in-laws)
```

**Two Genders (Critical for Every Question):**
```
MALE   → Father, Son, Brother, Grandfather, Grandson,
          Uncle, Nephew, Husband, Brother-in-law
FEMALE → Mother, Daughter, Sister, Grandmother, Granddaughter,
          Aunt, Niece, Wife, Sister-in-law
```

---

### Standard Symbols Used in Family Trees:

```
□  = Male person
○  = Female person
□─○ = Married couple (horizontal line)
│   = Parent-child relationship (vertical line)
═══ = Siblings (connected horizontally at same level)

Example:
    □─────○         (Grandfather married Grandmother)
    │
  ──┴──
  │   │
  □   ○             (Son and Daughter — siblings)
```

## 3. GENERATION CHART — THE MASTER FRAMEWORK

> This is the SINGLE MOST IMPORTANT concept. Every question becomes simple once you understand generations.

---

### The 5-Generation Framework:

```
GENERATION +2  →  Great-Grandparents
GENERATION +1  →  Grandparents
GENERATION  0  →  Parents, Uncles, Aunts (YOUR parents' generation)
GENERATION -1  →  YOU, Siblings, Cousins (YOUR generation)
GENERATION -2  →  Your Children, Nephews, Nieces
GENERATION -3  →  Your Grandchildren
```

---

### Generation Rules:

```
Moving UP one generation:    Father, Mother, Uncle, Aunt
Moving DOWN one generation:  Son, Daughter, Nephew, Niece
Same generation:             Brother, Sister, Cousin,
                             Husband, Wife,
                             Brother-in-law, Sister-in-law
```

---

### Visual Multi-Generation Family Tree:

```
Generation +1:    □─○         □─○
(Grandparents)  GF GM       GF GM
                   │           │
Generation  0:  ┌──┴──┐    ┌──┴──┐
(Your parents)  │     │    │     │
               □─○    □  ○    □─○
              Dad Mom Unc Aunt      (Uncle married, Aunt married)
                │               │
Generation -1: ┌┴──┐        ┌──┴┐
(Your gen.)    │   │        │   │
               □   ○        □   ○
              You Sis     Cou  Cou
                              (Cousins)
```

## 4. TYPE 1 — DIRECT BLOOD RELATION QUESTIONS

> **Format:** A statement gives a chain of relations. You must find the relationship between the first and last person.

---

### Sub-Type 1A — Simple Two-Step Chain

**Example 1:**
```
"A is the father of B. B is the sister of C. How is A related to C?"

Step 1: Draw it.
A (father) → B (daughter of A)
B is sister of C → C is also child of A

          A (Male)
          │
       ───┴───
       │     │
       B     C
    (Female) (?)

A is the father of C.
Answer: A is the father of C.
```

---

### Sub-Type 1B — Three-Step Chain

**Example 2:**
```
"A is the father of B. B is the mother of C. C is the brother of D.
How is A related to D?"

Build step by step:
A → father of B → B is male? No! B is MOTHER of C → B is FEMALE.
So A is father, B is daughter of A (and also mother of C).

A (□) ─── (not shown, B's husband)
      │
      B (○) ─── B's husband
              │
           ───┴───
           │     │
           C     D
         (Male)  (?)

A is B's father. B is C and D's mother.
Therefore: A is C's and D's MATERNAL GRANDFATHER.
Answer: A is the grandfather of D.
```

---

### Sub-Type 1C — Four-Step Chain

**Example 3:**
```
"Pointing to a photograph, Ravi said, 'She is the daughter of my
grandfather's only son.' How is the girl in the photograph related to Ravi?"

Step 1: Start from Ravi and trace.
"My grandfather's only son" → grandfather has only one son → that son is Ravi's FATHER.
"She is the daughter of my father" → She is Ravi's SISTER.

Answer: The girl is Ravi's Sister.
```

**Example 4:**
```
"A is the son of B. C is the daughter of A. D is the husband of B.
How is D related to C?"

B is female (son A belongs to B, and D is husband of B → B is wife).
A is son of B and D.
C is daughter of A.

D ─────── B
          │
          A (son)
          │
          C (granddaughter of D)

Answer: D is the grandfather of C.
```

---

### Sub-Type 1D — "Only" Clue Questions

> When you see "only son," "only daughter," "only child" — this is a KEY restriction!

**Example 5:**
```
"Pointing to a man, a woman said, 'His mother is the only daughter
of my mother.' How is the woman related to the man?"

"Only daughter of my mother" = the woman herself!
So his mother = the woman.
Therefore the woman is his MOTHER.

Answer: The woman is the man's Mother.
```

**Example 6:**
```
"A said to B: 'Your mother's husband's sister is my aunt.'
How is A related to B?"

"Your mother's husband" = B's Father (since husband of B's mother = B's father)
"B's father's sister" = B's Aunt = A's Aunt too.
If A's aunt = B's aunt → A and B have the same aunt → A and B are COUSINS.

Answer: A is B's Cousin.
```

## 5. TYPE 2 — CODED / SYMBOLIC BLOOD RELATIONS

> **Format:** Symbols (+, −, ×, ÷, @, $, #, etc.) are used to represent relationships. A chain is given and you must decode it.

---

### Common Symbol Formats Used in Exams:

```
FORMAT 1 (Operations):        FORMAT 2 (Symbols):
A + B = A is father of B      A @ B = A is mother of B
A − B = A is wife of B        A # B = A is brother of B
A × B = A is son of B         A $ B = A is sister of B
A ÷ B = A is daughter of B    A % B = A is husband of B

FORMAT 3 (Mixed):
A * B = A is brother of B
A & B = A is father of B
A ^ B = A is wife of B
```

---

### Example 7 — Symbolic Chain (Format 1):

```
Given: A + B means A is the father of B
       A − B means A is the mother of B
       A × B means A is the brother of B
       A ÷ B means A is the sister of B

Question: If P + Q − R, how is P related to R?

Decode:
P + Q → P is the father of Q
Q − R → Q is the mother of R (Q is female, and Q is father P's child → daughter)

Build tree:
         P (□, father)
         │
         Q (○, daughter of P, mother of R)
         │
         R

P is the grandfather of R.
Answer: P is the grandfather of R.
```

---

### Example 8 — Symbolic Chain (Format 2):

```
Given: P $ Q means P is the father of Q
       P @ Q means P is the sister of Q
       P # Q means P is the mother of Q
       P % Q means P is the son of Q

Question: If A $ B % C @ D, how is A related to D?

Decode step by step:
A $ B → A is father of B
B % C → B is son of C (so C is parent of B)
Wait: B is son of C AND A is father of B → C must be A's wife (mother of B).

C @ D → C is sister of D.

Build tree:
A ─────── C
          │
          B (son)

C is sister of D → D is C's sibling (same generation as C).
C is A's wife. D is C's sibling → D is A's sibling-in-law? 
More precisely: D is A's BROTHER-IN-LAW or SISTER-IN-LAW.
Without D's gender: D is A's sibling-in-law.
Answer: A is the brother-in-law of D (if D is male) or A is the brother-in-law of D.
```

---

### Example 9 — Symbolic Chain (Finding relationship of X to Y):

```
Given:
A * B → A is the wife of B
A & B → A is the father of B
A ^ B → A is the brother of B

Question: What is the relationship of X to Z in: X ^ Y & Z?

X ^ Y → X is the brother of Y
Y & Z → Y is the father of Z

So X is the brother of Y (Y's father), meaning X is Z's UNCLE (father's brother = paternal uncle).
Answer: X is the uncle of Z.
```

## 6. TYPE 3 — PUZZLE / PARAGRAPH TYPE

> **Format:** A paragraph describes a complex family. Multiple relationships are given. You must draw the complete family tree and answer questions.

---

### The SYSTEMATIC Approach to Paragraphs:

```
Step 1: List all PERSONS mentioned.
Step 2: Note each person's GENDER (from names or stated gender).
Step 3: Draw the tree starting from the OLDEST generation.
Step 4: Add each clue ONE BY ONE.
Step 5: Verify your tree is consistent.
Step 6: Answer the question.
```

---

### Example 10 — Medium Paragraph:

```
"There are 6 members in a family — A, B, C, D, E, F.
B is the son of C but C is not the mother of B.
A and C are married couple.
E is the brother of C.
D is the daughter of A.
F is the brother of B."

Find: (a) Who is the mother of B?
      (b) How is E related to D?
      (c) How is D related to F?

SOLUTION — Step by Step:

Step 1: List persons: A, B, C, D, E, F

Step 2: Identify genders:
"B is the son of C" → B is MALE.
"C is NOT the mother of B" → C is MALE (father!).
"A and C are married couple" → one is male, one is female. C is male → A is FEMALE.
"E is the brother of C" → E is MALE.
"D is the daughter of A" → D is FEMALE.
"F is the brother of B" → F is MALE.

Step 3: Build tree:

    E    A(○)──────C(□)
              │
         ─────┴──────
         │     │    │
         B     D    F
        (□)   (○)  (□)

Step 4: Verify: B is son of C ✓ (C is father), A is mother of B ✓

ANSWERS:
(a) A is the mother of B.
(b) E is the brother of C. C is the father of D. So E is D's UNCLE (paternal).
(c) D is the sister of F (both children of A and C).
```

---

### Example 11 — Hard Paragraph (3 Generations):

```
"M is the son of N. N is the daughter of O. P is the son of O.
Q is the wife of P. R is the daughter of P.
How is M related to R?"

Step 1: Parse each clue:
Clue 1: M is son of N → N is parent of M, N is female (mother).
Clue 2: N is daughter of O → O is parent of N, N is female ✓.
Clue 3: P is son of O → O has son P and daughter N (siblings!).
Clue 4: Q is wife of P → Q is female, P is male ✓.
Clue 5: R is daughter of P → R is child of P and Q.

Step 2: Build tree:

Generation 0:          O
                    ───┴───
Generation -1:      N       P ──── Q
                    │           │
Generation -2:      M           R

Step 3: Trace relationship:
M is son of N.
N is sibling of P.
P is father of R.
N is aunt of R (N is P's sister → N is R's aunt).
M is son of N → M is N's child → M is cousin of R.

Answer: M is the COUSIN of R.
```

---

### Example 12 — Very Hard Paragraph (Full Family):

```
"A family has 8 members: P, Q, R, S, T, U, V, W.
P and Q are a married couple. P is the male.
R is the son of Q but not of P.
S is the sister of R.
T is the brother of P.
U is the mother of P.
V is the son of T.
W is the wife of T.
How is V related to S?"

Step 1: Determine genders:
P = male (stated), Q = female (P's wife).
R = male (son), S = female (sister of R).
T = male (brother of P).
U = female (mother of P).
W = female (wife of T).
V = male (son of T).

Step 2: Build tree:

         U (○)
         │
    ─────┴─────
    │          │
    P (□)──Q(○)  T (□)──W(○)
             │          │
         ────┴───        V (□)
         │      │
        R(□)   S(○)
    (son of Q  (sister
    but not P)  of R)

Wait: "R is son of Q but not of P" → R has a different father (not P).
This means Q was previously married or R is from a different relationship.
For exam purposes: Q is mother of R and S, but P is NOT their father.
The tree remains: R and S are Q's children. P is Q's husband but not R/S's father.

Step 3: Trace V to S:
V is son of T.
T is brother of P.
P is husband of Q.
Q is mother of S (and R).
V is T's son → V is nephew/niece of P → V's uncle is P → P's wife is Q → Q's daughter is S.
V's relationship to S: V is son of T (P's brother). S is daughter of Q (P's wife, not P's child).
V and S are NOT blood related. V's uncle is P. P is married to Q. Q is S's mother.
V is related to S through marriage: V's uncle (P) is S's stepfather (or mother's husband).
This is complex. In exams, they simplify: if question asks "how is V related to S" and the expected answer given the family structure = V is the COUSIN of S (assuming standard family, ignoring the "not of P" complexity) OR they say "Cannot be determined."

Simplified for exam: Answer = Data Inadequate / Cannot be determined.
```

## 7. TYPE 4 — POINTING / INTRODUCING TYPE

> **Format:** A person POINTS to someone (in a photo or in person) and describes their relationship. You must find how the person is related to the pointer.

---

### The Golden Formula:

```
"Pointing to X, A says: 'X is the [relation] of my [relation]'"

STEP 1: Start from A.
STEP 2: Find "my [relation]" starting from A.
STEP 3: Then find X = [relation] of that person.
STEP 4: That gives X's relationship to A.
```

---

### Example 13 — Classic Pointing Question:

```
"Pointing to a photograph, Arun said, 'She is the daughter of the
only son of my grandmother.' How is the girl in the photo related to Arun?"

Trace from Arun:
"My grandmother" → Arun's grandmother.
"Only son of my grandmother" → Arun's grandmother's only son = Arun's FATHER.
"Daughter of my father" = Arun's SISTER.

Answer: The girl is Arun's Sister.
```

---

### Example 14 — Tricky Pointing:

```
"Pointing to a woman, Suresh said, 'She is the mother of the only
child of my wife.' How is the woman related to Suresh?"

"My wife" → Suresh's wife.
"Only child of my wife" → Suresh's wife has only one child. That child belongs to both Suresh and his wife.
"Mother of that child" → Suresh's wife herself!
So the woman = Suresh's wife.

Answer: The woman is Suresh's Wife.
```

---

### Example 15 — Double Step Pointing:

```
"Pointing to a man in a photograph, a woman said, 'His mother's
husband's father is my grandfather.' How is the woman related to the man?"

Trace:
"His mother's husband" = the man's father (husband of his mother = his father).
"His father's father" = his grandfather.
"My grandfather" = the woman's grandfather too.

So: Man's grandfather = Woman's grandfather.
→ They share the same grandfather → They are COUSINS (or siblings, if same parents).
In most exam contexts: COUSINS.

Answer: The woman is the man's Cousin.
```

---

### Example 16 — Photograph + Gender Ambiguity:

```
"Pointing to a portrait, a man said, 'I have no brother or sister,
but that man's father is my father's son.' Who is in the portrait?"

"I have no brother or sister" → the man is an ONLY CHILD.
"My father's son" → since no siblings, father's son = the man HIMSELF.
"That man's father is myself (the man pointing)" → the man in portrait is the
pointer's SON.

Answer: The portrait shows the man's Son.
```

---

### Example 17 — Woman Pointing:

```
"Pointing to a man, a lady said, 'He is the son of the woman who is
the mother of the husband of my daughter.' How is the man related to the lady?"

Trace step by step:
"My daughter's husband" = lady's son-in-law.
"Mother of my son-in-law" = lady's son-in-law's mother.
"Son of that woman" = son of son-in-law's mother = son-in-law himself OR his brother.

If son = son-in-law himself → He is lady's SON-IN-LAW.
But "son of the woman who is the mother of son-in-law" could be son-in-law or his brother.
Since the lady is pointing to him and not her daughter's husband (who she knows), this must be son-in-law's brother.
Answer: The man is the lady's Son-in-law's brother = Lady's Son-in-law (if same person) OR Data insufficient.

Standard exam answer: The man is the lady's SON-IN-LAW.
```

## 8. TYPE 5 — MIXED / MULTI-STEP CHAIN

> **Format:** A long description gives multiple clues. You must trace a long chain to find the final relationship.

---

### Example 18 — Five-Step Chain:

```
"A is the brother of B.
B is the daughter of C.
C is the son of D.
D is the wife of E.
How is E related to A?"

Step-by-step tree:

E (□) ──── D (○)
           │
           C (□, son of D)
           │
      ─────┴─────
      │          │
      B (○)      A (□)
 (daughter      (brother of B,
  of C)          also child of C)

Wait: A is brother of B. B is daughter of C. So A is also a child of C!
A is son of C (since A is male, brother of B).

E is husband of D.
D is mother of C.
C is father of A.
So E is great-grandfather of A? No:

E──D (married)
   │
   C (D's son)
   │
 ──┴──
 │   │
 B   A

E is D's husband. D is C's mother. C is A's father.
E is grandfather of A (E's son is C? No: D's son is C, not E's son directly confirmed — but E is D's husband, so E is C's father → C is E and D's son → E is grandfather of A).

Answer: E is the Grandfather of A.
```

---

### Example 19 — In-Law Chain:

```
"Pointing to a man, a woman says, 'His brother's father is the
only son of my grandfather.' How is the woman related to the man?"

"My grandfather's only son" = the woman's father (grandfather has one son = her father).
"His brother's father" = the man's father (brother's father = man's own father, as they share the same father).
So: Man's father = Woman's father → They are SIBLINGS.
Since the woman is female: She is his SISTER.

Answer: The woman is the man's Sister.
```

---

### Example 20 — Multi-Person Mixed:

```
"P's mother is Q's daughter. R is P's brother. S is R's uncle.
How is S related to Q?"

Build tree:
"P's mother is Q's daughter" → P's mother is female child of Q.
Let P's mother = M (a daughter of Q).

Q
│
M (daughter of Q, mother of P)
│
──┴──
│   │
P   R (siblings)

S is R's uncle → S is brother of R's parent (M or M's husband).
If S is M's brother → S is Q's son.
S is Q's SON.

Answer: S is the Son of Q.
```

## 9. MASTER TRICKS & SHORTCUTS

---

### 🚀 TRICK 1 — The "My Father's Son" Shortcut

```
"My father's son" (when speaker has no siblings) = MYSELF
"My father's son" (when speaker has brothers) = me or my brother
"My mother's daughter" (speaker has no sisters) = MYSELF
"My mother's daughter" (with sisters) = me or my sister

KEY: Always check if "only child" is mentioned!
If only child → father's son/mother's daughter = the person themselves.
```

### 🚀 TRICK 2 — "Only Son/Daughter" = Direct Identification

```
"My grandfather's only son" = My FATHER (if grandfather has one son)
"My mother's only daughter" = My MOTHER's only daughter = might be the mother's self or specific person
"Her only son's daughter" = the son's daughter (granddaughter of her)
```

### 🚀 TRICK 3 — Reverse Relationship Chart

```
If A is father of B → B is son/daughter of A
If A is mother of B → B is son/daughter of A
If A is brother of B → B is brother/sister of A
If A is uncle of B → B is nephew/niece of A
If A is grandfather of B → B is grandson/granddaughter of A
If A is husband of B → B is wife of A
If A is son-in-law of B → B is father-in-law/mother-in-law of A
```

### 🚀 TRICK 4 — The Generation Level Trick

```
+2 levels UP from you = Grandparent
+1 level UP from you = Parent / Uncle / Aunt
Same level as you = Sibling / Cousin / Spouse / Sibling-in-law
-1 level DOWN from you = Child / Nephew / Niece
-2 levels DOWN from you = Grandchild

Use this to quickly verify your answer!
```

### 🚀 TRICK 5 — Gender Clue Words

```
MALE indicators:    son, father, brother, uncle, nephew,
                    grandfather, grandson, husband, sir, Mr., he, his, him
FEMALE indicators:  daughter, mother, sister, aunt, niece,
                    grandmother, granddaughter, wife, madam, Mrs., she, her
```

### 🚀 TRICK 6 — In-Law Quick Rules

```
SPOUSE'S relative = YOUR [same relation]-in-law
SIBLING'S spouse  = YOUR sibling-in-law
CHILD'S spouse    = YOUR child-in-law (son-in-law or daughter-in-law)
SPOUSE'S sibling  = YOUR sibling-in-law

Example: Spouse's brother = Brother-in-law ✓
         Spouse's sister  = Sister-in-law ✓
         Sister's husband = Brother-in-law ✓
         Brother's wife   = Sister-in-law ✓
```

### 🚀 TRICK 7 — Coded Relations: Decode Each Symbol One at a Time

```
Never try to read the whole chain at once.
Decode: Symbol 1 → draw → Symbol 2 → draw → Symbol 3 → draw → Answer.

Example: A # B @ C means:
A # B → A is [relation] of B (draw it)
B @ C → B is [relation] of C (extend the draw)
Then read relationship of A to C from your drawing.
```

### 🚀 TRICK 8 — The "Mother's Husband" Shortcut

```
Mother's husband = FATHER (always, in standard family)
Father's wife    = MOTHER (always, in standard family)

BUT: In exam questions, sometimes they use "mother's husband"
to mean stepfather (if they say "not her natural father").
Watch for qualifiers!
```

### 🚀 TRICK 9 — Pointing Type: Simplify the Chain

```
For "X is the [r1] of [person's r2]":

Step 1: Find [person's r2] from the pointer.
Step 2: Find what [r1] of that person means.
Step 3: That's your answer about X relative to the pointer.

Shortcut table:
"Son of my father"         = MY BROTHER (or me if only child)
"Daughter of my father"    = MY SISTER (or me if only child)
"Son of my mother"         = MY BROTHER (or me)
"Daughter of my mother"    = MY SISTER (or me)
"Father of my father"      = MY GRANDFATHER
"Mother of my father"      = MY GRANDMOTHER
"Father of my mother"      = MY GRANDFATHER
"Mother of my mother"      = MY GRANDMOTHER
"Son of my grandfather"    = MY FATHER or MY UNCLE
"Daughter of my grandfather" = MY AUNT (or my mother)
"Son of my grandmother"    = MY FATHER or MY UNCLE
```

### 🚀 TRICK 10 — When Answer is "Cannot Be Determined"

```
Choose "Cannot be determined" or "Data Inadequate" when:
1. Gender of a key person is not specified and it matters.
2. Two valid family trees can be drawn from the same clues.
3. A relationship is ambiguous (e.g., could be aunt or grandmother).

Example: "A is the child of B" → A's gender unknown → 
Cannot say if A is son or daughter without more info.
```

## 10. GENDER DETERMINATION RULES

> One of the most critical skills. Many questions hinge on correctly identifying gender.

---

### Rule 1 — Explicit Statement

```
"X is the father" → X is MALE
"X is the mother" → X is FEMALE
"X is a man/boy/he" → MALE
"X is a woman/girl/she" → FEMALE
```

---

### Rule 2 — Role-Based Determination

```
If X is described as:                  X is:
─────────────────────────────────────────────
Father / Son / Brother / Husband       MALE
Uncle / Nephew / Grandfather           MALE
Grandson / Brother-in-law              MALE
─────────────────────────────────────────────
Mother / Daughter / Sister / Wife      FEMALE
Aunt / Niece / Grandmother             FEMALE
Granddaughter / Sister-in-law          FEMALE
```

---

### Rule 3 — Negative Clue (Very Important in Exams!)

```
"X is the son of Y but Y is NOT the mother of X"
→ Y is the FATHER of X (not mother, so father!)

"A and B are married. B is not the husband of A."
→ A is the husband, B is the wife.

"C is the parent of D but C is not the father of D."
→ C is the MOTHER of D.
```

---

### Rule 4 — Inference from Relations

```
If A is the wife of B, and C is the son of A:
→ B is male (husband), A is female (wife), C is male.
→ B is the father of C.

If D is the sister of E, and E is the son of F:
→ D is female (sister), E is male (son).
→ D and E are siblings, both children of F.
```

---

### Rule 5 — Indian Name Gender Clues (Common in Exams)

```
Common MALE names:   Ramesh, Suresh, Arun, Rajesh, Mohan,
                     Arjun, Vikram, Rahul, Ravi, Anand
Common FEMALE names: Priya, Rekha, Sunita, Meera, Anita,
                     Kavita, Seema, Asha, Nisha, Uma
Note: Don't assume — always rely on stated gender when given.
```

## 11. STEP-BY-STEP ATTACK STRATEGY

---

### For ALL Blood Relation Questions:

```
STEP 1: READ the entire question once fully.
        Don't start drawing mid-way through reading.

STEP 2: LIST all persons mentioned.
        Example: A, B, C, D, E, F (6 persons)

STEP 3: DETERMINE gender for each person.
        Use explicit clues, role clues, negative clues.
        Mark: □ = Male, ○ = Female, ? = Unknown

STEP 4: IDENTIFY the OLDEST generation first.
        Usually mentioned as grandparents or oldest person.

STEP 5: DRAW the tree top-down.
        Use this format:
        □─○ for couples (horizontal)
        │  for parent-child (vertical)
        siblings connected at same horizontal level

STEP 6: ADD each clue to the tree ONE AT A TIME.
        Verify after each addition.

STEP 7: ANSWER the question by reading your tree.
        Trace from person A to person B through the tree.

STEP 8: VERIFY your answer makes sense:
        → Are generations correct?
        → Is gender consistent?
        → Does every clue hold in your tree?
```

---

### Quick Decision Tree for Pointing Questions:

```
Person says: "X is the [r1] of my [r2]"
                        │
              ┌─────────┴─────────┐
           Find MY [r2]         Then find [r1] of that person
                        │
              That is X. What is X to ME?
```

## 12. COMMON MISTAKE ALERTS

---

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 1 — Assuming Gender Without Clue

```
Wrong: Assuming A is male just because A comes first.
Right: Only assign gender when explicitly stated or clearly inferable.
Names are NOT reliable gender indicators in exams (unless stated).
```

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 2 — Confusing "Son of" with "Father of"

```
Wrong: "A is the son of B" → B is A's son.
Right: "A is the son of B" → B is A's parent (father or mother).
The sentence structure: "[Subject] is the [relation] of [Object]"
Subject HOLDS the relation TO the Object.
```

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 3 — Missing the Negative Clue

```
"B is the son of A but A is not the father of B"
Wrong: Ignoring "A is not the father" → A could be mother!
Right: A is not the father → A is the MOTHER of B.
```

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 4 — "Father's Son" = Me? Not Always!

```
"My father's son is the doctor" — if you have brothers, it could be your brother!
Only say "myself" if the person is stated to be an only child.
Otherwise: "father's son = me or my brother."
```

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 5 — In-Law Confusion

```
Wrong: "Sister's husband" = Brother.
Right: "Sister's husband" = Brother-IN-LAW.

Wrong: "Wife's brother" = Brother.
Right: "Wife's brother" = Brother-IN-LAW.

The "-in-law" is CRITICAL and often tested!
```

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 6 — Maternal vs Paternal Mix-Up

```
Mother's brother = MATERNAL UNCLE
Father's brother = PATERNAL UNCLE
Both are your uncles for exam purposes (same answer: UNCLE).
But don't confuse: Uncle ≠ Grandfather.
```

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 7 — Cousin vs Sibling

```
"My aunt's son" = COUSIN (not brother).
"My uncle's daughter" = COUSIN (not sister).
Cousins are NEVER brothers/sisters (they are separate family units).
```

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 8 — Generation Counting Error

```
MY grandfather's son = MY FATHER or MY UNCLE (same generation as parent).
MY grandfather's daughter = MY AUNT (or mother, if mother's parent is grandfather).
Don't jump: grandfather's son ≠ MY SON.
```

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 9 — Drawing Tree in Wrong Direction

```
Wrong: Drawing child at top, parent at bottom.
Right: ALWAYS draw with oldest generation at TOP, youngest at BOTTOM.
       This prevents generation confusion.
```

### ⚠️ MISTAKE 10 — Not Drawing and Solving in Head

```
NEVER solve blood relation puzzles mentally.
ALWAYS draw the family tree on paper.
90% of errors come from not drawing the tree.
```

### 🟢 EASY — Direct Relations

**A1.** (B) Sister
My grandfather's only son = My father. Daughter of my father = My sister.

**A2.** (B) Brother or Sister
A is father of both B and C. B and C are siblings. Since B's gender is unknown: brother or sister.

**A3.** (A) Brother
X is brother of Y → X is male. Y is sister of Z → Y is female. X and Y are siblings. Y and Z are siblings. So X and Z are siblings. X is male → X is Z's brother.

**A4.** (C) Daughter
"Only son of my father" = the man himself (his father's son = him). Woman is the daughter of the man himself. Answer: His Daughter.

**A5.** (C) Sibling (brother or sister)
Blue is brother of Green → male. Green is sister of Red → female. Blue, Green, Red are all siblings. Red's gender is not given. Red is Blue's sibling = brother or sister.

**A6.** (B) Son
"Only daughter of my mother" = the woman herself. His mother = the woman. So he is her son.

**A7.** (C) Husband
P is Q's father. Q is R's sister → Q and R are siblings (children of same parents, one of whom is S since R is S's daughter). P is Q's father → P is also R's father (as Q and R are siblings under S). Wait: R is S's daughter. P is Q's father. Q is R's sister. So Q and R are siblings. They both have the same parents. S is R's mother → S is also Q's mother → P is S's husband (father of Q and R). P is husband of S. Answer: P is S's husband. ✓

**A8.** (B) Father-in-law
"Wife of the grandson of my mother" — Ravi's mother's grandson = Ravi's son (if Ravi's mother's grandson is through Ravi). Ravi's son's wife = Ravi's daughter-in-law. Ravi is the daughter-in-law's father-in-law.
Answer: Father-in-law.

**A9.** (C) Brother
"My brother's mother" = Sita's mother (same mother). "Only son of Sita's mother" = Sita's brother (if only one son). Answer: Brother.

**A10.** (B) Granddaughter
A is B's sister. C is B's mother → A and B are sisters, both children of C → A is also C's daughter. D is C's father. So A is the granddaughter of D.

## 🏆 TOP 10 EXAM TIPS

1. **ALWAYS draw the family tree** — never solve blood relations in your head. Even for "easy" questions, draw it. 90% of errors are eliminated by drawing.

2. **Start with the oldest generation** — place grandparents at the top, work downward. This prevents generation confusion.

3. **Determine gender BEFORE drawing** — assign □ or ○ to each person using explicit, role-based, and negative clues before connecting anyone in the tree.

4. **"Not the mother/father" = the other parent** — this is the most commonly tested negative clue. Never skip it.

5. **"Only son/daughter" = direct identification** — "grandfather's only son" = father (or specific uncle). Use this to collapse the chain quickly.

6. **In-law ≠ blood relation** — brother-in-law, sister-in-law, son-in-law, daughter-in-law are marriage relations. Never confuse them with blood relations.

7. **For coded/symbol questions — decode ONE symbol at a time** — draw after each decoded symbol before moving to the next.

8. **For pointing questions — trace from the speaker, not from the person being pointed to** — always start from "I/my" and build outward.

9. **Siblings share the same parent level** — draw siblings at the same horizontal level connected by a bracket. This visually confirms they are not parent-child.

10. **Check your answer's generation level** — if your answer is "grandfather" but the tree shows same-generation, you made an error. Always verify the generation before finalizing.
