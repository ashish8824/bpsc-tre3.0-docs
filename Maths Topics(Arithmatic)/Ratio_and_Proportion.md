## 1. What is a Ratio?

A **ratio** is a comparison between two or more quantities of the **same kind** and in the **same unit**.

- Written as **a : b** or **a/b**
- Read as **"a is to b"**
- a is called the **antecedent** (first term)
- b is called the **consequent** (second term)

### 📌 Example:
If a class has 20 boys and 15 girls:
- Ratio of boys to girls = 20 : 15 = **4 : 3** (simplified by dividing by HCF = 5)

> ⚠️ **Key Rule:** A ratio has **no units**. Both quantities must be in the **same unit** before forming a ratio.

**Example:** Ratio of 1 hour to 45 minutes = 60 : 45 = **4 : 3** (convert to same unit first)

---

## 2. Types of Ratios

| Type | Condition | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Equal Ratio** | a : b = 1 : 1 | 5 : 5 |
| **Greater Inequality** | a > b | 7 : 3 |
| **Lesser Inequality** | a < b | 3 : 7 |
| **Ratio of Equality** | Both terms equal | 4 : 4 |
| **Inverse / Reciprocal Ratio** | b : a for a given a : b | If 3:5, inverse = 5:3 |

---

## 3. Key Properties of Ratios

### 3.1 Simplification
Always reduce a ratio to its lowest terms by dividing both by their **HCF**.
- 36 : 48 → HCF = 12 → **3 : 4**

### 3.2 Multiplying / Dividing both terms
A ratio remains the same if both terms are multiplied or divided by the same non-zero number.
- 2 : 3 = 4 : 6 = 6 : 9 = 10 : 15 (all equivalent)

### 3.3 Comparing Ratios
To compare a : b and c : d, cross-multiply:
- If **ad > bc**, then a/b > c/d
- If **ad = bc**, then a/b = c/d
- If **ad < bc**, then a/b < c/d

**Example:** Compare 3:4 and 5:7
- 3×7 = 21, 4×5 = 20 → 21 > 20 → **3:4 > 5:7**

### 3.4 Ratio between 3 or more quantities
If A : B = 2 : 3 and B : C = 4 : 5, find A : B : C

**Method:** Make B's value equal (LCM of 3 and 4 = 12)
- A : B = 8 : 12
- B : C = 12 : 15
- **A : B : C = 8 : 12 : 15**

## 4. What is Proportion?

When two ratios are **equal**, the four quantities are said to be in **proportion**.

**a : b = c : d** is written as **a : b :: c : d**

- a and d are called **extremes**
- b and c are called **means**

### 📌 Fundamental Property (Cross Product Rule):
> **Product of Extremes = Product of Means**
> **a × d = b × c**

**Example:** Are 6, 8, 9, 12 in proportion?
- 6 × 12 = 72 and 8 × 9 = 72 ✅ → **Yes, they are in proportion**

---

## 5. Types of Proportion

### 5.1 Direct Proportion
When one quantity increases, the other also increases **proportionally**.

**Symbol:** x ∝ y → x = ky (k is constant)

**Example:** More workers → More work done in same time.
If 5 workers do work in 8 days, how many days for 10 workers?
- 5 × 8 = 10 × d → d = **4 days**

### 5.2 Inverse Proportion
When one quantity increases, the other **decreases** proportionally.

**Symbol:** x ∝ 1/y → xy = k (constant)

**Example:** More speed → Less time to cover same distance.
If 6 men complete a job in 12 days, 9 men will complete it in?
- 6 × 12 = 9 × d → d = **8 days**

---

## 6. Mean Proportion & Third Proportion

### 6.1 Mean Proportion
The mean proportion between a and b is **√(ab)**

- If x is the mean proportion between a and b → x² = ab → x = **√(ab)**

**Example:** Mean proportion between 4 and 25 = √(4×25) = √100 = **10**

### 6.2 Third Proportion
If a : b = b : c, then c is the **third proportion** to a and b.
- c = **b²/a**

**Example:** Third proportion to 4 and 6:
- c = 6²/4 = 36/4 = **9**

### 6.3 Fourth Proportion
If a : b = c : d, then d is the **fourth proportion**.
- d = **bc/a**

**Example:** Fourth proportion to 5, 6, 15:
- d = (6 × 15)/5 = 90/5 = **18**

---

## 7. Componendo & Dividendo

This is a very powerful rule used to simplify complex proportion problems.

If **a/b = c/d**, then:

| Rule | Formula |
|------|---------|
| **Componendo** | (a+b)/b = (c+d)/d |
| **Dividendo** | (a-b)/b = (c-d)/d |
| **Componendo & Dividendo** | (a+b)/(a-b) = (c+d)/(c-d) |

### 📌 Example:
If (x+y)/(x-y) = 7/3, find x/y using Componendo-Dividendo:

Apply C&D in reverse: if (a+b)/(a-b) = 7/3 then a/b = (7+3)/(7-3) = 10/4 = **5:2**
So x/y = **5:2**

---

## 8. Duplicate, Triplicate & Sub-duplicate Ratios

| Name | Definition | Example (a:b = 2:3) |
|------|------------|---------------------|
| **Duplicate Ratio** | a² : b² | 4 : 9 |
| **Triplicate Ratio** | a³ : b³ | 8 : 27 |
| **Sub-duplicate Ratio** | √a : √b | √2 : √3 |
| **Sub-triplicate Ratio** | ∛a : ∛b | ∛2 : ∛3 |
| **Reciprocal Ratio** | b : a | 3 : 2 |

**Example:** Find the duplicate ratio of 3:4
- 3² : 4² = **9 : 16**

**Example:** Find the sub-duplicate ratio of 16:25
- √16 : √25 = **4 : 5**

---

## 9. Compound Ratio

The compound ratio of a:b, c:d, e:f is obtained by multiplying:
- **(a × c × e) : (b × d × f)**

**Example:** Compound ratio of 2:3, 4:5, 6:7
= (2×4×6) : (3×5×7)
= 48 : 105
= **16 : 35**

---

## 10. Ratio in Mixtures

### Alligation / Mixture Rule:
If two items of cost price CP₁ and CP₂ are mixed to get a mean price M:

```
     CP₁          CP₂
          \       /
           M (mean)
          /       \
     (CP₂-M)   (CP₁-M)

Ratio = (CP₂ - M) : (CP₁ - M)
```

**Example:** In what ratio should tea worth ₹40/kg and ₹60/kg be mixed so the mixture costs ₹54/kg?
- Ratio = (60-54) : (54-40) = 6 : 14 = **3 : 7**

---

## 11. Dividing a Quantity in a Given Ratio

### Two Parts:
Divide N in ratio a : b
- First part = N × a/(a+b)
- Second part = N × b/(a+b)

**Example:** Divide ₹1200 in ratio 3:5
- First = 1200 × 3/8 = **₹450**
- Second = 1200 × 5/8 = **₹750**

### Three Parts:
Divide N in ratio a : b : c
- First = N × a/(a+b+c)
- Second = N × b/(a+b+c)
- Third = N × c/(a+b+c)

**Example:** Divide 270 in ratio 2:3:4
- Sum = 9
- Parts = 60, 90, 120

## 12. Partnership Problems

### Simple Partnership (same time period):
Profit shared in ratio of **capital invested**.

**Example:** A invests ₹3000, B invests ₹5000. Total profit = ₹1600. Find each share.
- Ratio = 3:5
- A's share = 1600 × 3/8 = **₹600**
- B's share = 1600 × 5/8 = **₹1000**

### Compound Partnership (different time periods):
Profit shared in ratio of **(Capital × Time)**.

**Example:** A invests ₹5000 for 12 months, B invests ₹6000 for 10 months. Profit = ₹6100. Find B's share.
- A's equivalent = 5000 × 12 = 60,000
- B's equivalent = 6000 × 10 = 60,000
- Ratio = 1:1
- B's share = **₹3050**

---

## 13. Age-Based Ratio Problems

### Type 1: Present Age Ratio Given
**Example:** Ratio of A's age to B's age is 4:5. After 5 years it will be 5:6. Find present ages.

Let A = 4x, B = 5x
(4x+5)/(5x+5) = 5/6
→ 24x + 30 = 25x + 25
→ x = 5
A = 20 years, B = 25 years

### Type 2: Sum of Ages Given
**Example:** Sum of ages of A and B is 45. Ratio = 7:8. Find ages.
- A = 45 × 7/15 = **21 years**
- B = 45 × 8/15 = **24 years**

---

## 14. Income & Expenditure Ratio Problems

**Standard Template:**
If income ratio is a:b and expenditure ratio is c:d, find savings.

**Example:** Incomes of A and B are in ratio 3:4. Their expenditures are in ratio 5:7. Each saves ₹200. Find their incomes.

Let incomes = 3k, 4k and expenditures = 5m, 7m
- 3k - 5m = 200 ...(i)
- 4k - 7m = 200 ...(ii)
From (i) and (ii): multiply (i) by 7, (ii) by 5:
- 21k - 35m = 1400
- 20k - 35m = 1000
→ k = 400
A's income = 3×400 = **₹1200**
B's income = 4×400 = **₹1600**

## 15. ⚡ Short Tricks & Formula Sheet

### 🔥 TRICK 1: Quick Comparison of Ratios
To compare a/b and c/d → Compare **a×d** vs **b×c** (cross multiply)

---

### 🔥 TRICK 2: Combining Ratios A:B and B:C → A:B:C
1. Find LCM of the two "B" values
2. Scale both ratios to make B equal
3. Write combined ratio

**Shortcut:** A:B:C = (a×c) : (b×c) : (b×d) if A:B = a:b and B:C = c:d

---

### 🔥 TRICK 3: If a:b is given and you need a²:b² or a³:b³
- Simply square or cube both terms directly
- 3:4 → duplicate = 9:16; triplicate = 27:64

---

### 🔥 TRICK 4: Fraction-Based Ratio Problem
"A number is divided into two parts such that one part is p/q of the other"
→ Ratio of parts = p : q (directly)

---

### 🔥 TRICK 5: Change in Ratio After Adding/Subtracting
"If x is added to both terms of ratio a:b, new ratio becomes p:q"
→ **x = (aq - bp)/(p - q)**

Example: x added to 3:5 gives 5:7 → x = (3×7 - 5×5)/(5-7) = (21-25)/(-2) = (-4)/(-2) = **2**

---

### 🔥 TRICK 6: Mean Proportion
Mean proportion of a and b = **√(ab)**
- This is essentially the geometric mean

---

### 🔥 TRICK 7: Partnership Shortcut
If capitals C₁ and C₂ are for times T₁ and T₂:
- Profit ratio = C₁T₁ : C₂T₂
- If profit P is to be split: Partner's share = P × (CᵢTᵢ)/(ΣCᵢTᵢ)

---

### 🔥 TRICK 8: Alligation Cross Rule
```
Higher price (H)    Lower price (L)
         \              /
         Mean Price (M)
         /              \
      H - M           M - L
Ratio of lower : higher = (H-M) : (M-L)
```

---

### 🔥 TRICK 9: Age Problems — Use Variable × Ratio
If present ratio is a:b and k years ago/later ratio changes:
- Let present ages = ak and bk
- Form equation from given condition and solve for k

---

### 🔥 TRICK 10: Componendo-Dividendo — Fast Version
If x/y = p/q, then:
- (x+y)/(x-y) = (p+q)/(p-q) **← Most asked form!**

---

## 📌 EXAM STRATEGY TIPS

1. **Always simplify** ratios to lowest terms first
2. **When combining 3 ratios** (A:B and B:C → A:B:C), use LCM to equalise the common term
3. **For age problems**, set up with variable and form linear equation
4. **For mixture problems**, use Alligation Cross — fastest method
5. **Componendo-Dividendo** is GOLD for fraction-type proportion equations
6. **Partnership = Capital × Time** — never forget the time factor
7. **Option D tip (BPSC TRE):** In "more than one correct" type, verify all individual statements with numbers — don't assume
8. **Time management:** Ratio problems with known tricks should take **30-60 seconds** max
